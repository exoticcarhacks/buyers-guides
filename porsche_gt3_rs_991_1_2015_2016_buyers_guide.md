# Porsche GT3 RS 991.1 2015-2016 Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Porsche 991.1 GT3 RS is a track-focused machine, representing the pinnacle of naturally aspirated performance in its generation. It's a prime hack candidate due to its limited production, strong enthusiast following, and the potential for appreciation if well-maintained. The main risk, however, lies in potential engine issues related to connecting rod bolts, which can lead to catastrophic failure.

## 6. Known Problems and Recalls

Always get a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) from a qualified specialist before buying.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Engine failures | Connecting rod bolts could break, puncturing the cylinder block and leading to oil leaks and potential engine fires. (Cost: Engine replacement, not specified) |
| Finger followers/valvetrain wear | Faulty finger followers in the valvetrain leading to premature wear or failure, especially on the intake side. (Cost: Not specified) |
| Coolant leaks | Cracked coolant lines or faulty connections leading to coolant leaks. (Cost: Not specified) |
| Connecting rod bolt issue | A metallurgical defect in the connecting rod bolts of early 991.1 GT3 models led to engine failures and fires. This resulted in a recall and engine replacements. |
