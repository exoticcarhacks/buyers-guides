# BMW M3 M4 F8X Generation 2015-2020 (3.0L twin-turbo I6, includes CS) Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The BMW F8X generation M3 and M4 represent a significant departure from their naturally aspirated predecessors, embracing a potent 3.0L twin-turbocharged inline-six engine. This era of M cars offers a compelling blend of high performance, daily usability, and modern technology, making them prime candidates for the Exotic Car Hacks philosophy. The primary risk associated with these vehicles lies in the potential for significant repair costs if common issues are not addressed proactively or if the car has been poorly maintained.

## Known Problems and Recalls

Perform a thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a qualified BMW specialist.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| AC System Issues | Especially when set on "auto" or not blowing cold. |
| Crank Hub Failure | Happens in a very low 0.5% of cars but is a catastrophic failure. Consider preventative measures if tracking the car. |
| Oil Cooler Location | Exposed to large road debris; inspect for damage. |
| Rocks between Dust Plate and Brake Rotor | Can occasionally get stuck, causing grinding noises. |
| DCT Issues | Slow going from 1 to R, sluggish downshifting from 2 into 1st. Test thoroughly. |
| Minor Cosmetic Issues | Peeling or cracking of interior trim. |
| Carbon Buildup | Requires frequent cleaning, especially for direct-injection engines. |
| Burning Leg Problem | Hot air blown from vent; check for proper HVAC function. |
| Wheel Balance Issues | Not balanced at high speeds, causing vibration. |
| BMW M4/M2 Fuel Injector Defect Class Action Lawsuit | 2020 BMW M4 Coupe and 2020-2021 BMW M2 Coupe recalled for fire risk. Verify if fixed. |
| TSB SI B34 06 15: Grinding Noise from Rear Brakes | Affects early F80/F82/F83 models with standard compound brakes. Caused by foreign materials; check for updated protection plates. |
| FUEL/PROPULSION SYSTEM | Fuel tank leaks reported; inspect for any signs of fuel leakage or odors. |
| SERVICE BRAKES | Turbine-like noise from rear axle during deceleration in first 300 miles of operation. |
| ELECTRICAL SYSTEM | Discharged battery complaints; check battery health and charging system. |
| SUSPENSION | Cracking/clicking noise from front struts when turning steering wheel, especially when extended. |
| SERVICE BRAKES, HYDRAULIC | Functional noises (squealing/honking) when braking shortly before coming to a stop. |
| SUSPENSION, ENGINE | Wheelbase failure and front end raising off the ground while turning, leading to engine replacement. Thoroughly inspect suspension and engine mounts. |
| STRUCTURE | "Cracking" sound from rear wheel arch area on smooth/uneven roads. |

The BMW F8X M3 and M4 are **strong hack candidates** for those seeking a modern, high-performance German sports car with significant aftermarket potential.
