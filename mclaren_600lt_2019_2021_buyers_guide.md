# McLaren 600LT 2019-2021 Buyer Guide

The McLaren 600LT, a track-focused derivative of the already potent 570S, represents a compelling "Longtail" variant designed for enthusiasts seeking raw performance and exclusivity. Its limited production and strong performance credentials may suggest potential for value retention, but its highly specialized nature and the inherent complexities of McLaren ownership introduce significant considerations, particularly concerning maintenance costs and the potential for depreciation if not carefully managed.

## Known Issues and Recalls

A comprehensive Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a McLaren specialist is non-negotiable.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Intermittent electrical issues: Owners have reported sporadic malfunctions including the electronic parking brake engaging unexpectedly, windows lowering autonomously, and key fob recognition failures preventing vehicle start. These issues are often difficult to diagnose and may stem from complex wiring harnesses or sensor failures. Inspect for diagnostic trouble codes (DTCs) and ensure all electrical components function consistently during a PPI. | cost varies |
| Front-end vibrations: Some owners of 600LTs equipped with Pirelli P Zero Trofeo R tires have reported front-end vibrations. This is often attributed to the aggressive, track-focused nature of these tires and their sensitivity to road conditions and wear patterns, rather than a manufacturing defect of the car itself. Inspect tire condition, balance, and alignment, especially if the car is frequently driven on the street. | cost varies |
| Transmission mount failure: While not a widespread issue, some McLaren models, including the 600LT, can experience transmission mount degradation or failure over time, particularly with aggressive driving or track use. Symptoms may include excessive engine movement, clunking noises during gear changes or acceleration, and vibrations. During a PPI, inspect the transmission mounts for cracks, tears, or excessive play. | cost varies (repair/replacement) |
| Potential coolant leak in radiator hoses (recall related) | cost covered by recall. |

### NHTSA Recalls

The following NHTSA recalls are applicable to the McLaren 600LT:
*   **23V-484 (Coolant Hoses):** Affects 2019-2020 600LT Coupe and Spider models. Addresses potential coolant leaks in radiator hoses.
*   **25V-216 (Brake Lines):** Affects 2019-2021 600LT models. Addresses potential issues with brake lines.
