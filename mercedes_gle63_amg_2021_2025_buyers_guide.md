# Mercedes-AMG GLE 63 S 2021-2025 Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction
The Mercedes-AMG GLE 63 S (2021-2025) is a high-performance luxury SUV that blends AMG's potent engineering with Mercedes-Benz's opulent comfort. It features an AMG SPEEDSHIFT TCT 9-speed automatic transmission and AMG Performance 4MATIC+ all-wheel drive. It can accelerate from 0-60 mph in approximately 3.4 seconds and has an electronically limited top speed of 174 mph with the AMG Driver's Package [11, 14]. It stands as a compelling hack candidate due to its significant depreciation from new, offering a gateway to a premium performance SUV experience at a fraction of its original cost. However, potential buyers must be wary of the complex and costly maintenance associated with its advanced powertrain and sophisticated electronics, which represent the primary ownership risk.

## 6. Known Problems and NHTSA Recalls

**Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) is highly recommended.**

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Engine Issues | While general engine complaints exist for the broader GLE-Class, specific widespread issues for the M177 4.0L V8 biturbo engine in the GLE 63 S (2021-2025) are not widely reported. Buyers should focus on regular maintenance records and listen for unusual noises during a PPI. |
| Electrical System Issues | General electrical issues are common across many modern luxury vehicles. For the GLE 63 S, buyers should specifically check for proper operation of the MBUX infotainment system, active safety features, and all interior electronics during a PPI. Look for error messages or intermittent functionality. |
| Brakes Issues | Given the high performance of the GLE 63 S, brake wear is expected. Buyers should inspect brake pad and rotor condition, especially if the vehicle is equipped with the optional AMG High-Performance Ceramic Composite Braking System. Listen for squealing or grinding during test drives. |
| Safety Systems Issues | Modern safety systems are complex. During a PPI, ensure all driver assistance features (e.g., adaptive cruise control, lane-keeping assist) are functional and free of error codes. Check for proper calibration of sensors and cameras. |
| Wheels & Tires Issues | The 22-inch wheels are prone to curb rash and potential bending from potholes. Inspect all four wheels for damage and ensure tires have adequate tread depth and even wear. Uneven wear can indicate alignment issues. |
| Transmission Issues | The AMG SPEEDSHIFT TCT 9-speed automatic transmission is generally robust. During a test drive, pay attention to smooth gear changes, especially under hard acceleration and deceleration. Any hesitation, slipping, or harsh shifts warrant further investigation. |
| Fuel System Issues | No specific widespread fuel system issues are reported for the GLE 63 S. However, buyers should check for any fuel odors, leaks, or rough idling that could indicate a problem. |
| Oil vapor separator failure leading to failed rear main seal (M177 4.0L V8 biturbo engine) | This is a known issue on some M177 engines. Inspect the rear of the engine for oil leaks, particularly around the bell housing. Repair can be costly due to engine removal [20]. |
| Cooled seats shut off after 3 seconds | This is a reported issue where the cooled seat function prematurely deactivates. Verify proper operation during a PPI; repair often involves replacing the seat ventilation fan or control module [21]. |
| Critical battery warning | This can indicate a failing 12V auxiliary battery or issues with the 48V EQ Boost system. Check for persistent warning messages and ensure all electrical components function correctly. Diagnosis may require specialized tools [22]. |
| Valve body issue (transmission) | A faulty transmission valve body can cause shifting problems. During a test drive, pay close attention to transmission behavior. If detected, repair can be extensive and parts may have lead times [23]. |
