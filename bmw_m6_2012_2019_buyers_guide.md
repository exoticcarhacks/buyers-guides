# BMW M6 2012-2019 Buyer Guide

The BMW M6, spanning the F06 Gran Coupé, F12 Convertible, and F13 Coupé generations from 2012 to 2019, represents a potent blend of luxury and high-performance grand touring. Powered by the formidable 4.4L twin-turbo S63 V8, it offers exhilarating performance and sophisticated styling, making it an intriguing candidate for the discerning Exotic Car Hacks enthusiast. However, prospective owners must be acutely aware of the significant mechanical risks, particularly the well-documented rod bearing wear, which can lead to catastrophic engine failure and substantial repair costs.

## Known Problems and NHTSA Recalls

**Perform a thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a BMW specialist before any purchase.** This should include a comprehensive diagnostic scan, a leak down test, and a close inspection of all critical mechanical and electronic components.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| **Rod Bearing Wear (S63 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8)** | The S63 engine is prone to premature rod bearing wear, which can lead to catastrophic engine failure if not addressed. Symptoms may include a faint knocking sound from the engine, especially at idle, or metallic particles in the oil during an oil change. A thorough PPI should include an oil analysis and potentially a rod bearing inspection. This issue is most prevalent in early F-chassis models (2012-2014) but can affect all years. Repair costs are substantial, often requiring a full engine rebuild or replacement. |
| **Turbocharger Failure (S63 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8)** | Common issues include wastegate rattle, oil starvation, and seal failure. Symptoms include reduced power, increased turbo lag, a noticeable whining or whistling sound from the turbos, and excessive oil consumption. Inspect for oil leaks around the turbochargers and listen for abnormal noises during acceleration and deceleration. This can be a costly repair, often requiring turbocharger replacement. |
| **High-Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) & Fuel Injector Issues (S63 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8)** | Malfunctions can lead to misfires, rough idling, poor fuel economy, and difficulty starting the engine. Symptoms often trigger a "Drivetrain Malfunction" warning on the iDrive screen. A diagnostic scan will typically reveal specific fault codes related to fuel pressure or injector performance. Inspect service records for previous replacements. |
| **Valve Stem Seal Failure (S63 4.4L Twin-Turbo V8)** | This issue manifests as excessive oil consumption and visible blue smoke from the exhaust, particularly on cold starts or during heavy acceleration after idling. It can also lead to reduced engine performance and spark plug fouling. Inspect the exhaust for smoke and monitor oil levels closely. Repair involves significant labor to replace the seals. |
| **Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT) Issues (Getrag 7-speed)** | While generally robust, the 7-speed M-DCT can experience rough shifting, overheating warnings during aggressive driving, or clutch slippage, especially in higher mileage examples or those subjected to frequent track use. A PPI should include a test drive to assess shift quality and responsiveness. Inspect for any fluid leaks around the transmission. |
| **Coolant Leaks & Water Pump Failure** | The cooling system, particularly plastic components and the electric water pump, can be a weak point. Common failures include leaks from hoses, radiator end tanks, and the water pump itself. Symptoms include a low coolant warning, visible coolant puddles under the car, or engine overheating. Inspect all coolant hoses, the radiator, and the water pump for signs of leakage or corrosion. |
| **Oil Cooler Leaks** | Leaks from the engine oil cooler or its lines can lead to noticeable oil drips under the vehicle and potentially low engine oil pressure if severe. Inspect the oil cooler and surrounding lines for any signs of oil residue or active leaks. |
| **iDrive System Malfunctions** | The iDrive infotainment system can experience freezing, lagging, or complete failure. This often requires software updates, but in some cases, the head unit or associated control modules may need replacement. Test all iDrive functions thoroughly during a PPI. |
| **Battery Drain Issues** | Due to the M6's complex electrical system, rapid battery drain can occur from faulty Intelligent Battery Sensors (IBS), alternator issues, or excessive parasitic draw from malfunctioning electronic modules (e.g., Comfort Access, infotainment). Symptoms include a dead battery after short periods of inactivity. A diagnostic scan can help identify parasitic draws. |
| **Comfort Access & Keyless Entry Failure** | Issues typically stem from faulty door handle sensors or wiring, preventing keyless entry or locking. Symptoms include intermittent or complete failure of the Comfort Access function for specific doors. Test all door handles for proper operation. |
| **Premature Brake Wear (M Carbon Ceramic Brakes)** | While offering superior performance, the optional M Carbon Ceramic Brakes have very high replacement costs for rotors and pads. Owners may also experience functional noises (squealing or honking) when braking shortly before the vehicle comes to a complete stop, which is considered normal operation (BMW Technical Service Bulletin SI B34 07 14, January 2015). Inspect rotor and pad wear carefully during a PPI. |
| **Suspension Component Wear** | Components such as control arms, bushings, and adaptive dampers can wear prematurely, especially on vehicles driven aggressively or on poor roads. Symptoms include clunking noises over bumps, uneven tire wear, and a degraded ride quality. Inspect all suspension components for play, cracks in bushings, or leaks from adaptive dampers. |
| **Exhaust Flap Failure** | The exhaust flap mechanism, which controls exhaust sound, can fail, leading to rattling noises or a constant loud exhaust note. Inspect the flap mechanism for proper operation and listen for rattles. |
| **Soft-Top Convertible Issues (F12 Models)** | The convertible soft-top mechanism can experience slow operation, hydraulic leaks, or failure to latch properly. Inspect the operation of the soft top thoroughly, checking for smooth movement, proper sealing, and any signs of hydraulic fluid leaks. |

### NHTSA Recalls

| NHTSA Recall Campaign Number | Date Issued | Affected Model Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| **12V475000** | September 28, 2012 | 2013 M6 Coupé, 2013 M6 Convertible | **Engine Oil Pump Driveshaft Issue:** Due to a manufacturing process error, the tolerance between the engine oil pump's drive shaft and the pump's rotor was not within specification. This could cause the pump's driveshaft to separate from the rotor, leading to a loss of engine lubrication and potential engine damage. |
| **16V540000** | July 18, 2016 | 2015 M6 Coupé, 2015 M6 Convertible, 2015 M6 Gran Coupé | **Inadequately Welded Driveshaft:** The driveshaft may have been inadequately welded during manufacturing. An improperly welded driveshaft may fracture and fail, causing a loss of drive to the rear wheels and a loss of propulsion. If the car is turned off and exited without the parking brake applied, the car may roll. |
| **17V495000** | August 8, 2017 | 2013 M6 Coupé, 2012-2013 M6 Convertible | **Driver's Frontal Air Bag Inflator:** The affected vehicles have a driver's frontal air bag inflator that may have been improperly welded, potentially causing the inflator to rupture during deployment and spray metal fragments. |
| **20V094000** | February 18, 2020 | 2014-2019 M6 Gran Coupé | **Third Brake Light May Become Loose:** The third brake light may become loose, possibly allowing the light to detach from the vehicle, increasing the risk of a crash. |

## Conclusion

The BMW M6 is a **medium hack candidate**. Its potent performance and luxurious appointments are highly appealing, but the significant risk of S63 V8 engine issues, particularly rod bearing wear, demands careful consideration and a robust maintenance budget. The best ownership logic involves acquiring a well-documented example with a strong service history, ideally one that has already addressed common failure points, and budgeting for potential major repairs. Prioritize Competition Package models, especially the 600 hp variants, for maximum enjoyment and long-term value retention.
