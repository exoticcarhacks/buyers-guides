# BMW M6 2012-2019 Buyer Guide

The BMW M6, spanning the F06, F12, and F13 generations from 2012 to 2019, represents a potent blend of luxury and high-performance grand touring. Powered by the formidable 4.4L twin-turbo S63 V8, it offers exhilarating performance and sophisticated styling, making it an intriguing candidate for the discerning Exotic Car Hacks enthusiast. However, prospective owners must be acutely aware of the significant mechanical risks, particularly the well-documented rod bearing wear, which can lead to catastrophic engine failure and substantial repair costs.

## Known Problems and NHTSA Recalls

**Perform a thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a BMW specialist before any purchase.**

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Rod Bearing Wear | premature rod bearing wear in the S63 4.4L twin-turbo V8, leading to catastrophic engine failure — costly repairs |
| Turbocharger Failure | wastegate rattle and oil starvation, causing power loss, increased turbo lag, and whining noises — costly repairs |
| Fuel Injector & High-Pressure Fuel Pump (HPFP) Issues | misfires, rough idling, poor fuel economy, and difficulty starting the engine — costly repairs |
| Valve Stem Seal Failure | excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust, resulting in poor engine performance and rough cold starts — costly repairs |
| Dual-Clutch Transmission (DCT) Issues | rough shifting, overheating, and clutch slippage — costly repairs |
| Drivetrain Malfunction Warning | fuel system issues, ignition coil failures, or turbo problems, causing the car to enter limp mode with reduced power — costly repairs |
| Coolant Leaks & Radiator Failures | coolant leaks, failing water pumps, and cracked radiators, leading to engine overheating — costly repairs |
| Oil Cooler Leaks | low oil pressure and increased engine wear — costly repairs |
| iDrive System Malfunctions | freezing, lagging, or complete failure, often requiring software updates or a head unit replacement — costly repairs |
| Battery Drain Issues | quick battery drain due to high electrical demand, faulty IBS (Intelligent Battery Sensor), alternator issues, or excessive parasitic draw — costly repairs |
| Comfort Access & Keyless Entry Failure | faulty door handle sensors or a weak key fob battery — costly repairs |
| Premature Brake Wear (Carbon Ceramic Brakes) | high replacement costs and occasional squeaking or uneven wear — high replacement costs |
| Suspension Component Wear | control arms, bushings, and adaptive dampers wear prematurely, leading to clunking noises, uneven tire wear, and a rougher ride — costly repairs |
| Exhaust Flap Failure | rattling noises or reduced exhaust sound — costly repairs |
| Catalytic Converter Clogging | power loss and increased emissions errors — costly repairs |
| Soft-Top Convertible Issues (F12 Models) | slow operation, hydraulic leaks, or failure to latch properly with the soft-top mechanism — costly repairs |
| Door Handle & Mirror Motor Failures | automatic folding mirrors and Comfort Access door handles fail — costly repairs |
| Carbon Ceramic Brake Noise | Functional noises (squealing/honking) when braking shortly before the vehicle comes to a stop (TSB SI B34 07 14, January 2015) |
| Transfer Case Defect | Jerking or shuddering when driving, especially when shifting gears, making turns or driving at low speeds (Class Action Lawsuit, affects 2019-2025 models including X-series, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8 Series, but not specifically M6) - Note: M6 is not explicitly mentioned in the class action, but the platform is related to other BMW models with the issue. |
| Faulty Oil Filter Housings | Class action lawsuit against BMW for faulty oil filter housings (not specific to M6, but related to BMW engines). |

## Conclusion

The BMW M6 is a **medium hack candidate**. Its potent performance and luxurious appointments are highly appealing, but the significant risk of S63 V8 engine issues, particularly rod bearing wear, demands careful consideration and a robust maintenance budget. The best ownership logic involves acquiring a well-documented example with a strong service history, ideally one that has already addressed common failure points, and budgeting for potential major repairs. Prioritize Competition Package models for maximum enjoyment and resale value.
