# Acura NSX (2017–2022) Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The second-generation Acura NSX (NC1) is a highly complex, 573-horsepower hybrid supercar that combines a twin-turbo V6 with three electric motors. As an Exotic Car Hack, it offers tremendous value because it suffered from massive initial depreciation and slow sales, meaning secondary buyers can pick up a $160,000+ technological marvel for significantly less. The best buys are 2019+ models which received suspension and software updates, or heavily depreciated 2017s with extended warranties. The biggest risks are the immense complexity of the powertrain: out-of-warranty hybrid battery replacements and the notorious $20,000 engine-out 75,000-mile valve adjustment service make an extended warranty or strict PPI essential.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

PPI reminder: Use an Acura NSX certified technician. The hybrid system requires specialized diagnostic tools.

### Known Problems / Inspection Issues

| Issue | What To Know / Check |
|---|---|
| 75,000-Mile Valve Adjustment Service | The most critical maintenance cost to be aware of. Acura requires an engine-out valve adjustment at 75,000 miles. Quotes for this service range from $15,000 to $20,000+. If buying a high-mileage NSX, this must be factored into the purchase price. |
| Hybrid Battery Failure / Drain | Early models (2017) had reports of premature 12V and hybrid battery drain. Out-of-warranty hybrid battery replacement is extremely expensive (estimated $5,000+). Verify the hybrid system warranty status (typically 8 years/100k miles, or 10 years/150k miles in CARB states). |
| Carbon-Ceramic Brake Rotor Damage | Inspect CCB rotors carefully during PPI. Cracks or chips in the carbon-ceramic rotors require full replacement, which costs thousands of dollars per corner. |
| Interior Rattles and Panel Gaps | Owners frequently report door rattles at highway speeds and inconsistent exterior panel gaps. While not mechanically fatal, they detract from the supercar experience. |

### Known NHTSA Recalls To Verify By VIN

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years / Models | Issue | What To Know |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 20V-314 (expanded by 23V-858) | Fuel Pump Failure | 2017–2020 NSX | The low-pressure fuel pump inside the fuel tank may fail, causing the engine to stall while driving. | A critical safety recall. Dealers will replace the fuel pump assembly. Note: 23V-858 is an expansion of 20V-314 affecting a broader range of Honda/Acura vehicles; for the NSX, 20V-314 is the primary relevant recall. |
| 18V-600 | Fuel Tank Crossover Pipe | 2017–2018 NSX | The crossover pipe couplers between the left and right fuel tanks may leak, increasing fire risk. | Dealers replace the crossover pipe. |
| 18V-599 | High Mount Brake Light | 2017 NSX | Road vibrations may loosen screws on the center brake light circuit board, blowing the fuse and disabling brake lights. | Dealers replace the center high-mounted stop light. |


## 8. Conclusion

The 2017–2021 Acura NSX is a brilliant, usable supercar that suffered from poor initial marketing and high MSRPs, making it a fantastic used buy. The 2019 model year is the sweet spot due to significant suspension and software improvements. However, the extreme complexity of the hybrid powertrain means buyers must be hyper-vigilant about warranties and maintenance history. The looming $20,000 engine-out service at 75,000 miles and potential hybrid battery replacement costs mean this is not a car to buy cheap and hope for the best. A comprehensive PPI by a certified NSX tech is mandatory.

## 9. Car Hacking Shortcuts

* Years to buy: 2019+ (best driving dynamics) or 2017 (lowest entry price)
* Options to look for: Carbon Fiber Exterior Package, Carbon-Ceramic Brakes, ELS Audio
* Preferred colors: Thermal Orange Pearl, Nouvelle Blue Pearl, Valencia Red Pearl
* PPI focus: Hybrid battery health/warranty status, carbon-ceramic rotor condition, 75k-mile service timeline
* Known recalls: 23V-858/20V-314 (Fuel Pump), 18V-600 (Fuel Pipe Leak)
