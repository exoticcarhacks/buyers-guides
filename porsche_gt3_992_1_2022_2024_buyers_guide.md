## Known Problems and NHTSA Recalls

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Oil Consumption | While some oil consumption is normal for high-performance engines, excessive consumption (e.g., more than 1 quart per 500 miles) in the 992.1 GT3 could indicate issues. Monitor oil levels regularly and check service records for consistent top-ups. Consult a Porsche specialist for diagnosis if consumption is unusually high. |
| Windshield/Rear Window Seal Issues | Some 992 models have reported issues with seals melting/deteriorating or not being properly secured, potentially leading to detachment. Inspect seals thoroughly for any signs of degradation or improper fitment. |
| Center Lock Wheel Fractures | Instances of center lock wheel fractures have been reported. Verify proper wheel mounting and inspect wheels for any signs of stress or damage, especially after track use or tire changes. |

### NHTSA Recalls

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 22V-803 | 2022 | 2022 | The screw that attaches the lower seat belt anchor to the seat frame may not have been tightened properly. |
| 23V-446 | 2022-2024 | 2022-2024 | A software error in the rear spoiler control unit may allow both the vehicle and spoiler to exceed certain speed limits (Incorrect activation of brake lights) [4, 9, 10]. |
| 24V-155 | 2020-2024 | 2020-2024 (all 911 models, including GT3) | The front windshield and rear window may not be properly secured and can detach [5, 11]. |
