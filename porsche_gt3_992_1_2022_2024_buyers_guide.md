## Known Problems and NHTSA Recalls

Always perform a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a qualified specialist.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Oil Consumption | Some owners report significant oil consumption (1 quart every 400-800 miles). While some consider it normal, excessive consumption might indicate issues like cylinder wall pitting. Check service records for oil top-ups and monitor levels. |
| Interior Squeaks and Rattles | Minor interior rattles, especially from door cards, are reported. Listen carefully during a test drive. |
| Battery Drain | Batteries going flat is a consistent issue. Ensure the car comes with a battery tender or plan to acquire one. |
| Windshield/Rear Window Seal Issues | Some 992 models have reported issues with seals melting/deteriorating or not being properly secured, potentially leading to detachment. Inspect seals thoroughly. |
| Center Lock Wheel Fractures | Instances of center lock wheel fractures have been reported. Verify proper wheel mounting and inspect wheels for any signs of stress or damage. |

### NHTSA Recalls

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |

| 24V-155 | 2020-2024 | 2022-2024 | The front windshield and rear window may not be properly secured and can detach. |
