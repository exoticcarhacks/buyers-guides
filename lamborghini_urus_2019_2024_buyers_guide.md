# Lamborghini Urus Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The Lamborghini Urus redefined the performance SUV segment, blending supercar DNA with everyday usability. As a high-value, high-performance vehicle, it presents a compelling hack candidate due to its significant initial depreciation, offering an entry point into exotic ownership at a reduced cost. ## Known Problems and Recalls

Always obtain a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) from a qualified, independent specialist before purchase.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Transmission Problems | Look for slipping or jerking movements during shifts, check for low transmission fluid levels, and listen for unusual noises. Faulty torque converters or worn-out clutch plates can lead to expensive repairs. |
| Electrical System Glitches | Test the infotainment system thoroughly for freezing or unresponsiveness. Check all audio components for cutting out or distortion. |
| Suspension Concerns | Pay attention to ride quality; an overly bumpy ride or persistent suspension warning lights indicate potential issues with the adaptive suspension system. |
| Engine Related Issues | Inspect for oil leaks, listen for misfires, and check for signs of overheating. Carbon build-up and camshaft wear are known issues that can lead to significant engine work. |
| Fuel Leak Recall | Specifically for 2019-2020 models, ensure the fuel line quick connector has been inspected and replaced if necessary, due to risk of damage from high engine compartment temperatures. |
| Production Defects in Engine Block Casting | Be aware of potential engine block casting issues that could lead to complete engine replacements. A thorough PPI should include a detailed engine inspection. |
