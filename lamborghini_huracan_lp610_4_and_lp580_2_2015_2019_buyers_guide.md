# Lamborghini Huracan LP610-4 and LP580-2 2015-2019 Buyer Guide

The Lamborghini Huracan (2015-2019) represents a massive leap forward from the Gallardo, introducing a highly reliable 5.2L V10 paired with a 7-speed dual-clutch transmission that makes it one of the most usable and bulletproof modern exotics. As a prime hack candidate, its striking design, visceral naturally aspirated V10 sound, and robust Audi-derived electronics ensure strong demand and predictable depreciation curves, especially for well-optioned LP610-4 and LP580-2 models. The main risk lies in acquiring a poorly maintained ex-rental unit with hidden gearbox abuse or missing crucial factory options like the front axle lift, which severely limits its resale liquidity.

## Known Problems & Recalls

Always secure a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) from a qualified independent specialist or Lamborghini dealer before purchase.

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
| :--- | :--- |
| Gearbox Abuse | Ensure smooth changes from 1st to 2nd gear. While generally bulletproof, ex-rentals can suffer abuse. |
| Engine Stuttering/Slipping | RPM stuttering at startup or slipping between 3rd and 4th gear can indicate a need for a new clutch, E-gear Actuator, or compression issues. |
| Catastrophic Engine Failure | Rare but reported engine blow at low miles (e.g., 14k miles) costing $55k+ for rebuild or $100k for new engine. Verify maintenance history and avoid heavily tracked/rented cars. |
| Brake Fluid Boiling | Complaints regarding brake fluid performance when driving hard; ensure fluid is fresh and upgraded if tracking. |
| Check Engine Lights | Various reports of CELs; ensure all codes are scanned and addressed during PPI. |
| Mirror Vibration | Excessive vibration of passenger or driver side door mirror (Bulletin TPI-LB62X-04-19). Requires thread lock on fixing screws. |
| Air Deflector Installation | Possible error in the installation of the two rear air deflectors (Bulletin L62X-A.03.18). |

## Conclusion

The Lamborghini Huracan LP610-4 and LP580-2 are **strong** hack candidates. They offer a reliable, modern exotic experience with a relatively low cost of ownership compared to older Italian exotics. The main risk is buying a car with a hidden history of rental abuse or lacking essential options like the front lift and transparent engine bonnet. The best ownership logic is to find a well-optioned, brightly colored example with a clean history, enjoy it for 6-12 months, and exit with minimal depreciation thanks to the strong, consistent demand for naturally aspirated V10 Lamborghinis.
