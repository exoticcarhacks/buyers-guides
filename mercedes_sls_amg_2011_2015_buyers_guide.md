## Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) & Known Issues

A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection by a qualified Mercedes-Benz specialist is non-negotiable for any SLS AMG.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Transmission Lag | Many 2011-2012 SLS AMG owners reported slow shifts with the dual-clutch transmission. Mercedes-AMG later offered a free software upgrade to address this issue. |
| Head Bolt Failure (M159 Engine) | Potential for head bolt failure in the M159 6.2L V8 engine, especially when exposed to coolant passages. Symptoms include unexplained coolant loss, engine misfires, and coolant entering the combustion chamber. |
| Camshaft and Lifter Wear | Premature camshaft and lifter wear reported on the M159 engine, manifesting as ticking noise on cold startup and persistent valvetrain noise. Root cause is accelerated wear between cam lobes and lifters. |
| Fuel Injector Failure | Reported fuel injector failures, particularly on the M159 engine, leading to rough idle, misfires, poor fuel economy, and engine knocking or failure to start. Typically an injector sticking open. |
| Radiator Failure | Rare reports of radiator leaks or failures, typically manifesting as coolant loss, overheating, or visible coolant under the vehicle. Root cause is usually internal corrosion or physical damage to the radiator core. |
| Harsh Suspension/Jarring Ride | Multiple reviews and owner reports note that the SLS AMG Roadster's suspension can be harsh and unforgiving, especially on poor road surfaces. This is a design characteristic rather than a defect. |
| Seat Occupancy Detection Issues | System could fail to recognize passengers and compromise airbag deployment. |
| Brake Pad and Rotor Wear | Standard steel brakes require replacement (pads around $400, rotors up to $2,500 for a full set). |
| Electrical Gremlins | Intermittent issues with infotainment system and minor electronic components, often fixed with software updates or module replacements. |
