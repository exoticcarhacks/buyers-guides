## Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) and Known Issues

A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection by a Viper specialist is non-negotiable.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Piston Ring Issues | Some early 2013-2014 models experienced piston ring land failures, leading to high oil consumption and potential engine damage. Solutions include replacing piston rings or full engine rebuild, costing $2,500 to $7,000. Inspect for excessive oil consumption and blue smoke from the exhaust. |
| Sagging Doors | The lightweight aluminum doors on all Gen V models can sag over time due to the weight of the power window motors and general use. This can lead to misalignment and wind noise. Solutions include adjusting door hinges or replacement, costs vary. Inspect for uneven door gaps and difficulty closing. |
| Wind Noise and Water Leaks | Inspect weather stripping around windows and doors for wear or damage. Solutions for general wind noise/water leaks include replacing weather stripping or adjusting window alignment, costs around $250 for minor fixes. |
| A/C Airflow Issues | A common issue in 2013-2014 models where the A/C system switches to defrost mode during acceleration due to a vacuum leak in the HVAC system. Solutions include inspecting the vacuum lines and actuators for leaks and repairing them. Costs vary depending on the source of the leak. |
| Oil Forced into Make Up Air Hose (MUA) | In some 2013-2015 models, oil can be forced into the Make Up Air (MUA) hose at higher speeds and RPMs, leading to oil consumption and potential engine issues. This was addressed by NHTSA Recall 15V-461 (Campaign R40) in 2015. Solutions include installing an updated oil management system or rerouting the MUA hose. Costs around $2,400 if not covered by recall. |
| Water Pump Issues | Premature water pump failure can occur, leading to coolant leaks and potential engine overheating. This is more common in higher mileage examples. Solutions include replacing the water pump, costs between $380 and $750. Inspect for coolant leaks around the water pump housing and unusual noises. |

|---|---|---|---|

| 16V736000 | S75 | 2016 | Improper Driver Air Bag Wire Routing: If the driver's air bag wires are pinched, the air bag may not deploy properly in the event of a crash necessitating air bag deployment, increasing the risk of injury. |
