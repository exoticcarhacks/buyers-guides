# Mercedes E63 AMG 2018-2021, 2023 Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The Mercedes-AMG E63, particularly the W213 generation from 2018-2021 and 2023, represents a compelling blend of executive luxury and supercar-baiting performance, making it an intriguing candidate for the discerning enthusiast seeking a high-performance sedan or wagon. Note that the E63 S was not available in the U.S. market for the 2022 model year due to a V8 production suspension [1]. Its potent 4.0L twin-turbo V8 engine and sophisticated all-wheel-drive system offer exhilarating dynamics, while its depreciation curve can make it an accessible entry into elite performance motoring. The primary risk, however, lies in the potential for high maintenance and repair costs associated with its complex engineering and high-performance components.

## Pre-Purchase Inspection & Known Problems
A thorough pre-purchase inspection (PPI) by a qualified Mercedes-Benz or AMG specialist is highly recommended before any purchase.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Transmission Issues | Look for shifting delays, harsh gear changes, hesitation, or abrupt shifts. |
| Electrical Problems | Check for unresponsive displays, malfunctioning driver aids, or flickering cabin lights. |
| Suspension Concerns | Inspect for premature wear or leaks in air suspension components, uncomfortable ride, or inability to maintain proper ride height. |
| Engine-related Problems | Look for oil leaks (oil spots under the car), burning smells, reduced engine performance, or odd noises from the turbochargers. |
| Brake Noise | Listen for squeaking or squealing sounds under certain braking conditions. |
| Paint Quality Issues | Examine paint for premature chipping or scratching. |
| Windshield Wipers | Verify proper operation; check for mechanical or electrical issues causing them to stop working. |

## Conclusion
The Mercedes-AMG E63 (W213) is a **strong hack candidate** for those seeking a high-performance luxury vehicle with significant depreciation.
