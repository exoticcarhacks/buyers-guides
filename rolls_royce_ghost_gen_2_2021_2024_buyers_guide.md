## Known Problems and Recalls

Always perform a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a qualified Rolls-Royce specialist.

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| **Electrical System Complexity** | The Ghost Gen 2 features highly integrated and sophisticated electrical systems. While no widespread, specific component failures are commonly reported, diagnosing and repairing any electrical issue (e.g., infotainment glitches, sensor malfunctions) requires specialized Rolls-Royce diagnostic tools and expertise. Generic advice to "find a specialist" is particularly relevant here due to the bespoke nature of the vehicle's electronics. |
| **Air Suspension System Wear** | The advanced air suspension system provides the Ghost's signature "magic carpet ride." Components such as air springs, air compressor, and height sensors can wear over time. Listen for unusual noises from the suspension, check for uneven ride height when parked, or a noticeably harsher ride. Replacements are costly. |
| **V12 Engine Oil Leaks** | While the N74B68 6.75L twin-turbo V12 engine is generally robust, some owners have reported oil leaks, particularly from valve cover gaskets or oil filter housing gaskets, especially on higher mileage examples. During a PPI, inspect the engine bay thoroughly for any signs of oil seepage or drips. |
| **ZF 8-Speed Transmission Reliability** | The ZF 8HP automatic transmission is widely used across luxury brands and is generally considered reliable. There are no widespread, documented issues of shift delays, jerking motions, or slipping gears specific to the Ghost Gen 2. However, ensure smooth shifts during a test drive and check for proper fluid maintenance records. |
| **Turbocharger Longevity** | The twin-turbochargers on the V12 engine are designed for durability. Specific widespread issues like wastegate failures or oil line blockages are not commonly reported for the Ghost Gen 2 within this model range. A PPI should include checking for excessive turbo whine, smoke from the exhaust, or reduced power output, which could indicate turbocharger wear. |
| **Cooling System Components** | The cooling system for the powerful V12 engine is complex. While not a common failure point for this generation, components like the water pump, thermostat housing, and various coolant lines can eventually degrade. Check for coolant leaks, proper operating temperature, and ensure the system has been regularly serviced with the correct coolant. |
| **Brake Disc & Pad Wear** | Given the Ghost's weight and performance, brake components experience significant wear. This is normal, but replacement costs are high due to specialized parts. Check for brake pad thickness, rotor condition (grooves, warping), and ensure smooth, confident braking without pulsation. |
