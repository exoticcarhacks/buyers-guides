# Ferrari California T (2015-2017) Buyer's Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Ferrari California T, produced from 2015 to 2017, represents a significant evolution from its predecessor, the original Ferrari California. This model marked a pivotal shift with the introduction of a new 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine in 2014, enhancing both performance and efficiency. Available in the U.S. market for the 2015, 2016, and 2017 model years, its production concluded with the introduction of the Ferrari Portofino in 2018. The base Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price (MSRP) for these model years was $198,973 for 2015, and $202,723 for both the 2016 and 2017 model years. This guide will delve into the specifics of owning and appreciating this Grand Tourer, offering insights crucial for any prospective buyer.

## 2. Changes by Model Year

The Ferrari California T underwent several refinements during its production run, with each model year bringing subtle yet impactful improvements:

*   **2015:** The inaugural year for the California T in the U.S. saw a strong focus on interior enhancements and infotainment system upgrades. The cabin received improved materials, including premium leather and higher-grade finishes, alongside redesigned seats for better comfort and ergonomics. The infotainment system benefited from a software update, offering a more intuitive user interface, upgraded navigation with detailed maps and real-time traffic, and enhanced Bluetooth connectivity. The display screen also saw improvements in resolution and responsiveness.

*   **2016:** This model year continued with the standard offerings, maintaining the enhancements introduced in the previous year.

*   **2017:** This model year was highlighted by the introduction of the highly anticipated **Handling Speciale (HS) Package** (announced in early 2016 but available for 2017 models). This optional package was designed to offer a sportier driving experience with a stiffer suspension setup and a more aggressive sports exhaust system. It also included unique badging and interior trim options for aesthetic customization. As the California T approached its final production year, Ferrari also implemented further refinements to the car's software systems, enhancing the responsiveness and functionality of various electronic controls and infotainment features.

## 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

The Ferrari California T was primarily offered as a single trim level: the **T Convertible 2D**. Unlike some other Ferrari models, there were no other distinct trim levels or special editions in the U.S. market during its 2015-2017 production years. The most significant variation available was the **Handling Speciale (HS) Package**, introduced for the 2017 model year. While not a separate trim level, its comprehensive impact on driving dynamics and inclusion of unique aesthetic elements allowed it to function as a performance-oriented sub-trim, significantly altering the car's character.

## 4. Available Factory Options / Packages

Ferrari is renowned for its extensive customization options. While a complete list with U.S. pricing can be elusive, several notable factory options and packages for the California T have been identified:

*   **Handling Speciale (HS) Package (2017):** Priced at $8,120, this package was a significant performance upgrade, including a sportier suspension, a sports exhaust, unique badging, and interior trim.
*   **HELE (High Emotion Low Emission) System:** This start/stop system, while more common on earlier California models, was an option. However, owner feedback often advises against it due to potential error messages and increased battery demands.
*   **Full Electric Daytona Seats:** A comfortable seating option frequently praised by owners.
*   **AFS System (Adaptive Frontlight System):** An exterior option that enhances visibility during night driving.
*   **Heat Insulating Windscreen:** An exterior option designed to improve cabin comfort.
*   **Touring Suspension:** An option priced at $5,500, likely offering a more compliant ride.
*   **Auto-dimming Side Mirrors:** A convenience option costing over $3,000.
*   **Upgraded Sound System:** An enhancement from 7 to 12 speakers, carrying a premium of $6,242.
*   **Carbon Ceramic Brakes:** These high-performance brakes were standard on the California T.
*   **Apple CarPlay:** The California T was one of the first Ferrari models to feature in-built Apple CarPlay functionality, debuting shortly after its introduction in the Ferrari FF.

## 5. Must-Have Factory Options

Enthusiast consensus suggests that a **2017 Ferrari California T equipped with the Handling Speciale (HS) Package** is a highly desirable configuration for those seeking a more engaging drive. This recommendation is based on several compelling factors:

*   **Enhanced Driving Dynamics:** The HS package significantly transforms the car's handling with a stiffer suspension and a more engaging sports exhaust. Enthusiast consensus suggests this directly addresses criticisms of the standard California T being too soft for spirited driving.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

When considering a Ferrari California T, prospective buyers should be aware of specific recalls affecting the U.S. market:

*   **Brake Fluid Reservoir Cap (Recall 22V536000):** A major recall affecting 2015-2017 models involves the brake fluid reservoir cap, which may not vent properly, potentially causing a vacuum that can lead to a brake fluid leak and a partial or complete loss of brake function.
*   **Low-Pressure Fuel Line (Recall 15V840000):** Affecting specific 2016 models, this recall addresses a low-pressure fuel line that may not connect properly to the fuel pump feed pipe, posing a risk of a fuel leak and potential fire hazard.
