
# Aston Martin V8 Vantage Gen 1 (2009–2017) Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Aston Martin V8 Vantage Gen 1 (2009–2017) is widely considered one of the most beautiful sports cars ever made. For the 2009 model year, Aston Martin upgraded the engine from 4.3L to a much more robust 4.7L V8 producing 420 horsepower, fixing many of the early car's power deficits. As an Exotic Car Hack, the 4.7L Vantage is a fantastic entry-level exotic that has largely bottomed out in depreciation. The manual transmission cars are highly sought after and appreciate, while the SportShift automated manuals are cheaper but suffer from clunky operation and expensive clutch wear. The primary risk is a rare but catastrophic big end bearing failure on the 4.7L engine.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

PPI reminder: Use an Aston Martin specialist. Focus on clutch life (via computer readout for SportShift cars) and engine health.

### Known Problems / Inspection Issues

| Issue | What To Know / Check |
|---|---|
| Big End Bearing Failure | The most terrifying issue for the 4.7L V8. A sporadic but catastrophic failure of the rod bearings that destroys the engine. Listen for knocking noises from the lower engine and check oil pressure history. |
| Premature Clutch Wear | SportShift transmissions (especially the 6-speed SSI) eat clutches if driven like a traditional automatic (e.g., creeping in traffic). Replacement is $4,000–$6,000. Always request a clutch life readout during the PPI. |
| Door Strut Failure | The swan doors are heavy, and the gas struts fail frequently, causing the doors to fall closed on your legs. Cheap and easy to fix. |
| Washer Fluid Reservoir Leaks | The plastic reservoir cracks easily. Check for puddles under the front right of the car. |
| Infotainment / Navigation Failure | The pop-up Volvo/Garmin screens frequently break their gears, and the systems are prone to freezing. |
| Central Locking Motor Failure | Doors may intermittently fail to lock or unlock. |

### Known NHTSA Recalls To Verify By VIN

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 17V-390 / 17V-391 | SportShift Transmission Connector | 2011–2014 | Connector failure on 7-speed SportShift II transmission leads to a loss of clutch fluid, inability to select gears, or stalling. |
| 17V-389 | Transmission Software | 2011–2012 | Software issue causing the 7-speed SportShift II ASM transmission to go into neutral without warning or clutch slipping/overheating. |
| 14V-010 | Throttle Pedal Lever | 2008–2014 | The throttle pedal lever can fracture, leading to a loss of engine speed control. |

## 8. Conclusion

The 4.7L V8 Vantage is a spectacular entry-level exotic. The golden rule is to buy a 6-speed manual; they are reliable, engaging, and hold their value exceptionally well. The 2015 Vantage GT manual is highly regarded by enthusiasts as one of the best value propositions in the Aston Martin portfolio. If you must buy an automated manual, stick to 2012 or newer models with the 7-speed SportShift II, and demand a clutch wear readout before buying. Avoid 2009–2011 SportShift cars unless heavily discounted, and always get a PPI to listen for the dreaded big end bearing knock.
