# Aston Martin DBS Superleggera (2019–2023) Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Aston Martin DBS Superleggera (2019–2023) is a monstrous, 715-horsepower twin-turbo V12 grand tourer that serves as the ultimate flagship of the modern Aston Martin lineup. Based on the DB11 but draped in aggressive carbon fiber bodywork, it produces a staggering 664 lb-ft of torque. As an Exotic Car Hack, the DBS Superleggera is a depreciation masterpiece—a $330,000+ hyper-GT that can be acquired for significantly less on the secondary market. The primary risks involve expensive oil leaks (oil pan and timing cover) and the typical electrical gremlins associated with modern Aston Martins.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

PPI reminder: Use an Aston Martin specialist. The primary focus must be on finding engine oil leaks, which are devastatingly expensive to fix.

### Known Problems / Inspection Issues

| Issue | What To Know / Check |
|---|---|
| Oil Pan and Timing Cover Leaks | The most critical mechanical issue. The 5.2L twin-turbo V12 is prone to leaking from the oil pan and timing cover. Repairing these leaks is extremely labor-intensive and expensive. A lift inspection during PPI is absolutely mandatory. |
| Squealing Carbon Ceramic Brakes | The massive CCB rotors are notorious for squealing at low speeds. While it doesn't compromise safety, it is highly irritating. Check rotor condition and pad life. |
| Exhaust Gasket Leaks | Can cause a ticking noise and impede vehicle performance or trigger emissions warnings. |
| Faulty O2 Sensors | Frequent failures lead to Check Engine Lights, reduced fuel economy, and rough running. |
| Electronic / Infotainment Issues | Random glitches affecting the infotainment system, stereo dropouts, or spurious dashboard warnings. |

### Known NHTSA Recalls To Verify By VIN

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20V-604 / 19V-806 | Front Seat Airbag Fasteners | 2019–2020 | Loose fasteners can cause the airbag to deploy improperly, increasing injury risk. Dealers will tighten or replace the fasteners. |

## 8. Conclusion

The Aston Martin DBS Superleggera is a depreciation monster, making it one of the most compelling V12 hyper-GT hacks on the market. The 2019 and 2020 models have absorbed the worst of the depreciation curve and offer $330,000 performance for a fraction of the price. However, the twin-turbo V12 is susceptible to catastrophic oil pan and timing cover leaks. If a PPI uncovers an active oil leak, walk away or demand a massive discount to cover the engine-out repair. Buy a highly optioned car (B&O audio, heavy carbon fiber) in a unique color to ensure liquidity when it's time to sell.
