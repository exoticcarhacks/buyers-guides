## Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) and Known Issues

A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection by a qualified Porsche specialist is paramount for any GT3 Touring. Focus on these known areas:

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Engine premature wear (hydraulic lash adjusters, variable oil pressure system) | While not widespread, some instances of premature wear in hydraulic lash adjusters and issues with the variable oil pressure system have been reported. Symptoms can include unusual engine noises or reduced performance. A comprehensive PPI should include an engine bore scope inspection and oil analysis. Repair costs can be significant, potentially ranging from $5,000 to $15,000+ depending on the extent of the issue. |
| Oil leaks | General oil leaks are not a common, widespread issue specific to the 991.2 GT3 Touring. If observed during a PPI, investigate the source thoroughly, as it could indicate a minor gasket issue or a more significant problem. |
| Paint issues | No widespread or model-specific paint issues are commonly reported for the 991.2 GT3 Touring. Any paint imperfections are typically due to environmental factors or previous owner care. Inspect for rock chips, swirl marks, and consistent panel gaps during a PPI. |
| Rear wheel steering sensitivity to diagonal tie downs | self-corrected by driving - no cost |
| Exhaust valve tubing coming undone | This is a known minor issue where the exhaust valve tubing can come loose, leading to rattling noises. It's generally not a performance concern but can be an audible annoyance. Easily identifiable during a PPI. Repair is typically straightforward and relatively inexpensive, often under $500. |
| Intake manifold issues (loss of power under full load) | While rare, some owners have reported issues with the intake manifold leading to a loss of power under full load. This can be difficult to diagnose without specific fault codes or dyno testing. A PPI should include a thorough diagnostic scan for any stored fault codes. |

### NHTSA Recalls

Two NHTSA recalls affect the 2018 and 2019 Porsche 911 GT3 models:
*   **19V875000:** Improper Child Restraint System (CRS) instructions in the owner's manual. This recall addresses potential confusion regarding child seat installation.
*   **19V179000:** Airbag sensors may be loose. This recall involves the potential for front passenger airbag sensors to be improperly secured, which could affect airbag deployment.
Verify by VIN before purchase.
