# Ferrari Portofino and Portofino M 2018-2023 Buyer Guide

The Ferrari Portofino, introduced in 2018 as the successor to the California T, and its more potent evolution, the Portofino M, represent Ferrari's accessible grand touring convertible. Powered by a potent 3.9L twin-turbo V8, these models offer a compelling blend of performance and open-air driving, making them intriguing candidates for the discerning exotic car enthusiast seeking a "hackable" Ferrari. However, potential buyers must be acutely aware of the significant brake defect affecting these vehicles, which poses a critical safety risk and is the primary concern for ownership.

## Known Problems and Recalls

Always perform a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a Ferrari specialist.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Power Top/Roof Mechanism Issues | Owners have reported persistent problems with the roof cycle, receiving error messages like “Complete roof cycle” and “Roof Failure.” Replacement roof can cost $26,000 before labor or tax. Hydraulic cylinder leak repair at a dealership can be $1,200 for labor plus $600 for parts. |
| Engine Cylinder Head Oil Leaks | Mentioned as a potential major issue in owner forums. Dealer quotes range from $3,000 to $4,500 for repair. |
| Gearbox Issues | Mentioned as a potential major issue in owner forums. Complete DCT replacement can cost $45,000 (CAD), with repairs around $30,000 (CAD). Refurbished gearboxes are available for $19,000 plus shipping and core charge. |
| Clutch slave cylinder weeping | Reported in owner forums. Cost not specified. |
| Engine cooling fan failure | Reported in owner forums. Cost not specified. |

## Conclusion

The Ferrari Portofino and Portofino M are **medium hack candidates**. While they offer compelling performance and the allure of a modern Ferrari convertible, the significant brake defect represents a major risk that necessitates careful consideration and due diligence. The best ownership logic involves thorough pre-purchase inspection, understanding the implications of the brake recall, and budgeting for potential future repairs, especially concerning the power top mechanism and gearbox.
