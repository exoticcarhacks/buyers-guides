# Ferrari California (2010-2014) Buyer's Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Ferrari California was produced from 2008 to 2014 globally, with U.S. model years typically running from 2009 to 2014. The 2014 model year was the last for the original California before the introduction of the California T. It represented a significant departure for the marque, offering a more accessible grand touring experience with its retractable hardtop and front-mid mounted V8 engine. Designed to attract new buyers to the brand, it blended performance with everyday usability. This guide focuses on the U.S. model years 2010-2014, including the transitional 2014 California T, providing essential insights for prospective owners.

## 2. Changes by Model Year

**2010 Ferrari California:**
*   Introduction of the optional **Handling Speciale package**, featuring a stiffer suspension setup for sportier handling.

**2011 Ferrari California:**
*   Minor tweaks to software and interior features to enhance the driving experience.

**2012 Ferrari California:**
*   Significant update with a **weight reduction of 66 lbs** (achieved through aluminum usage), improving handling and agility.
*   Engine output for the **Ferrari California 30** (or California 30) increased to **490 hp** (from 453 hp).
*   Refined suspension settings for improved handling dynamics.

**2013 Ferrari California:**
*   Marked the end of the original Ferrari California production.

**2014 Ferrari California T:**
*   Introduced as the successor to the California, featuring a new **3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine** producing **553 hp** and **557 lb-ft of torque**.
*   0-60 mph in 3.6 seconds, top speed of 196 mph.
*   Updated design with a more aggressive front fascia, new headlights, and improved aerodynamics.
*   Enhanced infotainment system, new steering wheel design, and additional customization options.

## 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

The original Ferrari California (2010-2013) did not feature traditional trim levels. It was offered as a single base model with various optional packages and individual options. The most notable special edition/package was the **Handling Speciale package**, introduced in 2010, which focused on performance enhancements.

For the 2014 model year, the successor, the **Ferrari California T**, was introduced. This was a new model rather than a trim level of the original California, signifying a significant evolution in the California lineage.
