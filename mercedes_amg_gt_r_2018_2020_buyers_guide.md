## Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) and Known Issues



### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Power steering pump failure | Symptoms include whining noises, heavy steering, or fluid leaks. Inspect for leaks around the pump and reservoir. Replacement cost can range from $300 to $1,350, depending on parts and labor. |
| Dynamic engine/transmission mounts issues | Symptoms include excessive engine vibration, clunking noises, and a rough feel, especially under dynamic driving. Inspect mounts for cracks, looseness, or hydraulic fluid leaks. Replacement costs can range from $2,500 to $5,000+ for parts and labor, depending on the specific mount and repair facility. |
| Exhaust rattling (2020 AMG GTR Pro) | $10,000 for full exhaust replacement |
| Electrical problems (sensor failures, battery drainage) | Symptoms include warning lights (check engine, battery), intermittent system malfunctions (e.g., blind spot assist, cruise control), or the car failing to start. Common sensor issues can include intake manifold pressure sensors, vehicle speed sensors, or RPM sensors. Battery drainage can be caused by parasitic draws, faulty electronics, or issues with the lithium-ion battery's charging system. A thorough diagnostic scan for fault codes and inspection of wiring and connectors is crucial. Repair costs vary widely depending on the specific component, but can range from a few hundred dollars for a sensor to several thousand for complex electrical system diagnostics and repairs. |

## U.S. Production Numbers

The 2020 Mercedes-AMG GT R Roadster was a limited production model with only 750 units produced worldwide. The AMG GT R Pro, also a 2020 model year vehicle, had approximately 150 units allocated to the U.S. market out of 750 built worldwide.

## Conclusion

The Mercedes AMG GT R offers exceptional performance. Its main risk is the potential for expensive repairs on its highly complex powertrain and electronics. The best ownership logic involves acquiring a well-optioned example with a documented service history, ideally one that has already taken its initial depreciation hit.
