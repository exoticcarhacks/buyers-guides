# McLaren 675LT 2016 Buyer Guide

The McLaren 675LT is a limited-production, track-focused evolution of the 650S, offering significant weight reduction, increased power, and enhanced aerodynamics. It is a prime hack candidate due to its limited production numbers (500 Coupes, 500 Spiders) and historical depreciation curve, making it a highly desirable collector and driver car. The main risk involves potential high running costs associated with specialized components like the Proactive Chassis Control (PCC) accumulators and track-related wear.

## Powertrain & Specs

The McLaren 675LT is powered by a 3.8-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine. It utilizes a 7-speed dual-clutch Seamless-Shift Gearbox (SSG) supplied by Graziano Trasmissioni. The car is Rear-Wheel Drive (RWD).

## Known Problems & Recalls

Always secure a comprehensive Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) from a qualified McLaren specialist before purchase.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| AC randomly "calibrates" for 10-15 seconds | Minor issue, no cost specified |
| Check engine light for "misfires on multiple cylinders" | Minor issue, no cost specified |
| Passenger window erratic operation | Minor issue, no cost specified |
| Rear wing post loose/rattles | Minor issue, no cost specified |
| Alarm goes off randomly | Minor issue, no cost specified |
| PCC fault to "stop the car" | Related to accumulators, cost not specified but covered under warranty for some |
| Front splitter issues (car is very low) | Damage from scraping, cost not specified |
| Trickle charger failed | Replacement cost not specified |
| TPMS error | Sporadic error, cost not specified |
| Overheating (oil and water temps getting high) during track use | Cost not specified, but car spent time at service center |
| Proactive Chassis Control (PCC) hydraulic accumulators lose pressure over time | Affects ride and steering. V Engineering offers a Senna-based, rechargeable accumulator upgrade as a solution. |
| Graziano gearbox leaks | Common problem, previously necessitated £20,000+ transmission replacement. V Engineering can now sort leaks. |
| Lightened con-rods not strong enough | Occurs when power is added without strengthening internals. |

## Conclusion

The McLaren 675LT is a strong hack candidate due to its limited production and established depreciation floor. The main risk is the cost of out-of-warranty repairs, particularly concerning the PCC accumulators and transmission leaks. The best ownership logic is to buy a well-documented, properly serviced example with essential options like Vehicle Lift and Carbon Fiber packages, enjoy it for 6-12 months, and exit with minimal depreciation.
