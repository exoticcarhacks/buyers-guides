# BMW Alpina B7 2017-2022 Buyer Guide

The BMW Alpina B7 (G12 generation, 2017-2022) is a high-performance luxury sedan, representing a unique blend of BMW's engineering and Alpina's bespoke craftsmanship. It offers significant performance and luxury, often available at a fraction of its original MSRP due to depreciation. The primary consideration for buyers is the potential cost and complexity of maintaining its sophisticated powertrain and air suspension systems.

### Engine and Performance Specifications

The Alpina B7 is powered by a 4.4-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine, producing 600 horsepower and 590 lb-ft of torque. Power is delivered through an 8-speed automatic transmission to all four wheels via BMW's xDrive all-wheel-drive system.

*   **0-60 mph:** 3.6 seconds (2017-2019 models), 3.5 seconds (2020+ models)
*   **Top Speed:** 193 mph (2017-2019 models), 205 mph (2020+ models)

## Known Problems & Recalls


### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Turbocharger Coolant Line Leaks | Specifically, plastic coolant lines connected to the turbochargers (often with plastic T-fittings) are prone to cracking and leaking, leading to coolant loss. Inspect for visible leaks around the turbochargers and check coolant levels regularly. Repair often requires turbocharger removal. |
| Air Suspension Components | Common failures include leaks in the air springs (air bags) or issues with the air suspension compressor, leading to the vehicle sagging, uneven ride height, or a harsh ride. Listen for compressor noise and check for visible sagging when the vehicle is parked overnight. |
| G11/G12 Front and Rear Air Suspension Struts | Limited warranty extension to 8 years/80,000 miles due to loss of air when stationary, causing vehicle to drop or lean, and acoustic noises (rattling, cracking). |
| N63 Engine Concerns | While the Alpina B7 uses the updated N63TU2 engine, which addressed many issues of earlier N63 variants, some owners still report higher-than-average oil consumption. Monitor oil levels regularly. Valve stem seal failures, common in earlier N63s, are less prevalent but still a potential concern. A comprehensive PPI should include a leak-down test and inspection for oil leaks. |
