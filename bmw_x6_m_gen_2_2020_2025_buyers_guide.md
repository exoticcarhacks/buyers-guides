### Known Problems & Recalls


#### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Engine Failure (S63/S68 V8) | While not widespread, some owners have reported issues with the S63 and S68 V8 engines, including oil consumption or, in rare cases, catastrophic failure. This is often linked to aggressive driving without proper warm-up/cool-down or extended oil change intervals. Check service records for consistent maintenance, look for oil leaks around the valve covers and oil pan, and listen for abnormal engine noises (e.g., ticking, knocking) during a cold start and throughout the test drive. A Blackstone Labs oil analysis can provide insights into engine health. |
| Adaptive M Suspension | The Adaptive M Suspension can be firm, especially in Sport+ modes, which some drivers may perceive as harsh. This is generally a characteristic of the performance tuning rather than a defect. However, inspect for worn suspension components (bushings, control arms, struts) if there is excessive wandering, uneven tire wear, or clunking noises over bumps. Test drive on varied surfaces to evaluate ride comfort and stability. |
| Integrated Brake System (IBS) Defect | Potential problems with the integrated brake system could lead to increased brake pedal force requirements, affecting ABS and stability control functions. Verify if the electronic brake system controller replacement recall has been performed (2023-2025 models). |
| Roof Function Center (FZD) Control Unit Software Issue | The FZD control unit software may not have been programmed to specifications, allowing the sunroof to close without the remote control key inside the vehicle, posing a safety risk. Verify if the FZD control unit reprogramming recall has been performed (2020 models). |
