# Porsche GT3 991.1 2014-2016 Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The Porsche 991.1 GT3, produced from 2014 to 2016, represents a pinnacle of naturally aspirated performance within the 911 lineage, making it a prime candidate for the discerning enthusiast seeking a high-performance vehicle with strong investment potential. Its raw driving experience, combined with limited production numbers, positions it as a desirable asset in the exotic car market. However, prospective owners must be acutely aware of the primary risks associated with this model: potential engine failures and PDK transmission issues, which can lead to significant repair costs if not properly addressed.

## Known Problems & Recalls
Always perform a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a qualified Porsche specialist before purchase.

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Engine failure (connecting rod bolts, finger followers) | This issue led to a recall (NHTSA 14V-090) and an extended 10-year/120,000-mile warranty on the engine. Verify that all recall work has been performed and that the extended warranty is still valid. Listen for unusual engine noises, check for oil leaks. |
| PDK transmission issues | Some owners have reported issues with the PDK transmission, including leaks and failures. Check for smooth shifts, any warning lights, and inspect for fluid leaks around the transmission housing. |

### NHTSA Recalls
| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 14V-090 | 2014 | Engine crankcase damage due to loosened piston rod screw, leading to oil leakage and potential fire. |
| 19V-875 | 2014-2016 | Improper CRS Instructions in Owner\'s Manual |
