# Corvette Z06 C7 2015-2019 Buyer Guide

The C7 Corvette Z06, produced from 2015 to 2019, represents a significant performance bargain in the exotic car market. With its supercharged LT4 V8 engine, it delivers supercar-level performance at a fraction of the cost, making it a prime candidate for the Exotic Car Hacks philosophy. The main risk lies in potential issues related to its high-performance nature, particularly concerning cooling and transmission reliability in early models.

## Known Issues and Recalls

Always perform a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a qualified specialist.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Cracked and warped wheels | Class action lawsuits filed against GM for defective and cracking C7 Corvette wheels (2015-2019 Z06s and 2017-2019 Grand Sports). |
| Overheating issues | Class action lawsuits filed against GM for overheating problems in C7 Z06 models (2015-2018). GM issued Special Coverage #N202302720 for overheating. |
| Automatic transmission problems | Class action lawsuit against GM for faulty 8-speed automatic transmissions (2015-2019 models). GM issued TSB #18NA355 regarding original automatic transmission fluid moisture content problems. |
| Infotainment screen issues | description and cost — Varies, may require wiring repair, HMI module replacement, or screen replacement. |
| Early LT1 engine issues | Some early-build C7 owners experienced blown engines due to connecting-rod bearing failure, machining burrs, and oil filter issues. |
| Interior rattles and creaks | description and cost — Varies, often minor and cosmetic. |
