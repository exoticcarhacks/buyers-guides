The main risk lies in the potential for high maintenance costs associated with its complex S63TU4 twin-turbo V8 engine and advanced M-specific components.

## 6. Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) and Known Issues
A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a reputable BMW M specialist is non-negotiable for any M8 purchase. This is crucial to identify potential issues before they become costly problems.

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| S63TU4 Rod Bearing Wear (M8/M8 Competition) | premature rod bearing wear due to tight bearing clearances and extreme heat/boost pressure stress |
| S63TU4 V8 Excessive Oil Consumption | oil consumption exceeding 1 quart per 1,500 miles, related to valve stem seals and piston ring design |
| Adaptive Suspension Damper Leaks | adaptive M suspension dampers can develop leaks from electronically controlled valving |
| M8 Carbon Ceramic Brake (CCB) Issues | premature rotor cracking, excessive squealing, and thermal shock damage |

## 8. Conclusion
The BMW M8 is a **strong hack candidate** for those seeking immense performance and luxury at a depreciated price point. The main risk is the potential for significant repair costs, particularly related to the S63TU4 engine and specialized M components. The best ownership logic involves diligent pre-purchase inspection, setting aside a maintenance fund, and understanding that while depreciation is your friend, running costs can be substantial.
