# Porsche 911 Turbo S 991.2 2017-2019 Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The Porsche 911 Turbo S 991.2, produced from 2017 to 2019, represents the pinnacle of Porsche's 911 performance, offering blistering acceleration and track-ready dynamics in a surprisingly usable package. While these models initially experienced depreciation, recent market data suggests a more stable or even appreciating market for well-maintained examples, making them compelling acquisition candidates for enthusiasts seeking supercar performance. The primary ownership risks are potential turbocharger and oil pump failures, which can be costly to address outside of warranty.

## Known Problems & Recalls

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| **Oil feed issues leading to turbo failure** | This is a significant concern, particularly for cars that are sparingly used or have not had consistent oil changes. Symptoms often include excessive blue or white smoke from the exhaust, especially on startup or after idling, and a noticeable loss of power. The issue is often attributed to carbon buildup in the oil lines leading to the turbochargers, restricting oil flow and causing premature wear. While replacement turbos are often the same part, regular oil analysis and ensuring the car is driven regularly can help mitigate risk. A comprehensive warranty is highly recommended, and a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) should specifically check for any signs or history of turbo-related issues, including inspecting the turbo inlet tubes for oil residue [5]. |
| **Fragile oil pumps** | Often associated with the turbocharger issues, oil pump failures can lead to catastrophic engine damage due to insufficient lubrication. Symptoms may include a sudden drop in oil pressure (indicated by a warning light or gauge), unusual engine noises (ticking, knocking), or engine seizure. While less common than turbo issues, it's a critical concern. Ensure the PPI includes a thorough inspection of the oil system's health, including oil pressure tests and a review of service records for any related repairs [5]. |

## Market Analysis

The Porsche 911 Turbo S 991.2 (2017-2019) has demonstrated a remarkably stable market value, with some examples even appreciating in recent years, particularly well-optioned and low-mileage cars. Unlike many high-performance vehicles that experience significant initial depreciation, the 991.2 Turbo S has shown resilience, often retaining a substantial portion of its original MSRP. For instance, data from Classic.com indicates an average sale price of $193,143 for the 991.2 Turbo S [9]. This trend suggests that the claim of 
"rapid depreciation from new" is not consistently supported by recent market data. Instead, the market for these models appears to be more stable or appreciating, especially for desirable configurations. Buyers should focus on acquiring well-maintained examples with thorough service histories to capitalize on this market stability.

## Conclusion

The Porsche 911 Turbo S 991.2 is a **strong hack candidate** for those seeking supercar performance and luxury with a stable market value. The main risks remain the potential for turbocharger and oil pump failures, making a comprehensive warranty and thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) paramount. The best ownership logic involves acquiring a well-optioned example, ideally with an active warranty, and enjoying its immense capabilities while mitigating the known mechanical risks.
