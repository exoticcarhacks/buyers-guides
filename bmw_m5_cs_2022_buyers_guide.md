## Powertrain & Performance

The 2022 BMW M5 CS is powered by a 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 engine, producing 627 horsepower and 553 lb-ft of torque. Power is sent to all four wheels via an 8-speed M Steptronic automatic transmission. This formidable powertrain allows the M5 CS to accelerate from 0-60 mph in just 2.9 seconds and reach a top speed of 190 mph.

## Known Problems and Recalls

**Always get a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) from a qualified specialist.**

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Transmission Rough Shifting and Hesitation | Abrupt gear changes, delayed response, occasional jerks, particularly in lower gears and during aggressive downshifts. May require software updates or mechanical adjustment. |
| Coolant Reservoir Leak and Low Coolant Warning | Dashboard alerts, visible coolant drops, puddles under car. Common issue with OEM plastic reservoir developing micro-cracks or poor sealing. |
| Exhaust Flap Malfunction | Inconsistent exhaust notes, error messages, rattling noises. Often due to faulty actuators or wiring. |
| Suspension Creaks and Clunks | Audible noises from front or rear suspension, looseness in steering, occasional vibration. Check for worn bushings, loose sway bar links, or degraded shock mounts. |
| High Tire Wear and Understeer | Rapid tread loss on front tires, increased understeer, instability in wet conditions. Inherent characteristic of high-performance setup and factory alignment, leading to frequent tire replacement. |
| Abrupt Brake Pedal Feel and Rapid ABS Engagement | Strong initial bite, difficulty modulating brake pressure, frequent ABS activation. Inherent characteristic of the carbon ceramic brake system, not a mechanical fault. |
| Infotainment and Digital Dashboard Glitches | Unresponsive navigation, Bluetooth drops, frozen digital instrument clusters. Often resolved with system resets or dealer intervention. |
| Low Headrest Position and Comfort Niggles | Fixed headrests positioned too low for taller drivers, discomfort on long drives. This is a design characteristic of the carbon bucket seats, not a defect. |
