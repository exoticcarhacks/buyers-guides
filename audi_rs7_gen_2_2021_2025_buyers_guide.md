# Audi RS7 Gen 2 2021-2025 Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The Audi RS7 Gen 2, produced from 2021 to 2025, is a high-performance sportback that blends luxury with blistering speed. Its potent 4.0L twin-turbo V8 engine and sophisticated all-wheel-drive system make it a compelling choice for those seeking high-performance in a premium package. While known for its robust engineering, potential buyers should be aware of reported issues with the 48-volt mild hybrid system, which can be a significant ownership consideration.

## Known Problems & Recalls

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| 48-volt mild hybrid system/alternator failure | This is a common problem in early Gen 2 models (2021-2022), leading to various electrical issues and potential vehicle immobilization. Symptoms include dashboard warning lights, erratic electrical behavior, or complete power loss. Inspect service records for any related repairs or replacements. Cost: $2,000 - $4,000 (estimated, based on complexity of repair and part cost) |
| MMI infotainment system glitches | Intermittent freezing, slow response, or black screen issues have been reported across various model years. Check for proper functionality of all infotainment features during a test drive. Cost: $500 - $1,500 (estimated, depending on software update or module replacement) |
| Oil separator failure | Can cause a noticeable whistling or whining noise from the engine bay, and is a labor-intensive repair due to its location. Inspect for unusual engine noises and check service history for PCV system maintenance. Cost: $1,000 - $2,500 (estimated, based on labor and part cost) |
| Inaccurate fuel count in digital instrument cluster | A software-related issue that can cause incorrect fuel level readings. This is typically addressed by a software update. Cost: Covered by warranty/software update (estimated) |
| Faulty rear axle alignment | Can lead to premature and uneven tire wear, as well as compromised handling. Inspect tire wear patterns and request a recent alignment report. Cost: $200 - $500 (estimated, for alignment and potential component replacement) |
| Dash delamination | Reported in some instances, requiring dash replacement. Inspect the dashboard for any signs of bubbling or separation. Cost: $3,000 - $6,000 (estimated, based on parts and extensive labor) |
| Oxygen sensor failure | Can lead to reduced fuel efficiency and illuminated check engine light. Inspect service records for O2 sensor replacements. Cost: $300 - $700 per sensor (estimated) |
| Air suspension wear | A known weak point, leading to leaks or compressor failure, resulting in uneven ride height or a harsh ride. Inspect for sagging at any corner of the vehicle and listen for compressor noise. Cost: $1,000 - $3,000 per corner (estimated, for air spring or compressor replacement) |
| Timing chain tensioner issues | While less common in Gen 2, issues with timing chain tensioners can lead to catastrophic engine failure if not addressed. Listen for rattling noises from the engine, especially on cold start-up. Cost: $3,000 - $8,000 (estimated, for repair or replacement) |
| Variable intake actuators | Failure can cause engine codes and limit engine RPM. Check for illuminated check engine light and any hesitation or power loss during acceleration. Cost: $800 - $1,500 (estimated, for part and labor) |

### NHTSA Recalls
| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 21V-339 | | 2021 | Engine Block Casting: Due to a process error that occurred during the engine block casting process, it is possible for cracks to develop on the running surface of the ‘cylinder 6’ bore. During engine operation, a crack in the bore can lead to an impaired contour of the piston`s running surface. If this occurs, there is potential for engine damage and oil leakage. Engine damage while driving may cause a loss of power or an engine stall, increasing the risk of a crash. |
| 20V-749 | | 2021 | Door Crash Sensors: The electrical connector can detach from the front door crash sensor on both sides of the car. If this happens, the side airbags and seat belt pretensioners may not deploy as intended in a side impact crash, increasing the risk of injury. |
| 22V-178 | | 2014-2017 (Previous Gen RS7) | Oil Screen Safety Recall 21H7. This recall applies to the previous generation (C7) RS7 models, not the Gen 2 (C8) models (2021-2025). |

## Conclusion
The Audi RS7 Gen 2 remains a compelling choice for those who prioritize performance and luxury in a practical package. While generally robust, potential owners should be particularly mindful of the reported issues with the **48-volt mild hybrid system**. Proactive maintenance and budgeting for potential electrical system repairs are advisable to ensure long-term reliability and enjoyment.
