# Aston Martin DBX 707 (2022–2025) Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Aston Martin DBX 707 (2022–2025) is a hyper-SUV that redefined the segment upon its release. Powered by an AMG-sourced 4.0L twin-turbo V8 producing a massive 697 horsepower (707 PS) and paired with a 9-speed wet-clutch transmission, it offers true supercar performance in an SUV chassis. As an Exotic Car Hack, the DBX 707 is highly desirable but, like many luxury SUVs, can experience significant depreciation. Key considerations for buyers include potential electrical anomalies, a dated infotainment system on pre-2024 models, and important NHTSA recalls regarding the rear suspension and oil cooler hose.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

A Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by an Aston Martin specialist is crucial. Focus on suspension health, particularly the rear lower suspension arms, and ensure all electrical modules are updated to the latest software. Verify all open recalls have been completed.

### Known Problems / Inspection Issues

| Issue | What To Know / Check |
|---|---|
| Rear Suspension Failure / Wobble | Owners report a wobbling feel and vibrations from the rear. This is tied to NHTSA recall 26V-200, where rear lower suspension arms can be damaged, leading to a loss of control. Ensure this recall has been addressed. |
| Electrical Anomalies / Battery Drain | Widespread reports of battery drainage, phantom park-assist alerts, and random dashboard warnings. Often requires software flashes to resolve. |
| Suspension Malfunction Warnings | Owners report dashboard warnings stating "Do not exceed 50 MPH" due to air suspension faults. Check for a car leaning to one side after sitting. |
| Outdated Infotainment (2022–2023) | Not a mechanical defect, but the lack of a touchscreen and clunky interface on early models is the #1 owner complaint. |
| Minor Quality Control Issues | Missing roof-panel clips, weak washer pumps, and inadequate rear-seat climate control airflow. |

### Known NHTSA Recalls To Verify By VIN

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 26V-200 | Rear Suspension Arm | 2021–2026 | Damage to the rear lower suspension arm(s) may occur, which can lead to a loss of vehicle control. This is a critical safety recall. |
| 25V-001 | Oil Cooler Hose | 2024 | An oil hose for the oil cooler may burst, causing a massive oil leak and increasing the risk of an engine fire. This recall specifically affects 2024 DBX 707 models. |
| 26V-187 | Vehicle Control Unit (VCU) | 2024–2026 | Incorrect coding of the VCU may cause the engine to stall while driving. This is a confirmed recall. |

## 8. Conclusion

The Aston Martin DBX 707 is one of the fastest and best-handling SUVs on the planet, but the buying strategy is heavily dependent on the model year. 2022 and 2023 models have experienced significant depreciation but come with a frustratingly outdated infotainment system. The 2024 model is often considered the sweet spot, offering the modern touchscreen interior while allowing secondary buyers to avoid the initial MSRP hit. Buyers must be hyper-vigilant about the rear suspension recall (26V-200) and the oil cooler hose recall (25V-001) for 2024 models. A PPI must verify that all applicable recalls have been performed and that the air suspension is leak-free.
