# Aston Martin DBX 707 (2022–2025) Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Aston Martin DBX 707 (2022–2025) is a hyper-SUV that redefined the segment upon its release. Powered by an AMG-sourced 4.0L twin-turbo V8 producing a massive 697 horsepower (707 PS) and paired with a 9-speed wet-clutch transmission, it offers true supercar performance in an SUV chassis. As an Exotic Car Hack, the DBX 707 is highly desirable but suffers from the steep depreciation typical of luxury SUVs. The biggest risks are a slew of frustrating electrical anomalies, a dated infotainment system on pre-2024 models, and a critical NHTSA recall regarding rear suspension failure.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

PPI reminder: Use an Aston Martin specialist. Focus on suspension health and ensure all electrical modules are updated to the latest software.

### Known Problems / Inspection Issues

| Issue | What To Know / Check |
|---|---|
| Rear Suspension Failure / Wobble | Owners report a wobbling feel and vibrations from the rear. This is tied to a major NHTSA recall (26V-200) where rear lower suspension arms can be damaged, leading to a loss of control. |
| Electrical Anomalies / Battery Drain | Widespread reports of battery drainage, phantom park-assist alerts, and random dashboard warnings. Often requires software flashes to resolve. |
| Suspension Malfunction Warnings | Owners report dashboard warnings stating "Do not exceed 50 MPH" due to air suspension faults. Check for a car leaning to one side after sitting. |
| Outdated Infotainment (2022–2023) | Not a mechanical defect, but the lack of a touchscreen and clunky interface on early models is the #1 owner complaint. |
| Minor Quality Control Issues | Missing roof-panel clips, weak washer pumps, and inadequate rear-seat climate control airflow. |

### Known NHTSA Recalls To Verify By VIN

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 26V-200 | Rear Suspension Arm | 2021–2026 | Damage to the rear lower suspension arm(s) may occur, which can lead to a loss of vehicle control. A critical safety recall. |
| 25V-001 / RA-41-2086 | Oil Cooler Hose | 2023–2024 | An oil hose for the oil cooler may burst, causing a massive oil leak and increasing the risk of an engine fire. |
| 26V-187 | Vehicle Control Unit (VCU) | 2024–2026 | Incorrect coding of the VCU may cause the engine to stall while driving. |

## 8. Conclusion

The Aston Martin DBX 707 is one of the fastest and best-handling SUVs on the planet, but the buying strategy is heavily dependent on the model year. 2022 and 2023 models have taken massive depreciation hits but saddle you with a frustratingly outdated infotainment system. The 2024 model is the definitive sweet spot, offering the modern touchscreen interior while still allowing secondary buyers to avoid the initial MSRP hit. Buyers must be hyper-vigilant about the rear suspension recall and the oil cooler hose fire risk. A PPI must verify that all recalls have been performed and that the air suspension is leak-free.
