# McLaren 570S 2016-2020 Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The McLaren 570S is a mid-engine supercar that delivers hypercar-level performance and striking dihedral doors at an accessible price point, making it a prime exotic car hack. Its steep initial depreciation curve allows buyers to enter the McLaren brand at a fraction of the original MSRP, offering tremendous value and liquidity in the secondary market. However, the main risk lies in its reputation for electrical gremlins and potential high-cost repairs, making a thorough pre-purchase inspection and warranty coverage critical.

## Reliability & Maintenance
Always secure a comprehensive Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) from a qualified McLaren specialist before purchase.

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Not starting | Depress brake pedal harder. In rare cases, the brake pedal switch needed to be replaced. |
| Key not recognized | Key should be placed closer to Start/Stop button. |
| Misc dash errors/codes | Mostly "ghost of the machine". Stop/Restart the vehicle, or disconnect battery for 1hr. Sometimes idling for 5min clears codes. |
| Windows roll down after door close | Weather stripping may be protruding, causing window to drop for safety measures. Re-seat the weather stripping. |
| Miscellaneous door latch | If the car is spec'd with "soft close" doors, the latch mechanism likely failed. |
| Comfort entry not working | Battery reset, sometimes a reflash. |
| Battery dying quickly | Key is left too close when vehicle not in use. Constant communication drains the battery. |
| Headlight condensation | Occurs after rain driving or deep cleaning. Let sit and it disappears. |
| Windshield stress cracks | Happens early, dealer needs to use a more flexible sealant. May be accelerated by poor design of frame around windshield. |
| IRIS (interface) errors | Majority are remedied with reset. Software updates have fixed many issues. Requires dealer to update. |
| Coolant tank leaks | Cracked coolant tanks, or loose/bad clamps on hose near tank. Proximity to intercoolers/exhaust causes expansion. |
| Locked out of car | Variety of causes: bad latch, poor signal, low car battery. |
| Small panels falling off | Double-sided tape and adhesive failures. Simple reattach. |
| Oil-related error/s | Due to oil filler cap not fully tightened. |
| Panel misalignments | Build quality from factory. Doors can be adjusted, but likely at cost of fitment elsewhere. |
| Light rattle from engine bay | Heatshield clamp bolts loosened, possible tab on catalytic converter mount snapped. |
| Fender liner becoming detached | Reattach. |
| Part of front bumper flying off | Incorrect re-installation of part (e.g., after PPF application). |
| A/C blowing hot air | Unconfirmed. Stop/restart vehicle. |
| Rear parking brake error | Unclear fix -- may be reset by stop/restart. |
| Paint bubbling | Corrosion on Aluminum - must repaint the panel. 5 year corrosion warranty from McLaren. Most popular on 2016's. |
| Rear diffuser separating | Failure of adhesive. Clean surface then dab silicone adhesive behind fins. |
| Transmission oil leak | Failure of basic o-ring, small but annoying job. |
| Oil leak by variable cam timing harness | Due to leaky o-rings on the filter's plastic housing. Replace whole housing as precaution. |
| Catastrophic motor failure | Rare but very high cost. |

## Conclusion
The McLaren 570S is a **strong** hack candidate due to its massive initial depreciation and hypercar-level curb appeal. The main risk is the potential for expensive out-of-warranty repairs and electrical gremlins, making a thorough PPI and potentially an aftermarket warranty essential. The best ownership logic is to buy a well-optioned 2017+ model (to avoid first-year issues) that has already absorbed the steepest depreciation, enjoy it for 6-12 months, and exit before major maintenance milestones hit.
