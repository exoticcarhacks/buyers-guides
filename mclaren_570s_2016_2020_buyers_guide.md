# McLaren 570S 2016-2020 Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The McLaren 570S is a mid-engine supercar that delivers hypercar-level performance and striking dihedral doors at an accessible price point, making it a prime exotic car hack. Its steep initial depreciation curve allows buyers to enter the McLaren brand at a fraction of the original MSRP, offering tremendous value and liquidity in the secondary market. However, the main risk lies in its reputation for electrical issues and potential high-cost repairs, making a thorough pre-purchase inspection and warranty coverage critical.

## Reliability & Maintenance

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Not starting | In rare cases, the brake pedal switch may need replacement. Ensure the brake pedal is fully depressed when starting. |
| Misc dash errors/codes | Often temporary electrical glitches. Try restarting the vehicle or disconnecting the battery for an hour. Sometimes idling for 5 minutes can clear codes. |
| Windows roll down after door close | Weather stripping may be protruding, causing the window to drop for safety measures. Re-seat the weather stripping. |
| Miscellaneous door latch | If the car is spec'd with "soft close" doors, the latch mechanism may fail, preventing proper door closure. |
| Comfort entry not working | May require a battery reset or a software reflash by a dealer. |
| Battery dying quickly | The key fob, if left too close to the vehicle when not in use, can cause constant communication that drains the battery. Ensure the key is stored away from the car. |
| Windshield stress cracks | Early models may experience stress cracks due to the design of the frame around the windshield and sealant flexibility. Inspect the edges of the windshield for small cracks. |
| IRIS (interface) errors | Most issues are resolved with a system reset. Software updates from the dealer have addressed many common problems. |
| Coolant tank leaks | Cracked coolant tanks or loose/bad clamps on hoses near the tank are common. The proximity to intercoolers/exhaust can cause expansion and stress. Inspect the coolant tank and surrounding hoses for leaks or cracks. |
| Locked out of car | Can be caused by a faulty door latch, poor key fob signal, or a low car battery. Check the key fob battery and car battery charge. |
| Small panels falling off | Double-sided tape and adhesive failures can cause small exterior panels to detach. These can usually be reattached with appropriate adhesive. |
| Oil-related error/s | Often due to the oil filler cap not being fully tightened. Ensure the cap is secure. |
| Panel misalignments | Some vehicles may have minor panel misalignments from the factory. While doors can be adjusted, it may affect fitment elsewhere. |
| Light rattle from engine bay | Heatshield clamp bolts may loosen, or a tab on the catalytic converter mount may snap. Listen for rattles from the engine bay. |
| Fender liner becoming detached | Fender liners can become detached. Inspect for loose liners and reattach as needed. |
| Part of front bumper flying off | Typically occurs due to incorrect re-installation of bumper components, often after PPF application or repairs. |
| Paint bubbling | Corrosion on aluminum panels can lead to paint bubbling, most commonly observed on 2016 models. McLaren offers a 5-year corrosion warranty. Inspect panels, especially on early models, for signs of bubbling. |
| Rear diffuser separating | Adhesive failure can cause the rear diffuser to separate. Clean the surface and re-secure with silicone adhesive behind the fins. |
| Transmission oil leak | Small but annoying leaks can occur from basic o-rings. Inspect the transmission housing for signs of oil leakage. |
| Oil leak by variable cam timing harness | Leaky o-rings on the plastic housing of the oil filter can cause leaks near the variable cam timing harness. Consider replacing the entire housing as a preventative measure. |
| Catastrophic motor failure | While rare, this is a very high-cost issue. There is no specific common cause identified, but regular maintenance and fluid checks are crucial. |

## Conclusion
The McLaren 570S is a **strong** hack candidate due to its massive initial depreciation and hypercar-level curb appeal. The main risk is the potential for expensive out-of-warranty repairs and electrical issues, making a thorough PPI and potentially an aftermarket warranty essential. The best ownership logic is to buy a well-optioned 2017+ model (to avoid first-year issues) that has already absorbed the steepest depreciation, enjoy it for 6-12 months, and exit before major maintenance milestones hit.
