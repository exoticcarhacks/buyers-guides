## Known Problems & NHTSA Recalls
### PPI Reminder
A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a Porsche specialist is paramount. Focus on the common issues outlined below.

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Belt Tensioner Failure | Hydraulic belt tensioner fluid leaks, causing eventual collapse. Recommended replacement around 60,000 miles or every 6 years. |
| Spoiler Motor Failure | Spoiler motor can fail, causing the spoiler to get stuck and trigger an error light. Solution is to replace the motor. |
| Third Brake Light Harness Breakage | Wiring harness becomes brittle from spoiler movement, causing wires to break. Solution is to replace the harness. |
| Door Panel Issues (Warping) | Top of door panels bow upwards due to glue separation from heat. Repair involves re-securing with epoxy and custom tools. |
| Door Panel Issues (Broken Brackets) | Specific plastic bracket inside the door panel breaks. Repair involves epoxy and a custom reinforcing bracket. |
| Changeover Valve Failure | Faulty vacuum lines with electronically controlled solenoids fail, triggering a coolant system fault light. Replace with revised Porsche part. Labor can be intensive depending on valve location. |
| DFI engine intake valve and port build-up | Oil laden vapors get past the AOS and into the intake, leading to significant build-up over time. |
| Cam gear bolts backing out or shearing | Particularly on manual transmission cars. |

## Conclusion
The Porsche 911 Turbo S 991.1 is a compelling candidate. Its main risk lies in the potential for expensive repairs if common issues like belt tensioner failure or changeover valve problems are not proactively addressed. The best ownership logic involves thorough pre-purchase inspections and budgeting for preventative maintenance to ensure long-term enjoyment.
