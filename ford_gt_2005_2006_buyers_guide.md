# Ford GT Gen 1 (2005-2006) Buyer's Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Ford GT Gen 1, produced for the 2005 and 2006 model years, is a highly sought-after American supercar that pays homage to the legendary GT40. This guide details its U.S. market specifications, pricing, model year variations, factory options, safety recalls, common mechanical issues, and provides guidance on selecting the best version from an enthusiast and value perspective.

## 2. Changes by Model Year

Despite being produced for only two years, there were subtle differences and refinements between the 2005 and 2006 Ford GT models.

**2005 Ford GT:**
*   **Debut Year:** The 2005 model marked the reintroduction of the Ford GT, celebrating the company's centennial anniversary [3].
*   **Production Numbers:** A total of 2,022 units were built for the 2005 model year [3].
*   **Initial Color Options:** Available in Mark IV Red, Midnight Blue, Centennial White, and Quick Silver, all typically paired with white racing stripes [3].
*   **Early Production Issues:** While there were reports of suspension control arm issues, the NHTSA recall 06V462000 was for 'Incorrect Park Position Assembly' and not directly for suspension control arms. However, Ford did implement stronger suspension components in 2006 models based on feedback.

**2006 Ford GT:**
*   **Final Production Year:** The 2006 model year concluded the production run for the first-generation Ford GT, with 2,011 units manufactured, making it slightly rarer than the 2005 model [3].
*   **New Color Options:** Introduced new exterior colors, including Tungsten Grey Metallic with white stripes and the highly iconic Heritage Blue with Epic Orange Gulf Livery, which paid tribute to the Le Mans-winning GT40s. The original four colors from 2005 remained available [3].
*   **Refinements and Improvements:** Ford implemented subtle refinements based on feedback from early 2005 production, including stronger suspension components.

## 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

The Ford GT Gen 1 was offered as a single, high-performance model. While there were no distinct trim levels, the introduction of the **Heritage Blue with Epic Orange Gulf Livery** in 2006 served as a special homage to the Le Mans-winning GT40s, making it a highly desirable variant for collectors [3].

For collectors and enthusiasts, certain factory options significantly enhance the value and desirability of a Ford GT Gen 1. ## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

Potential buyers should be aware of several safety recalls and common mechanical issues associated with the 2005-2006 Ford GT. It is crucial to ensure all recalls have been addressed.

**NHTSA Recall Campaign Numbers:**
*   **05V378000:** Fuel Return Hose
*   **05V379000:** Unwanted ABS Activation
*   **06V462000:** Incorrect Park Position Assembly
*   **07V010000:** Tow Hitch Failure
*   **07V011000:** Fuel Tanks


**Common Mechanical Issues:**
*   **Suspension Control Arms:** While there were reports of suspension control arm issues in early 2005 models, it was not a formal NHTSA recall. Ford did, however, implement stronger suspension components in 2006 models based on feedback. Buyers should still verify the condition of these components in any 2005 model.
*   **Airbag Inflators:** Multiple recalls related to Takata airbag inflators affect both model years. Verification of these repairs is paramount for safety.
*   **Clutch Issues:** Some owners have reported premature clutch wear, especially in cars driven aggressively. A thorough inspection of the clutch and transmission is advisable.
*   **Fuel Pump Issues:** While less common, some instances of fuel pump failures have been noted, which can lead to drivability problems.
*   **Electrical Gremlins:** As with many low-volume supercars, minor electrical issues can occasionally arise. These are typically not critical but can be frustrating.

## 8. Conclusion

The Ford GT Gen 1 remains an iconic and highly collectible American supercar. Its limited production, rich racing heritage, and stunning design ensure its place as a desirable asset. While both 2005 and 2006 models offer an exhilarating experience, the 2006 model benefits from subtle refinements, including stronger suspension components. Fully optioned examples, particularly those with the McIntosh stereo and painted racing stripes, tend to command higher values. Prospective buyers must prioritize verifying recall completions and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection to ensure a sound investment in this legendary machine.

## 9. Car Hacking Shortcuts

*   **Verify Recall Status:** Before any purchase, obtain the VIN and check the NHTSA website or contact a Ford dealership to ensure all safety recalls, especially those related to airbags and suspension, have been completed.
*   **Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI):** A comprehensive PPI by a specialist familiar with Ford GTs is non-negotiable. Focus on the clutch, suspension components, and any signs of previous accident damage.
*   **Documentation is Key:** Look for cars with extensive service records, original window stickers, and documentation of factory options. This adds significant value and provides peace of mind.
*   **Consider the 2006 Model:** While both years are fantastic, the 2006 model year received minor improvements, including stronger suspension components, making it a slightly more refined choice.
*   **Optioned Cars Command a Premium:** Be prepared to pay more for cars equipped with desirable factory options like the McIntosh stereo and painted racing stripes, as these are highly sought after by collectors.
*   **Understand Dealer Markups:** Remember that the original MSRP of $139,995 [3] was often exceeded by significant dealer markups due to high demand. Factor this into historical pricing research.
*   **Join Enthusiast Communities:** Engage with Ford GT owner forums and clubs. These communities are invaluable resources for insights into common issues, reputable service centers, and market trends.
