# Ford GT Gen 1 (2005-2006) Buyer's Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Ford GT Gen 1, produced for the 2005 and 2006 model years, is a highly sought-after American supercar that pays homage to the legendary GT40. This guide details its U.S. market specifications, pricing, model year variations, factory options, safety recalls, common mechanical issues, and provides guidance on selecting the best version from an enthusiast and value perspective.

## 2. Changes by Model Year

Despite being produced for only two years, there were subtle differences and refinements between the 2005 and 2006 Ford GT models.

**2005 Ford GT:**
*   **Debut Year:** The 2005 model marked the reintroduction of the Ford GT, celebrating the company's centennial anniversary.
*   **Production Numbers:** A total of 2,022 units were built for the 2005 model year.
*   **Initial Color Options:** Available in Mark IV Red, Midnight Blue, Centennial White, and Quick Silver, all typically paired with white racing stripes.
*   **Early Production Issues:** While there were reports of suspension control arm issues, the NHTSA recall 06V462000 was for 'Incorrect Park Position Assembly' and not directly for suspension control arms. However, Ford did implement stronger suspension components in 2006 models based on feedback.

**2006 Ford GT:**
*   **Final Production Year:** The 2006 model year concluded the production run for the first-generation Ford GT, with 2,011 units manufactured, making it slightly rarer than the 2005 model.
*   **New Color Options:** Introduced new exterior colors, including Tungsten Grey Metallic with white stripes and the highly iconic Heritage Blue with Epic Orange Gulf Livery, which paid tribute to the Le Mans-winning GT40s. The original four colors from 2005 remained available.
*   **Refinements and Improvements:** Ford implemented subtle refinements based on feedback from early 2005 production, including stronger suspension components.

## 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

The Ford GT Gen 1 was offered as a single, high-performance model. While there were no distinct trim levels, the introduction of the **Heritage Blue with Epic Orange Gulf Livery** in 2006 served as a special homage to the Le Mans-winning GT40s, making it a highly desirable variant for collectors.

## 5. Must-Have Factory Options

For collectors and enthusiasts, certain factory options significantly enhance the value and desirability of a Ford GT Gen 1. The **McIntosh Audiophile Stereo System** and **Painted Racing Stripes** are considered must-have options. Fully optioned examples, particularly those with these features, are generally more valuable. The painted racing stripes were a premium option, applied and clear-coated for a seamless finish, unlike vinyl decals, adding to their appeal.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

Potential buyers should be aware of several safety recalls and common mechanical issues associated with the 2005-2006 Ford GT. It is crucial to ensure all recalls have been addressed.

**NHTSA Recall Campaign Numbers:**
*   **05V378000:** Fuel Return Hose
*   **05V379000:** Unwanted ABS Activation
*   **06V462000:** Incorrect Park Position Assembly
*   **07V010000:** Tow Hitch Failure
*   **07V011000:** Fuel Tanks

**Common Mechanical Issues:**
*   **Suspension Control Arms:** While there were reports of suspension control arm issues in early 2005 models, it was not a formal NHTSA recall. Ford did, however, implement stronger suspension components in 2006 models based on feedback. Buyers should still verify the condition of these components in any 2005 model.
*   **Airbag Inflators:** Multiple recalls related to Takata airbag inflators affect both model years. Verification of these repairs is paramount for safety.
*   **Clutch Issues:** Some owners have reported premature clutch wear, especially in cars driven aggressively. A thorough inspection of the clutch and transmission is advisable.
*   **Fuel Pump Issues:** While less common, some instances of fuel pump failures have been noted, which can lead to drivability problems.
*   **Electrical Gremlins:** As with many low-volume supercars, minor electrical issues can occasionally arise. These are typically not critical but can be frustrating.

## 7. Market Analysis & Forecast

Original MSRP at launch was $139,995 for the base 2005 model, with options pushing well-equipped cars to $155,000 or more. In the current used market, well-maintained examples have been trading in the $400,000 to $600,000+ range at auction and private sale as of recent years. The 2006 models featuring the Gulf Heritage livery command the highest premiums.

Value is primarily driven by the Gulf Heritage livery, which holds the highest premium, followed by fully optioned cars equipped with the McIntosh stereo, painted racing stripes, and BBS wheels. Low mileage, no accident history, and a documented service history are also critical factors. Between the two years, the 2006 model is slightly rarer (2,011 units versus 2,022 units for 2005) and offers the exclusive Gulf Heritage option, though both years remain highly collectible. The long-term outlook is strong: with a limited production of approximately 4,033 total units and robust collector demand, the Ford GT is expected to continue appreciating as a landmark American supercar.

## 8. Conclusion

The Ford GT Gen 1 remains an iconic and highly collectible American supercar. Its limited production, rich racing heritage, and stunning design ensure its place as a desirable asset. While both 2005 and 2006 models offer an exhilarating experience, the 2006 model benefits from subtle refinements, including stronger suspension components. Fully optioned examples, particularly those with the McIntosh stereo and painted racing stripes, tend to command higher values. Prospective buyers must prioritize verifying recall completions and conducting a thorough pre-purchase inspection to ensure a sound investment in this legendary machine.

## 9. Car Hacking Shortcuts

*   **Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) Focus Areas:** A comprehensive PPI by a specialist is non-negotiable. Specifically inspect the fuel return hose condition (recall 05V378000), perform an ABS system check (05V379000), examine the park pawl and transmission (06V462000), verify tow hitch integrity (07V010000), and check the fuel tank condition (07V011000). Assess clutch wear and inspect the suspension control arms, particularly on 2005 models.
*   **Target the Right Spec:** The Gulf Heritage Blue and Orange is the most collectible 2006 specification. Fully optioned cars featuring the McIntosh stereo, BBS wheels, and painted racing stripes typically command a 10% to 20% premium over base models.
*   **Consider the 2006 Model:** The 2006 model year received minor improvements, including stronger suspension components, making it a slightly more refined choice.
*   **Understand Dealer Markups:** The original MSRP of $139,995 was often exceeded by significant dealer markups due to high demand. Factor this into historical pricing research.
