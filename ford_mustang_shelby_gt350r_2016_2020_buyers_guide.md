# Ford Mustang Shelby GT350R (2016-2020) Buyer's Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT350R, produced from 2016 to 2020, represents the pinnacle of track-focused performance within the Mustang lineup. Designed as a street-legal race car, the GT350R distinguishes itself with its aggressive aerodynamics, lightweight construction, and the high-revving 5.2-liter "Voodoo" flat-plane crank V8 engine, delivering 526 horsepower and 429 lb-ft of torque. This guide provides an in-depth look into what makes the GT350R a compelling choice for enthusiasts and collectors, covering model year changes, key features, common issues, and market insights.

## 2. Changes by Model Year

**2016:** Introduced as a new model alongside the GT350. Both featured the 5.2-liter Voodoo V8 engine, 6-speed Tremec manual transmission, and Magnetic Ride Control. The GT350R was specifically designed for the track, featuring carbon fiber wheels, a larger rear wing, and weight reduction through the deletion of rear seats, air conditioning, and stereo system. Base MSRP: ~$63,495.

**2017:** The Track Package became standard on all GT350 models, including an aluminum strut tower brace, transmission cooler, engine oil cooler, and rear differential cooler. Base MSRP: ~$63,495.

**2018:** Minor updates included a standard rearview camera and an updated Sync 3 infotainment system. Base MSRP: ~$64,645.

**2019:** Significant handling and performance improvements were implemented, including new Michelin Pilot Sport Cup 2 tires and a redesigned rear spoiler with an optional Gurney flap for improved aerodynamics. Interior upgrades featured standard dual-zone automatic climate control, a premium B&O Play audio system, and Recaro sport seats. The Heritage Edition package was introduced for the GT350 and GT350R. Base MSRP: ~$67,135.

**2020:** The final production year saw no major mechanical changes. The Heritage Edition package was reintroduced for both the GT350 and GT350R, offering retro styling. Base MSRP: ~$73,435.

## 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

*   **Shelby GT350R:** The primary trim, a track-focused variant that includes carbon fiber wheels, a larger rear wing, and weight reduction features such as the removal of rear seats, air conditioning, and stereo system.
*   **Heritage Edition Package:** Available on both GT350 and GT350R in 2019 and 2020, this special edition features Wimbledon White paint and Guardsman Blue racing stripes, paying homage to the original Shelby models.

## 4. Available Factory Options / Packages

*   **Track Package:** (Standard on later GT350 models) Includes a transmission cooler, engine oil cooler, and rear differential cooler for enhanced track performance.
*   **Handling Package:** Refines the driving experience with features like a Gurney flap and advanced suspension tuning.
*   **Technology Package:** Adds amenities such as a premium sound system and an upgraded infotainment system with navigation.
*   **Electronics Package:** Similar to the Technology Package, offering a premium sound system and upgraded infotainment.
*   **Heritage Edition Package:** (As described above) Retro-inspired styling.

## 5. Must-Have Factory Options

For a GT350R, many of its most desirable features are standard due to its track-focused nature. However, if considering a GT350 that might be optioned similarly, or for collectability:

*   **Track Package:** Essential for any serious track enthusiast, providing crucial cooling for demanding use.
*   **Heritage Edition Package:** For collectors, this package adds significant visual appeal and exclusivity, likely enhancing long-term value.
*   **Michelin Pilot Sport Cup 2 Tires:** (Standard on 2019+ models) These tires significantly improve grip and handling, making them a must-have for optimal performance.
*   **Gurney Flap:** (Optional on 2019+ models) Enhances aerodynamics and downforce, beneficial for track performance.

**Known Mechanical/Reliability Problems:**

*   **Excessive Oil Consumption:** A frequently reported issue, potentially caused by worn piston rings, a faulty PCV valve, or a clogged air filter.
*   **Engine Failures:** Some owners have experienced engine failures, particularly in early models, often attributed to defects in the Voodoo engine. Many required replacement under warranty.
*   **Clunky Shifts and Clutch Problems:** Difficulties during gear changes, especially when cold, linked to synchro problems or clutch noise.
*   **Suspension Issues:** Excessive bounce and poor handling, often due to worn shock absorbers, damaged bushings, or improper wheel alignment.
*   **Quality Control Concerns:** Noted body panel gaps and electrical problems, including faulty alternators, batteries, and occasional Sync 3 infotainment system issues.
*   **Class Action Lawsuit:** A settlement was reached for 2016 model year GT350 and GT350R models equipped with the Track Pack in specific states (Florida, Missouri, New York, and Washington) regarding overheating and limp mode issues due to alleged defective transmission and rear differential coolers.

## 8. Conclusion

The Ford Mustang Shelby GT350R (2016-2020) stands as a testament to American performance engineering, offering an exhilarating driving experience for those who crave track capability and raw power. The 2019 model year, with its significant performance and handling enhancements, represents an excellent balance of refinement and aggression. For the ultimate track weapon, the GT350R's dedicated features make it a standout. Collectors will find the Heritage Edition particularly appealing for its unique styling and homage to Shelby's legacy. Prospective buyers should prioritize well-maintained examples with documented service histories and be aware of common issues like oil consumption and potential engine concerns, especially in earlier models. Thorough pre-purchase inspections are highly recommended to ensure a sound investment in this iconic machine.

## 9. Car Hacking Shortcuts

*   **Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI):** Absolutely critical for any GT350R. Focus on engine health (compression/leak-down test), transmission and clutch condition, and suspension components. Look for signs of track use, such as excessive tire wear, brake wear, or undercarriage damage.
*   **Service Records:** Demand comprehensive service records. Regular oil changes with the correct oil type are paramount for the Voodoo engine. Pay attention to any warranty work, especially engine replacements.
*   **Oil Consumption Check:** Inquire about oil consumption habits from the previous owner. If possible, monitor oil levels during a test drive or after purchase.
*   **Recall Verification:** Ensure all applicable NHTSA recalls have been addressed. Check the VIN on the NHTSA website.
*   **Modifications:** Be wary of heavily modified examples unless the modifications are professionally installed and documented. Aftermarket tunes can void warranties and potentially stress components beyond their design limits.
*   **Underbody Inspection:** Look for scrapes, dents, or repairs that might indicate hard track use or accidents. Check for fluid leaks around the engine, transmission, and differential.
*   **Tire and Brake Condition:** These are consumables, but their condition can indicate how the car was driven. Fresh, high-performance tires and brakes are a plus.
*   **Fluid Analysis:** Consider sending samples of engine oil, transmission fluid, and differential fluid for analysis to detect potential internal wear issues.
*   **Negotiate Based on Known Issues:** Use knowledge of common problems (e.g., oil consumption, potential engine issues in early models) to negotiate a better price, especially if these issues haven't been proactively addressed.
