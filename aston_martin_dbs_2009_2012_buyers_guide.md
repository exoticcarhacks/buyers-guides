# Aston Martin DBS (2009–2012) Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Aston Martin DBS (2009–2012) is the quintessential modern classic V12 grand tourer, famous for its starring role in *Casino Royale* and *Quantum of Solace*. Built on the VH platform, it features a 6.0L naturally aspirated V12 producing 510 horsepower, draped in extensive carbon fiber bodywork. As an Exotic Car Hack, the DBS represents a fully depreciated, highly collectible piece of Aston Martin history. The main risk is deferred maintenance on aging components, particularly primary catalytic converter ingestion (which destroys the V12) and expensive carbon ceramic brake replacement.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

PPI reminder: Use an Aston Martin specialist. Focus on the V12 engine health, primary catalytic converters, and carbon ceramic brakes.

### Known Problems / Inspection Issues

| Issue | What To Know / Check |
|---|---|
| Primary Catalytic Converter Ingestion | The most catastrophic issue. The primary cats can break down and be sucked back into the engine, destroying the V12 (which lacks knock sensors). Many owners preemptively install secondary cat deletes or aftermarket headers. Check exhaust health during PPI. |
| Carbon Ceramic Brake Wear | The DBS features massive CCB rotors. They can be noisy, and replacing them costs $10,000+. Inspect rotors for chips, cracks, or minimum thickness. |
| Rear Subframe Corrosion | The VH platform is known for rear subframe rust on older models, especially those driven in wet/salty climates. Requires a lift inspection. |
| Headliner Sag | The Alcantara headliner adhesive fails over time, causing the ceiling to sag. Costs $1,000+ to have an upholstery shop repair. |
| Exhaust Valve Failure | The active exhaust valves can stick open or closed, affecting performance and sound. |
| Navigation / Infotainment Glitches | The pop-up screen mechanism can fail, and the system itself is prone to freezing or screen failure. |

### Known NHTSA Recalls To Verify By VIN

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 17V-796 | Transmission Park Pawl | 2009–2012 | A communication error between the ECM and TCM can cause the automatic transmission park pawl to not engage, leading to a roll-away risk. |
| 17V-795 | Battery Supply Cable | 2009-2016 DB9, 2009-2012 DBS, 2010-2015 Rapide, 2012 Virage, 2014 Vanquish | The battery supply cable may become damaged when the driver seat is moved fully rearward, increasing fire risk. |

## 8. Conclusion

The Aston Martin DBS is a stunning, analog V12 supercar that has bottomed out on its depreciation curve. Manual transmission coupes are the holy grail and will likely appreciate as collector items, commanding a significant premium due to their rarity and engaging driving experience. The Touchtronic automatics are cheaper to buy but harder to sell. The primary mechanical risk is V12 engine destruction from catalytic converter ingestion, making a meticulous PPI and exhaust inspection mandatory. Avoid rusty subframes and damaged carbon ceramic brakes.
