# Ferrari 812 Superfast and GTS 2018-2024 Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Ferrari 812 Superfast and GTS 2018-2024 represents the pinnacle of Ferrari's front-engine V12 grand tourers, offering a blend of blistering performance and exotic luxury. As the last naturally aspirated V12 production car from Ferrari, it stands as a significant hack candidate for those seeking a modern classic with immense power and a visceral driving experience. The main risk lies in the complexity of its advanced electronics and the potential for high maintenance costs if not properly vetted.

## 6. Known Problems and Recalls

A Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a Ferrari specialist is highly recommended.


### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |

|---|---|

| Cooling System Problems | Radiator hose detachment due to problematic hose clamp, leading to coolant spill and potential overheating. — Cost: Varies, potentially high if engine damage occurs |

| Electrical Faults | Issues with cameras, infotainment system, and other electronic components, sometimes requiring dealer intervention. — Cost: Varies, from simple resets to extensive dealer intervention |

| Battery and Charging Issues | Problems due to low battery voltage if not regularly connected to a trickle charger, leading to electronic faults and starting issues. — Cost: Battery replacement ~$500-$1000, diagnostics for electronic faults varies |

| Tire Wear and Vibration | Rapid tire wear and vibrations, especially with original Pirelli P Zero tires. — Cost: Set of high-performance tires ~$2,000 |

| Interior Rattles and Noises | Occasional rattles and squeaks from the interior, including hatch/rear luggage cover and seatbelt rattles. — Cost: Minor, typically covered under warranty or easily fixed |

| Sensor and Warning Light Glitches | Random warning lights and sensor errors, sometimes persistent and requiring dealer diagnostics. — Cost: Varies, from simple resets to dealer diagnostics and part replacement |

| Menettino failure | Leads to suspension failure, rear wheel failure, and other faults. |

| Oil pooling in the middle of the engine | Reported in pre-2020 models. |

| Faulty battery, loose connectors, or bad wiring | Reported in an 812 GTS, leading to error codes. |
