## Known Problems & Recalls

A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a qualified specialist is non-negotiable for any Escalade-V.

### Known Problems

Issue | What To Know/Check
----- | ------------------
Engine Failure | catastrophic engine failures reported at low mileage (e.g., 14k, 18k miles), often requiring engine replacement. This includes issues with the 6.2L V8 LT4 engine and potentially broader GM L87 engine family reliability concerns. Cost: Engine replacement is a significant cost, often leading to buybacks by GM.
Air Ride Suspension Failure | suspension system issues reported at low mileage (e.g., 18k miles). Cost: Not specified, but likely a high repair cost.
AC Leak | AC leak under the carpet requiring extensive diagnosis and repair. Cost: Not specified, but repair times can be lengthy.
Engine Defect (Lifter Failure) | Class action lawsuits are investigating lifter failure for 2014-present Cadillac Escalade and other GM vehicles. Issue: A component in the engine is not receiving adequate oil, causing friction and leading to engine failure.
Manufacturing Defects in 6.2L V8 L87 Engine | Recalls issued for problems where manufacturing defects can lead to loss of propulsion, engine damage, and engine failure. This is a platform-wide issue affecting Tahoe, Suburban, Yukon, and Escalade with the 6.2L V8 L87 engine.
