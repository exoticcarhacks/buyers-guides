## Inspection and Known Issues
### Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) Reminder
A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a BMW specialist is non-negotiable for any M5 F90 Competition.

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| **Electronics and Infotainment System Glitches** | Owners have reported intermittent issues with navigation freezing, Bluetooth connectivity dropping, and the digital instrument cluster displaying incorrect information. Check for software updates and test all infotainment functions thoroughly during a PPI. |
| **Oil Leakage** | Common areas for oil leaks include the oil cooler housing gasket and valve cover gaskets. Inspect these areas for any signs of weeping or active drips. Early detection can prevent more costly repairs. |
| **Transmission Issues** | Some F90 M5s may exhibit rough shifts or hesitancy, particularly in lower gears or during aggressive downshifts. These issues are often resolved with software updates. Ensure the vehicle's transmission software is up to date. |
| **Suspension Noises** | Creaks or clunks from the suspension system, especially in higher mileage vehicles, can indicate worn bushings, control arms, or shock absorbers. Pay close attention to these sounds during a test drive and inspect suspension components for wear. |
| **High Tire Wear** | The M5 Competition's high performance and aggressive alignment settings, combined with its weight, can lead to accelerated tire wear, particularly on the inner edges of the front tires. Inspect tire condition and tread depth, especially if the car has been driven aggressively. |
| **Exhaust Flap Malfunctions** | The exhaust flap mechanism can sometimes malfunction, leading to an inconsistent exhaust note or error messages. This is typically a dealer-serviceable item. |
| **Battery Drain Issues** | Some owners have reported parasitic battery drains, often linked to the electrical system's software not fully shutting down certain modules. This can lead to a dead battery if the car is left unused for extended periods. |
| **4-zone LCD panel lines** | A recurring issue with F90 and G30 models where lines appear on the 4-zone climate control LCD panel. Inspect the display for any pixelation or lines. |
| **Clunky sound from front axle** | A clunking sound from the front axle, particularly during turns or suspension travel, has been reported. This may be related to worn control arm bushings or steering components. |
| **Improperly torqued/loose bolts on stiffener plate** | Loose bolts on the front stiffener plate can cause clunking or clicking sounds. This is a known issue that can be rectified by proper torqueing. |
| **Low pressure fuel pump fault** | A fault with the low-pressure fuel pump can cause repetitive episodes of the engine dying on startup. This has often been repaired under warranty. |
| **Coolant Expansion Bottle** | The plastic coolant expansion bottle can develop leaks, potentially dripping coolant onto coilpacks and spark plugs, necessitating their replacement. Consider upgrading to a billet aluminum tank for durability. |
