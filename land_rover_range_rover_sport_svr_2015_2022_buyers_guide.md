# Land Rover Range Rover Sport SVR 2015-2022 Buyer Guide
## Introduction
The Land Rover Range Rover Sport SVR (2015-2022) is a high-performance luxury SUV that blends formidable off-road capability with supercar-rivalling acceleration, making it a compelling candidate for the discerning enthusiast. Its potent 5.0L supercharged V8 engine and sophisticated chassis dynamics offer an exhilarating driving experience rarely found in its class. As a hack candidate, its rapid depreciation from a high MSRP presents an opportunity for significant value, but potential buyers must be acutely aware of the substantial running costs and complex mechanical issues that can arise.

## Known Issues and Recalls
A Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a Land Rover specialist is non-negotiable for any SVR. This is a complex, high-performance vehicle where deferred maintenance can lead to catastrophic costs.

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Cooling System Failures | Premature failure of radiators or water pumps leading to overheating, warped cylinder heads, or cracked blocks. Repairs can cost $2,000 - $3,000+. |
| Timing Chain Wear and Tensioner Problems | Stretching or wear of metal timing chains, cracked plastic guides, and hydraulic tensioner failure, leading to rattling, reduced power, check-engine light, and increased oil consumption. Can lead to engine destruction if ignored. Preventive replacement is expensive, ranging from $3,000 - $7,000+. |
| Supercharger Belt and Pulley Issues | Replacement costs typically range from $100 - $350. |
| Oil Leaks and Lubrication Issues | Valve cover gasket replacement can cost $2,500 - $5,000+. |
| High Cost of Engine Replacement or Rebuild | Due to high-grade materials and advanced technology, replacement or rebuild costs can range from a few thousand to over ten thousand. |
| Front Air Springs | Failure around the 10-year mark. Replacement costs can be $2,000 - $5,000+ per axle. |
| Infotainment System Problems | Freezing or random restarts, issues with vehicle sensors and adaptive cruise control. Repair/replacement costs can range from $500 - $3,500+. |
| Transfer Case Judder | Repair costs can range from $350 to $9,000 for replacement, with shift motor replacement costing $800 - $1,350. |
| ARC lines rot out, ARC valve block failure | Replacement of ARC bushings can cost around $1,500. |
| Poor battery management system | Battery replacement can cost $45 - $650, but complex issues with the battery management system (BECM) can lead to costs in the thousands. |

### Platform Defects (General Range Rover Sport Issues)
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Brake Defect Class Action Lawsuit | A class action lawsuit (Zats et al. v. Jaguar Land Rover North America, LLC) alleges premature brake pad and rotor wear in Range Rover models, with owners reporting replacements needed before 15,000 miles and costs exceeding $2,000. |
| Suspension Investigation | NHTSA investigated a suspension problem in Range Rover and Range Rover Sport SUVs that could lead to steering loss. |
| Hybrid System Failure Recall | Jaguar Land Rover recalled 170,000 SUVs due to a fault within the hybrid system's DC-DC converter that could cause the battery to drain, resulting in a loss of drive power. |
| Turbocharger Defect Class Action Settlement | Jaguar Land Rover agreed to a class action settlement regarding defective turbochargers, extending the warranty to cover turbocharger and engine repairs for up to 10 years and 100,000 miles. |
| TSB LTB01076NAS3 | A 'clonk' noise from underneath the vehicle may be evident when driving at low vehicle speed, most prominent while accelerating/decelerating and when turning the steering wheel. |
| TSB SSM73943 | Customer may report the vehicle steering feels heavy with no warnings visible on the Instrument Cluster. |
| TSB LTB00401NAS6 | The steering wheel may exhibit a vibration / shimmy while driving. |
| TSB LTB00906NAS4 | On vehicles fitted with the twin speed transfer case, a judder may be felt from underneath the vehicle when steering at low vehicle speeds. |
| TSB SSM72030 | Customer may report a noise when turning the steering wheel, originating from the steering column. |
| TSB SSM49497 | An oil leak may be detected from the steering gear and incorrectly diagnosed as needing a replacement steering gear assembly. |

## Conclusion
The Land Rover Range Rover Sport SVR is a **medium hack candidate**. Its primary risk lies in the potential for high maintenance and repair costs, particularly with engine and suspension components. However, its strong performance, luxury appeal, and relatively steep depreciation make well-maintained examples an attractive proposition for those who understand and budget for its specific needs. The best ownership logic involves thorough pre-purchase inspection and setting aside a significant contingency fund for potential repairs.

## Car Hacking Shortcuts
* Years to buy: 2015-2017 for maximum depreciation value; 2018+ for updated tech and power if budget allows.
* Options to look for: Carbon Fiber Exterior Pack, Meridian Signature Audio, Head-Up Display, 22-inch wheels, Heated and Cooled Seats.
* Preferred colors: Generally, black, white, and metallic greys hold value best. Unique SVO paints can be desirable but narrow the buyer pool.
* PPI focus: Cooling system, timing chain, air suspension, transfer case, and infotainment system functionality.
* Known recalls: Verify all open recalls by VIN, especially those related to steering, latches, and camera systems.
* Market forecast: Expect continued gradual depreciation, but strong demand for well-optioned, clean examples should provide some stability.
