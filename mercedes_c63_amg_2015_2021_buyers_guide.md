## 6. Known Problems & NHTSA Recalls
### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Infotainment System Glitches | The COMAND infotainment system may experience occasional bugs, including unresponsive screens, freezing, or navigation errors. Common symptoms include a completely black screen or the system cycling on and off. Often, these issues can be resolved with a software update or a system reset (holding down the CD Eject button). In some cases, a faulty display connector or internal hardware malfunction may require professional diagnosis and repair. |
| Electronic Component Failures | Owners have reported issues with specific electronic components. **Camshaft Position Sensors** can fail, leading to engine warning lights, rough idling, and stalling. Inspection of the sensor connector for corrosion or damage is recommended. The **Electronic Steering Lock (ESL)** can also fail, immobilizing the vehicle. This is a known issue across various Mercedes models and often requires replacement or emulation of the ESL module. |
| Engine Mounts | The hydraulic engine mounts on the C63 (W205) are known to wear out prematurely, especially on vehicles driven hard. Symptoms include increased vibrations felt in the cabin, a 'thud' sound on takeoff or when shifting, a generally rougher driving experience, and noticeable noise or movement from the engine bay at idle. Inspect for fluid leaks from the mounts and excessive engine movement during acceleration and deceleration. Prompt replacement is recommended to prevent further stress on other components. |
| Transmission Issues | Both the AMG SPEEDSHIFT MCT 7-speed (2015-2018) and 9-speed (2019-2021) transmissions can exhibit rough shifting, hesitation, or clunkiness, particularly at low speeds or when cold. Owners have reported slipping at certain RPMs (e.g., around 2000 RPM) and jerky movements when shifting into drive or reverse from a standstill. These issues can sometimes be addressed with software updates, but may also indicate problems with the mechatronic unit, valve body, or solenoids. Low transmission fluid levels can also contribute to these symptoms. A thorough inspection of the transmission's health, including fluid levels and diagnostic codes, is crucial. |
| Exhaust Flap Rattle | The performance exhaust system, particularly in models with the AMG Performance Exhaust, can develop a rattle or chatter. This is often caused by worn or loose exhaust valve flaps, especially in the central H-pipe, or issues with the flap motors. Symptoms include a rattling noise during acceleration (around 2500-3000 RPM), particularly in Sport+ mode, or a squeaking noise when the car is started or throttle is blipped. This can be annoying and might require adjustment, lubrication, or replacement of the flaps or their actuators. |
| Suspension Components | The aggressive handling and performance of the C63 can lead to increased wear on suspension components. Common issues include prematurely worn **bushings** (e.g., control arm bushings), leading to clunking noises, vagueness in steering, and uneven tire wear. **Shocks and struts** can also fail, often manifesting as leaking fluid, a bouncy ride, or knocking sounds. Inspect for visible leaks on shocks/struts and check for excessive play in bushings. Vehicles driven hard or with higher mileage are more susceptible to these issues. |
| Differential Problems | The rear differential, particularly in C63 S models with the electronic limited-slip differential, can be a known weak point. Owners have reported whining noises from the rear, especially during acceleration or deceleration, and clunking sounds. In some cases, the differential can become inoperative. There have been reports of the rear axle differential mounting flange fracturing from aggressive launches in earlier models (2015-2017), which can lead to misalignment and costly repairs. Inspect for leaks, unusual noises, and ensure proper differential fluid maintenance. A thorough inspection of the differential and its mounting points is crucial. |
| Brake Wear | High-performance brakes, both standard and optional carbon-ceramic, can experience accelerated wear due to the vehicle's power and weight. Owners often report premature wear, particularly on rear brake pads, sometimes as early as 11,000 miles. Carbon-ceramic brakes are known to squeal, especially in cooler weather or before reaching optimal operating temperature; Mercedes-Benz even includes a disclaimer about this characteristic. The brake wear sensor typically monitors only one pad per axle, so regular visual inspection of all pads is crucial. Squealing can also be caused by light braking, lack of proper lubrication during installation, or uneven pad residue. Inspect for uneven pad wear, rotor scoring, and listen for persistent squealing or grinding noises. |
| Turbocharger Leaks | While the M177 twin-turbo setup is generally robust, isolated reports of oil leaks from turbocharger components exist. These leaks can originate from the turbocharger's breather ports, seals, or oil feed pipe lines. Symptoms may include visible oil around the turbochargers, a drenched oil pan, or smoke from the exhaust during hard acceleration. It's also important to note that failed oil separators in the M177 engine can cause excessive crankcase pressure, leading to valve-cover and front crank-seal leaks, which might be misdiagnosed as turbo leaks. Any oil leak in this area requires immediate attention to prevent further damage. |
| Radiator and Cooling Issues | Coolant leaks are a common concern in the W205 C63 AMG. These can stem from various points, including cracked or brittle coolant pipes and seals, a faulty expansion tank cap, the radiator itself, or the water pump. Some owners have also reported leaks from the timing case. Symptoms include visible coolant puddles, frequent low coolant warnings, or the engine overheating. Prompt diagnosis and repair are crucial to prevent more severe engine damage. Upgrading the intercooler is sometimes recommended for vehicles driven hard to help manage engine temperatures. |
| Air Conditioning Malfunctions | The A/C system in the W205 C63 AMG can experience various malfunctions. Common issues include a complete lack of cold air, weak airflow, or only one side of the cabin receiving cold air. Potential causes range from simple refrigerant leaks, clogged refrigerant lines, or a faulty pressure sensor, to more complex electrical climate control issues, a failing AC compressor, or problems with the blower motor. A blown fuse can also cause the AC button to not light up or function. Proper diagnosis is essential to identify the root cause, as repairs can vary from a simple recharge to component replacement. |
