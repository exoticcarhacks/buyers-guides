## 6. Known Problems & NHTSA Recalls
### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Infotainment System Glitches | Occasional bugs with the COMAND infotainment system, including unresponsive screens and navigation errors. Software updates typically resolve these issues. |
| Electronic Component Failures | Instances where electronic components such as sensors or the electronic steering lock may fail, leading to inconvenient repairs or replacements. |
| Engine Mounts | The engine mounts on the C63 can wear out prematurely, causing increased vibrations and a less refined driving experience. Requires prompt attention. |
| Transmission Issues | While the AMG SPEEDSHIFT MCT 7-speed transmission is generally reliable, there have been reports of rough shifting or hesitation, which might require software updates or mechanical adjustments. |
| Exhaust Flap Rattle | The performance exhaust system can develop a rattle due to issues with the exhaust flaps. This can be annoying and might need adjustment or replacement of the flaps. |
| Suspension Components | Aggressive handling comes at the cost of increased wear on suspension components. Bushings, struts, and mounts might need replacement sooner than expected, especially on vehicles driven hard or with high mileage. |
| Differential Problems | Some owners have experienced issues with the rear differential, including leaks or noises that suggest internal wear, which could be a costly repair if not covered under warranty. |
| Brake Wear | High-performance brakes, especially if equipped with the optional carbon-ceramic brakes, can exhibit accelerated wear and sometimes squeal, especially in cooler weather or before the brakes have warmed up. |
| Turbocharger Leaks | The twin-turbo setup is generally reliable, but there have been isolated reports of oil leaks from turbocharger components, which need immediate attention to prevent further issues. |
| Radiator and Cooling Issues | Occasional mentions of radiator leaks or issues with the cooling system that could lead to overheating if not diagnosed and fixed promptly. |
| Air Conditioning Malfunctions | The A/C system may sometimes fail to operate correctly, with some drivers reporting problems ranging from weak airflow to a complete lack of cold air. |
