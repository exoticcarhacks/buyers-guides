# Lamborghini Aventador SVJ 2019-2021 Buyer Guide

The Lamborghini Aventador SVJ, produced from 2019 to 2021, represents the pinnacle of Lamborghini's naturally aspirated V12 lineage, engineered for extreme track performance while retaining road legality. As a limited-production, high-performance variant, it presents a compelling hack candidate due to its exclusivity and potential for value appreciation, though the primary risk lies in the high cost of specialized maintenance and potential for significant depreciation if not carefully selected.

## Known Issues and Recalls

Always perform a comprehensive Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a qualified, independent specialist familiar with Lamborghini supercars.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Clutch failure | Premature clutch wear is a known issue, often exacerbated by aggressive driving or frequent launches. Replacement costs can be substantial, estimated between $13,000 - $20,000 including labor, and can potentially lead to associated gearbox damage. Consult with a specialist for specific wear tolerances and inspection points.

### NHTSA Recalls

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 19V-696 | N/A | 2017-2019 (Aventador S only) | Engine stall without warning due to engine management software. (Does NOT affect SVJ models) |
| 20V-618 | N/A | 2020 | Front frame suspension arm anchor bolt torque insufficient due to damaged aluminum frame threads, potentially leading to suspension arm joint collapse and loss of vehicle control. |
| 20V-151 | N/A | 2020 | Internal door handle mechanism may fail, leading to entrapment risk. |
| 21V-927 | N/A | 2021 | Heat exchanger oil line screws improperly torqued, leading to potential oil leak and fire risk. |

## Conclusion

The Lamborghini Aventador SVJ is a strong hack candidate for those seeking a visceral, naturally aspirated V12 experience with significant collectibility. Its limited production numbers and extreme performance credentials contribute to its appeal. The main risk remains the potential for high maintenance costs, particularly clutch wear, and the initial depreciation hit. Best ownership logic involves acquiring a well-optioned, low-mileage example, ideally a Roadster or SVJ 63, and focusing on meticulous preventative maintenance to preserve its long-term value and driving enjoyment.
