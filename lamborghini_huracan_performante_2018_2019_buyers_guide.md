# Lamborghini Huracan Performante 2018-2019 Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The Lamborghini Huracan Performante 2018-2019 is a track-focused variant of the already potent Huracan, offering an exhilarating driving experience through its advanced active aerodynamics (ALA) and increased power. It stands as a prime candidate for the 'Exotic Car Hack' due to its significant performance capabilities, with enthusiast consensus suggesting strong value retention in the used market. The main risk associated with this model, as with many high-performance exotics, lies in the potential for costly repairs related to its complex systems. The Performante Spyder, in particular, has been noted by Forbes as the "rarest and most desirable of all Huracáns" [8].

## Known Problems & Recalls

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---| 
| Front lift system malfunction | Inspect for slow operation, grinding noises, or complete failure to raise/lower. Often caused by hydraulic pump issues or sensor faults. Repair costs can range from $1,500 to $4,000+ depending on component failure. |
| Electrical system glitches | Owners have reported intermittent issues with infotainment systems, dashboard warnings, and sensor malfunctions. Often resolved with software updates or sensor replacements. Diagnosis typically $200-$500, repairs vary widely. |
| Brake fluid degradation | For vehicles driven hard or on track, brake fluid can degrade faster, leading to a spongy pedal feel and reduced braking performance. Regular fluid flushes (every 1-2 years) are crucial. Cost for flush: $300-$600. |
| Clutch wear (LDF transmission) | While the Performante uses a robust Lamborghini Doppia Frizione (LDF) dual-clutch transmission, aggressive driving can still lead to accelerated clutch wear. Symptoms include slipping, delayed engagement, or harsh shifts. A clutch replacement can be a significant expense, often $5,000-$10,000+. Inspect service records for transmission fluid changes and clutch health. |
| Hydraulic leaks (e.g., power steering, ALA system) | Inspect for fluid stains under the car or around hydraulic components. Leaks can occur in power steering lines, the active aerodynamic (ALA) system, or the front lift system. Repair costs vary widely depending on the source, from $500 for a hose replacement to several thousands for pump or actuator issues. |
| Cooling system issues | Overheating can occur due to radiator leaks, water pump failures, or clogged cooling lines, especially in hot climates or during track use. Check for coolant leaks, unusual temperature readings, or warning lights. Repairs can range from $800 for a hose replacement to $3,000+ for a new radiator or water pump. |
| Carbon build-up in engine | Direct-injection engines, like the Performante's V10, are susceptible to carbon build-up on intake valves, leading to reduced performance, misfires, and rough idling. While less common in newer models, it can occur with high mileage or specific driving conditions. Walnut blasting or chemical cleaning can resolve this. Cost: $1,000-$2,500. |
| 4WD system malfunction | Issues with the all-wheel-drive system can manifest as warning lights, unusual noises from the drivetrain, or a noticeable change in handling. Often related to differential issues, sensor failures, or control unit malfunctions. Diagnosis can be complex, with repairs ranging from $1,000 for sensor replacements to $5,000+ for differential work. |
| ALA (Aerodinamica Lamborghini Attiva) system malfunction | The active aerodynamic system is complex. Failures can involve actuators, sensors, or the control unit, leading to incorrect wing/flap deployment or warning lights. Symptoms include reduced aerodynamic efficiency or error messages. Diagnosis requires specialized equipment. Repairs can be costly, ranging from $2,000 to $10,000+ depending on the component. |
| Lamborghini Dynamic Steering (LDS) issues | Malfunctions in the variable-ratio steering system can lead to inconsistent steering feel, warning lights, or loss of power steering assist. This is a complex electronic and mechanical system. Diagnosis and repair often require specialized Lamborghini tools and expertise. Repair costs can be substantial, potentially $3,000-$8,000+. |
| Magneto-rheological suspension (MRS) shock failure | The adaptive MRS shocks can fail, leading to a harsh ride, poor handling, or error messages on the dashboard. Symptoms include fluid leaks from the shock body or noticeable changes in ride quality. Replacement of a single MRS shock can cost $1,500-$3,000+, plus labor. |
| Fuel system issues | Problems can include fuel pump failures, injector malfunctions, or sensor errors, leading to misfires, rough idling, or difficulty starting. Check for warning lights, fuel leaks, or unusual engine behavior. Diagnosis typically $200-$500, with repairs ranging from $800 for a fuel pump to $2,000+ for injector replacement. |
| Intermittent oil warning light | An intermittent oil warning light can be caused by faulty oil level sensors, pressure sensors, or wiring issues. While sometimes a minor electrical glitch, it can also indicate serious engine lubrication problems. Always investigate immediately. Diagnosis: $150-$400. Sensor replacement: $500-$1,000+. |

Its main risk lies in the potential for high maintenance and repair costs associated with its specialized components and active aerodynamic system. The best ownership logic involves meticulous pre-purchase inspections and a clear understanding of running costs, leveraging its performance pedigree for potential long-term value.
