# Audi RS7 Gen 1 2014-2018 Buyer Guide

The Audi RS7 Gen 1 2014-2018 is a high-performance sportback that combines aggressive styling with a potent 4.0L twin-turbo V8, making it a highly desirable candidate for exotic car hacking. Its steep depreciation curve from a six-figure MSRP creates a strong value proposition for buyers who understand the market dynamics. However, the main risk lies in potential catastrophic engine failures related to the turbochargers and PCV system, requiring diligent pre-purchase inspection and warranty consideration.

## Known Problems & Recalls

Always get a comprehensive Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) from a qualified independent specialist before buying.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| PCV Valve Failure | leads to oil consumption and engine damage, cost $1,000 - $2,000 |
| Carbon Buildup | affects engine performance and fuel economy, cost $500 - $1,000 |
| Turbocharger Failure | can lead to significant engine damage, cost $3,000 - $7,000 |
| Transmission Issues | rough shifting or complete failure, cost $5,000 - $10,000 |
| Air Suspension Leaks | causes uneven ride height, cost $1,000 - $2,500 per corner |
| Coolant Leaks | often from plastic components, cost $500 - $1,500 |

## Conclusion

The Audi RS7 Gen 1 is a strong hack candidate due to its massive initial depreciation and high desirability among enthusiasts. The main risk is the potential for expensive engine and turbocharger repairs, making a warranty or a thorough PPI absolutely critical. The best ownership logic is to target a well-optioned 2016+ Performance model that has already absorbed the steepest depreciation curve, drive it for 6-12 months, and exit before major maintenance milestones hit.

## Car Hacking Shortcuts

* Years to buy: 2016-2018 (Performance models preferred)
* Options to look for: B&O Sound, Dynamic Package, Carbon Optic Package
* Preferred colors: Nardo Gray, Daytona Gray, Sepang Blue
* PPI focus: Turbocharger health (oil screens), PCV valve, air suspension leaks
* Known recalls: Airbag control unit (16V-011, 17V-002, 18V-001), Fuel pump flange (14V-273)
* Market forecast: Stabilizing for early models, slight continued depreciation for late models.
