# Ferrari 360 Challenge Stradale Buyer Guide

The Ferrari 360 Challenge Stradale is a track-focused, limited-edition variant of the 360 Modena, designed for enthusiasts seeking a more visceral driving experience. It\"s a prime hack candidate due to its rarity and performance pedigree. The main risk lies in the specialized maintenance requirements and the potential for high repair costs associated with its bespoke components.

## Known Problems & Recalls

**Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) is crucial for this vehicle.**

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| F1 gearbox pump failure | $500-$2000 (Symptoms include slow or failed gear changes. Inspect for leaks around the F1 pump and actuator, and check F1 system pressure during PPI.) |
| Sticky interior buttons | Repair cost varies, generally inexpensive (Common Ferrari issue. Can be resolved with detailing services or specialized repair kits. Inspect all interior buttons for tackiness or residue.) |
| Suspension ball joints | Prone to wear (Specifically upper and lower front ball joints. Inspect for play, torn boots, and unusual noises during a PPI, especially on cars with track use. Replacement costs can be significant.) |
| Minor oil leaks | Cost not specified (Common areas include cam cover gaskets, oil sump gasket, and main seals. Inspect for oil residue, especially under the engine and around gasket lines. Severity varies, but can indicate deferred maintenance.) |
| Timing belt service | Upwards of $3,000 (part of full service) |
| Clutch wear (F1 transmission) | Cost not specified (Expected lifespan is typically 15,000-30,000 miles depending on driving style. Importance of clutch wear diagnostics during PPI, looking for remaining clutch life percentage. Symptoms include slipping or harsh engagement.) |
