# Ferrari GTC4Lusso (2017-2020) Buyer's Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Ferrari GTC4Lusso, a distinctive four-seater grand tourer, was available in the U.S. market from 2017 to 2020. Succeeding the Ferrari FF, this model offered a unique blend of performance, luxury, and practicality, making it a compelling choice for those seeking an exotic daily driver. It was offered in two primary configurations: the GTC4Lusso, featuring a naturally aspirated V12 engine and all-wheel drive, and the GTC4Lusso T, equipped with a twin-turbocharged V8 engine and rear-wheel drive. This guide provides an in-depth look at what to consider when acquiring this magnificent machine.

## 2. Changes by Model Year

**2017:** The GTC4Lusso debuted as an entirely new model, initially featuring a 6.3-liter V12 engine with all-wheel drive. Later in the same model year, Ferrari introduced the GTC4Lusso T, a rear-wheel-drive variant powered by a 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine, offering a more accessible entry point and a distinct driving dynamic.

**2018:** Both the GTC4Lusso and GTC4Lusso T continued without significant changes, with the V8-powered 'T' model becoming a more established offering.

**2019:** The GTC4Lusso lineup returned largely unchanged, with no major updates or revisions reported.

**2020:** This model year marked the final production year for the GTC4Lusso series, with no substantial changes implemented before its discontinuation. Models from this year benefit from all prior refinements.

## 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

Two primary trim levels were available in the U.S. market:

*   **GTC4Lusso:** This variant was powered by a 6.3-liter naturally aspirated V12 engine, delivering 680 hp and 514 lb-ft of torque. It featured Ferrari's advanced 4RM Evo all-wheel-drive system and four-wheel steering, providing exceptional grip and handling in various conditions. Estimated Base MSRPs ranged from $298,900 to $304,750.

*   **GTC4Lusso T:** This model was equipped with a 3.9-liter twin-turbocharged V8 engine, producing 602 hp and 561 lb-ft of torque. Unlike its V12 counterpart, the GTC4Lusso T was rear-wheel drive only, offering a lighter and more agile driving experience with its turbocharged power delivery. It was positioned to appeal to drivers seeking a sportier, more dynamic grand touring experience. Estimated Base MSRPs ranged from $257,000 to $263,750.

No specific special editions were widely marketed for the GTC4Lusso in the U.S. during its production run. However, individual cars could be highly customized through Ferrari's 'Tailor Made' program, allowing for unique specifications rather than distinct special editions.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

### NHTSA Recall Campaigns:

*   **NHTSA Campaign Number: 21V-833 (Expanded by 22V536)**: Addresses a potential issue with the brake fluid reservoir cap, which may not adequately vent, leading to a loss of brake fluid and potentially complete loss of braking capability. Affects a wide range of Ferrari models, including the GTC4Lusso. Remedy involves replacing the brake fluid reservoir cap and updating software, free of charge.

*   **NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V-090**: Concerns a defect where the fuel vapor separator may crack and leak fuel, increasing the risk of fire. Specifically impacts certain 2017-2019 Ferrari GTC4Lusso vehicles. Remedy is to replace the fuel vapor separator at no cost.

*   **NHTSA Campaign Number: 19V-089**: Addresses a problem with the door lock mechanism's Bowden cable. Excessive tension can make the door difficult to open/close or cause it to open unexpectedly. Affects certain 2017-2019 Ferrari GTC4Lusso and 2018-2019 GTC4Lusso T vehicles. Ferrari will inspect and replace the door lock mechanism as necessary, free of charge.

### Known Mechanical/Reliability Problems:

*   **Battery Drain and Electrical Issues:** Frequently reported, often leading to dead batteries after short periods of inactivity. Consistent battery tender use is crucial.
*   **Software Glitches:** Infotainment system screen freezes, persistent seat heater icons, and USB connectivity problems have been reported, often resolved with software updates.
*   **Oil Leaks:** At least one owner reported recurring oil leaks potentially linked to the gearbox. Drivetrain-related issues, though anecdotal, include reports of gearbox replacement due to leaks and harness disconnects affecting gear functionality.
*   **PTU (Power Transfer Unit):** A known potential concern in the all-wheel-drive system (GTC4Lusso V12) lineage, though specific widespread failures are not widely documented for the GTC4Lusso itself.
