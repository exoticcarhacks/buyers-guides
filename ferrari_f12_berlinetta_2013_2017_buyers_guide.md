## 5. Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) & Known Issues
A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a Ferrari specialist is highly recommended before any purchase.
 
### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Hot-start issues / Starter motor failure | The engine may struggle to start or fail to crank when hot (e.g., after a drive or short stop), often accompanied by electrical warning errors, lazy cranking, or radio/AC failure. This is commonly caused by a degraded starter motor cable (harness) that cannot supply sufficient current when hot, or a failing starter motor/relay. Fix involves replacing the starter motor, relay, and upgrading to a thicker starter cable harness. |
| Rough-shifting transmissions | 7DCL750 dual-clutch gearbox may leak fluid or experience speed sensor failure. Speed sensor failure fix: approximately $1,000. DCT leak rebuild: around $10,000. DCT replacement: $30,000-$40,000. |
| Peeling paint | occurs mostly on the rear mesh, possibly due to overheating from the exhaust. Simple fix, covered under factory warranty. |
| Dashboard leather shrinkage | The leather on the dashboard can shrink and pull away from the binnacle or defroster vents, primarily due to sun/heat exposure and adhesive failure. Repair requires removing the entire dashboard to stretch and recover or replace the leather. Cost: Typically ranges from $3,000 to over $11,000 depending on the shop and extent of damage. |
| Rear parcel shelf / sunscreen leather shrinkage | Similar to the dashboard, the leather on the rear parcel shelf and sunscreen area is prone to shrinking and pulling away due to UV and heat exposure. Repair involves removing the rear interior panels for reupholstery. Cost: A complete replacement rear parcel shelf can exceed $4,500, while custom reupholstery varies by specialist. |
| Creaking/clicking from rear driveshaft | A clicking or creaking noise from the rear when accelerating from a stop, shifting between reverse and drive, or at low speeds. This is often caused by loose driveshaft flange bolts or lack of lubrication at the splines where the axle meets the hub/differential. Fix involves cleaning the splines, applying Loctite, and re-torquing the bolts to spec. |
| Corrosion at rear mesh | covered under warranty or extended Ferrari warranty. |
| Racing seat lifter handle breaking | On models equipped with carbon fiber racing seats, the manual seat height adjustment lifter handle/bracket is prone to breaking under stress. Fix involves replacing the seat lifter mechanism or upgrading to stronger aftermarket aluminum/carbon fiber adjusters (e.g., Capristo). Cost: OEM replacement mechanisms are around $500+, while aftermarket billet adjusters run approximately $600-$800. |
 
### NHTSA Recalls
| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 22V-536 | 22V-536 | 2013-2017 | **Loss of Brake Function:** The brake fluid reservoir cap may not vent properly, creating a vacuum inside the reservoir. This can result in a brake fluid leak, potentially leading to a partial or total loss of braking capability, increasing the risk of a crash. **Remedy:** Dealers will replace the brake fluid reservoir cap with a modified version that vents properly and update the vehicle's software to provide a different warning message for low brake fluid, free of charge. |
| 19V-006 | 19V-006 | 2014-2017 | **Passenger Frontal Air Bag Inflator May Explode:** The passenger frontal air bag inflator (Takata non-desiccated ammonium nitrate) may degrade over time after prolonged exposure to high absolute humidity and temperature cycling. In the event of a crash, the degraded propellant can cause the inflator to rupture, sending metal fragments into the cabin, which may result in injury or death. **Remedy:** Dealers will replace the front passenger airbag assembly with a new non-ammonium nitrate inflator, free of charge. |
