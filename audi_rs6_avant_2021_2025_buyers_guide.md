# Audi RS6 Avant 2021-2025 Buyer Guide

The Audi RS6 Avant is not just a station wagon; it's a high-performance, twin-turbo V8 beast disguised as a family hauler, making it an intriguing hack candidate for those seeking supercar performance with daily usability. Its main risk lies in the complexity of its advanced systems, particularly the 48V mild-hybrid system, which can lead to costly repairs.

## Known Problems and Recalls

**Always get a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) from a reputable Audi specialist.**

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| 48V Mild-Hybrid System (BSG) Failures | sudden electrical shutdowns, replacement costs can exceed $3,000 |
| Alternator Failures | drain the battery and trigger warning lights, requires immediate repair |
| MMI Glitches & Connectivity Issues | system freezes, reboots, wireless CarPlay instability, OTA update limitations |
| Pre-Sense & Camera Reliability | weather sensitivity, camera failures, parking sensor glitches |
| Water Collection in the Hatch | water pools and pours onto bumper/legs when tailgate opens |
| Door Actuator Glitches | random "central locking malfunction" messages |
| Interior Rattles | faint rattles/vibrations from dash, door panels, speakers |
| Piano Black Trim | scratches easily, shows fingerprints and dust |
| Brake Dust and Squeal | standard steel brakes generate high dust, carbon ceramics may squeal at low speeds |
| Rev Limiter in Neutral | software limits revs in Neutral or Park |
| Air Suspension Issues | leaks or failing compressors, costly repairs if out of warranty |

## Conclusion

The Audi RS6 Avant is a **strong hack candidate** for those who appreciate understated performance and practicality. The main risk remains the potential for expensive repairs related to its complex electronic and mild-hybrid systems. Best ownership logic involves careful pre-purchase inspection and budgeting for potential maintenance, especially for early models.

## Car Hacking Shortcuts

* Years to buy: 2022-2023 for value, 2024+ for ultimate performance.
* Options to look for: Carbon Optic Package, Dynamic Ride Control (DRC) Suspension, Executive Package, Sport Exhaust.
* PPI focus: Thorough inspection of the 48V mild-hybrid system, MMI functionality, air suspension, and all electronic systems.
* Known recalls: 25V900000 (Rearview Camera), 25V201000 (Instrument Panel), 23V601000 (Brake Fluid Cap), 22V861000 (Control Module Shutdown).
* Market forecast: Expect stable depreciation for well-optioned examples, with Performance models holding value slightly better.
* Best ownership logic: Buy a well-maintained example with a comprehensive service history, ideally with an extended warranty covering complex electronics.
