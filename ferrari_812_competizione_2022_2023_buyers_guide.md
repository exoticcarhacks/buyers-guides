# 2022–2023 Ferrari 812 Competizione Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction
The Ferrari 812 Competizione (and its open-top sibling, the Competizione A) is the ultimate, track-focused evolution of the 812 Superfast. Unveiled in 2021 as the successor to the F12tdf, it represents the absolute pinnacle of Ferrari's naturally aspirated front-engine V12 lineage. With power bumped to 819 horsepower and the redline raised to an ear-piercing 9,500 rpm, it is a visceral, extreme machine. Production was strictly limited—999 coupes and 599 Aperta models—and every single allocation was sold out before the car was even revealed. This is not a car you casually cross-shop; it is a blue-chip collector asset, trading for massive premiums over its original MSRP.

## 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

*   **812 Competizione (Coupe):** Limited to 999 units globally. Features a patented rear aluminum panel with vortex generators instead of a traditional rear glass window, utilizing a digital rear-view mirror.
*   **812 Competizione A (Aperta):** Limited to 599 units globally. Features a removable carbon-fiber Targa top that stows in the trunk. The Aperta commands an even higher premium due to its lower production numbers and open-air V12 experience.

## 4. Available Factory Options / Packages

Given the exclusive nature of the Competizione, most were ordered through Ferrari's Tailor Made program. Key options that heavily influence value include:

*   **Carbon Fiber Exterior Upgrades:** Front air vents, under-door covers, rear diffuser, and engine bay carbon.
*   **Carbon Fiber Interior Upgrades:** Inner door handles, instrument cover, parcel shelf, and central bridge.
*   **Carbon Fiber Wheels:** 20-inch carbon-fiber wheels (often with colored detailing or yellow-line Pirelli P Zero tires).
*   **Suspension Lifter:** Essential for a car with such aggressive, low front aero.
*   **Advanced Front Driving Camera / Parking Cameras:** Crucial, especially since the Coupe has no physical rear window.
*   **Racing Seats:** Carbon fiber racing seats (Daytona style or standard).
*   **Telemetry / Track Cameras:** For those actually tracking the car.
*   **Livery / Stripes:** Factory painted stripes (e.g., Giallo Fly over Rosso Corsa) add significant premium over solid colors.

## 5. Must-Have Factory Options

1.  **Suspension Lifter:** Absolutely mandatory. The front splitter is massive, expensive, and sits very low.
2.  **Carbon Fiber Wheels:** Highly desirable and visually distinct; they reduce unsprung mass and elevate the car's aggressive stance.
3.  **Extensive Carbon Fiber (Interior/Exterior):** Buyers at this price point expect a fully loaded spec. Missing carbon fiber components will hurt resale value compared to a highly optioned car.
4.  **Factory Painted Stripe/Livery:** Solid color cars are less desirable than those with a factory racing stripe that highlights the aggressive aerodynamics.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

Given the limited production and the fact that these cars are rarely driven high miles, long-term reliability data is sparse. However, buyers must be aware of:

*   **Brake Fluid Reservoir Recall (NHTSA 25V716000 / 22V536000):** A critical recall affecting many modern Ferraris, including the 812 platform. The brake fluid reservoir cap may not vent properly, potentially leading to a loss of brake function. The fix involves replacing the cap and updating the software. **Ensure this has been performed.**
*   **Rear Window Recall (812 Superfast platform):** Early 812s had issues with incorrect bonding of the rear window. While the Competizione Coupe lacks a rear window, the Aperta and general platform bonding should be inspected.
*   **Extreme Aerodynamics Vulnerability:** The vortex generators, massive diffusers, and carbon splitters are incredibly expensive to replace. A thorough PPI must inspect for any underside damage or track abuse.

## 8. Conclusion

The Ferrari 812 Competizione is a guaranteed future classic. As one of the final, most extreme expressions of Ferrari's naturally aspirated V12 without hybrid assistance, its place in history is secure. It is not a car to buy for daily driving; it is an investment-grade asset and a sensory overload on the track. If you have the capital to play in the $1.5M+ arena, the Competizione offers exclusivity, staggering performance, and near-certain long-term value retention.

## 9. Car Hacking Shortcuts

*   **The "Million Dollar Premium":** Expect to pay roughly $1M over the original MSRP. Do not try to negotiate based on the sticker price; you are paying for the allocation and exclusivity.
*   **Aperta is the King:** If you have the choice and the budget, the Competizione A (Aperta) will always be the more valuable and sought-after variant due to the 599-unit production cap.
*   **Look for Tailor Made Specs:** Unique, highly optioned Tailor Made cars command the highest premiums. Avoid "boring" specs if you want maximum appreciation.
*   **Verify the Brake Recall:** The brake fluid reservoir recall is a serious safety issue. Do not drive the car hard until you confirm it has been addressed by a Ferrari dealer.
*   **Zero Miles vs. Driven:** Cars with delivery mileage command the highest prices. If you plan to actually drive the car, look for one with 1,000-2,000 miles where the initial "delivery mile premium" has slightly softened.
*   **Global Market Arbitrage:** Be aware that European and Middle Eastern market cars often trade for ~20% less than North American/US spec cars. Ensure you are comparing US cars when evaluating comps.
