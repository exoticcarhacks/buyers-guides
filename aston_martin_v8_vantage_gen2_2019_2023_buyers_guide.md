# Aston Martin V8 Vantage Gen 2 (2019–2023) Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The second-generation Aston Martin V8 Vantage (2019–2023) represents a massive leap forward in performance and technology, utilizing an AMG-sourced 4.0L twin-turbo V8 producing 503 horsepower, paired with a rear-mounted ZF 8-speed automatic transmission. As an Exotic Car Hack, the Gen 2 Vantage is an aggressive, modern sports car that has already taken its massive initial depreciation hit. The primary risks are irritating electrical and infotainment gremlins (inherited from older Mercedes architecture) and some early-build quality control issues like peeling leather and faulty headlight switches.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

PPI reminder: Use an Aston Martin specialist. Focus heavily on interior trim quality and electrical module scans.

### Known Problems / Inspection Issues

| Issue | What To Know / Check |
|---|---|
| Dashboard Leather Peeling | A notorious issue on 2019–2020 models. The leather around the windscreen vents shrinks and peels away. Repairing it requires replacing the entire dash section, which can cost tens of thousands out of warranty. Inspect the dash base carefully. |
| Infotainment / Electrical Gremlins | The older Mercedes COMAND system is prone to freezing, rebooting unexpectedly, and Bluetooth connectivity drops. 2019 models notably lack Apple CarPlay. |
| Headlight Switch Failure | On early models with a separate parking brake button, the headlight switch assembly is known to physically pop out of the dashboard. |
| Transmission Malfunctions | Some owners report rough shifting, delayed engagement, or sudden power loss due to slippage in the ZF 8-speed. Ensure the transmission software is fully updated. |
| Cooling System Leaks | Reports of coolant leaks and radiator issues leading to overheating warnings. |
| Noisy Rear Differential | A whining noise from the rear differential at highway cruising speeds is a common complaint. |

### Known NHTSA Recalls To Verify By VIN

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20V-604 / 19V-806 | Front Seat Airbag Fasteners | 2019–2020 | Loose fasteners can cause the airbag to deploy improperly, increasing injury risk. Dealers will tighten or replace the fasteners. |

## 8. Conclusion

The Gen 2 Aston Martin Vantage is a brutal, AMG-powered muscle car in a bespoke British suit. The 2019 models took the hardest depreciation hit but are missing crucial tech and exhaust upgrades, making the 2020+ models the smartest hacks. The AMG powertrain is generally bulletproof, but the car is let down by frustrating electrical gremlins and shrinking dashboard leather. A PPI must focus on the interior trim condition (specifically the dash) and ensure all software modules are updated. If you find a 7-speed manual, buy it immediately.
