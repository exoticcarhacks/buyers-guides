# Lamborghini Aventador (2012-2021) Buyer's Guide: U.S. Market

## 1. Introduction

This comprehensive guide provides detailed research on the Lamborghini Aventador for the U.S. market, covering model years 2012-2021, including S and roadster variants. The information is compiled from official press releases, NHTSA records, and automotive expert analyses.

## 2. Changes by Model Year

Throughout its production run, the Lamborghini Aventador received several updates and new variants. Key changes by model year are summarized below:

### 2012
*   **Key Changes:** First full year of production. Featured a 6.5L V12 engine (700hp) with a 7-speed ISR single-clutch gearbox. Carbon ceramic brakes were standard.

### 2013
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the LP 700-4 Roadster (manual removable roof panels). Mechanicals largely unchanged from 2012. New options included more color and trim selections, engine cylinder deactivation (V12 to V6 under low load), suspension refinements, and tweaks to the single-clutch transmission.

### 2014
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the LP 720-4 50th Anniversario (limited to 100 coupes and 100 roadsters globally). This special edition featured a unique aero package (larger air intakes, big flaps, huge splitter, massive rear diffuser), increased power (710hp), unique colors (Giallo Maggio for coupe, Grigio Titans for roadster), carbon fiber interior bits, and Q-Citura stitching. Other changes for the standard Aventador included new interior trim options, a sport exhaust software upgrade, and optional carbon fiber roof panels for the roadster.

### 2015
*   **Key Changes:** Regular LP 700-4 remained largely unchanged. Introduction of the LP 700-4 Pirelli Edition (limited to 100 units, 15 roadsters). This edition featured specific color options with a black roof, Pirelli branding, and red accents. A button to override the rear wing was added, and race exhaust hardware & software upgrades were introduced. More interior trim options and colors were available, and a mesh covering under the rear spoiler became an option.

### 2016
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the LP 750-4 SV (Superveloce) in both coupe (600 units) and roadster (500 units) formats. The SV featured increased power (740hp, 49hp up from base), significant weight reduction (217 lbs lighter), carbon fiber components (engine cover, wing, air intake, door panels, rear diffuser, seat shells), standard carbon ceramic brakes, a new instrument cluster, and optional racing shell seats. Optional Diantus wheels (20-inch front, 21-inch rear) and Magneto-rheological suspension were available. A lifting system was an add-on option. Centre-lock wheels from the SV could be ordered as an option for other models.

### 2017
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the Aventador S (facelifted version). Featured redesigned front and rear portions for aerodynamic improvements, four-wheel steering (LDS for front, LRS for rear), updated suspension, and new drive modes including a customizable EGO mode (Strada, Sport, Corsa, EGO). The V12 engine output increased to 740hp, and the maximum RPM was raised to 8,500. Apple CarPlay became standard, and the new exhaust system was 20% lighter. The single-clutch ISR transmission carried over. A Miura Homage trim (limited to 50 cars) was introduced, based on the base Aventador coupe with unique styling and colors.

### 2018
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the Aventador S Roadster. The base Aventador was dropped in favor of the Aventador S. The new flagship trim, SVJ (Superveloce Jota), was introduced, increasing horsepower to 770. Overall, roadster and coupe models received improved aerodynamic characteristics, exhaust tuning, and reconfigured driving modes.

### 2019
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the Aventador SVJ Roadster. The Aventador S remained largely unchanged.

### 2020
*   **Key Changes:** The convertible variant of the SVJ was introduced.

### 2021
*   **Key Changes:** The final iteration of the Aventador, the Ultimae, was introduced. This model combined elements of the S and SVJ, offering 770hp. It marked the end of the Aventador production.

## 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

Throughout its production, the Lamborghini Aventador offered various trim levels and special editions:

*   **LP 700-4 Coupe:** (2012-2017)
*   **LP 700-4 Roadster:** (2013-2017)
*   **LP 720-4 50th Anniversario Coupe & Roadster:** (2014) Limited to 100 coupes and 100 roadsters globally, with increased power (710hp) and unique aero.
*   **Jackie Chan Edition:** (2014) One-off special edition.
*   **Nazionale:** (2014) Limited one-off special edition.
*   **LP 700-4 Pirelli Edition Coupe & Roadster:** (2015) Limited to 100 units (15 roadsters), featuring specific color options and Pirelli branding.
*   **LP 750-4 SV (Superveloce) Coupe & Roadster:** (2016) Limited to 600 coupes and 500 roadsters, with increased power (740hp) and significant weight reduction.
*   **Aventador S Coupe:** (2017-2020) Facelifted version with aerodynamic improvements, four-wheel steering, and 740hp.
*   **Miura Homage:** (2017) Limited to 50 cars, based on the base Aventador coupe with unique styling.
*   **Aventador S Roadster:** (2018-2020)
*   **Aventador SVJ (Superveloce Jota) Coupe:** (2018-2021) Flagship trim with 770hp.
*   **Aventador SVJ Roadster:** (2019-2021)
*   **Aventador Ultimae Coupe & Roadster:** (2021) The final iteration, combining elements of S and SVJ, offering 770hp.
