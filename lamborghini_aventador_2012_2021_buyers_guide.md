# Lamborghini Aventador (2012-2022) Buyer's Guide: U.S. Market

## 1. Introduction

This comprehensive guide provides detailed research on the Lamborghini Aventador for the U.S. market, covering model years 2012-2022, including S, SV, SVJ, and Ultimae variants. The information is compiled from official press releases, NHTSA records, and automotive expert analyses.

## 2. Changes by Model Year

Throughout its production run, the Lamborghini Aventador received several updates and new variants. Key changes by model year are summarized below:

### 2012
*   **Key Changes:** First full year of production. Featured a 6.5L V12 engine (700hp) with a 7-speed ISR single-clutch gearbox. Carbon ceramic brakes were standard.

### 2013
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the LP 700-4 Roadster (manual removable roof panels). Mechanicals largely unchanged from 2012. New options included more color and trim selections, engine cylinder deactivation (which shuts down one cylinder bank, effectively making it a V6 under light loads), suspension refinements, and tweaks to the single-clutch transmission.

### 2014
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the LP 720-4 50th Anniversario (limited to 100 coupes and 100 roadsters globally). This special edition featured a unique aero package (larger air intakes, big flaps, huge splitter, massive rear diffuser), increased power (710hp), unique colors (Giallo Maggio for coupe, Grigio Titans for roadster), carbon fiber interior bits, and Q-Citura stitching. Other changes for the standard Aventador included new interior trim options, a sport exhaust software upgrade, and optional carbon fiber roof panels for the roadster.

### 2015
*   **Key Changes:** Regular LP 700-4 remained largely unchanged. Introduction of the LP 700-4 Pirelli Edition (limited to 100 units, 15 roadsters). This edition featured specific color options with a black roof, Pirelli branding, and red accents. Rear spoiler control was standard on all MY 2015 Aventadors, allowing manual override, and was available as a retrofit for MY 2013 and 2014 models. Race exhaust hardware & software upgrades were introduced. More interior trim options and colors were available, and a mesh covering under the rear spoiler became an option.

### 2016
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the LP 750-4 SV (Superveloce) in both coupe (600 units) and roadster (500 units) formats. The SV featured increased power (740hp, 49hp up from base), significant weight reduction (217 lbs lighter), carbon fiber components (engine cover, wing, air intake, door panels, rear diffuser, seat shells), standard carbon ceramic brakes, a new instrument cluster, and optional racing shell seats. Optional Diantus wheels (20-inch front, 21-inch rear) and Magneto-rheological suspension were available. A lifting system was an add-on option. Center-lock wheels were primarily associated with SV, SVJ, and Ultimae trims, and some Aventador S builds, and were not a general factory option for other models in 2016.

### 2017
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the Aventador S (facelifted version). Featured redesigned front and rear portions for aerodynamic improvements, four-wheel steering (LDS for front, LRS for rear), updated suspension, and new drive modes including a customizable EGO mode (Strada, Sport, Corsa, EGO). The V12 engine output increased to 740hp, and the maximum RPM was raised to 8,500. Apple CarPlay became standard, and the new exhaust system was 20% lighter. The single-clutch ISR transmission carried over. A Miura Homage trim (limited to 50 cars) was introduced, based on the base Aventador coupe with unique styling and colors.

### 2018
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the Aventador S Roadster. The base Aventador was dropped in favor of the Aventador S. The new flagship trim, SVJ (Superveloce Jota), was introduced, increasing horsepower to 770. The SVJ featured significant aerodynamic improvements with Aerodinamica Lamborghini Attiva (ALA) 2.0, enhanced exhaust tuning, and reconfigured driving modes for optimal performance. Overall, roadster and coupe models received improved aerodynamic characteristics, exhaust tuning, and reconfigured driving modes.

### 2019
*   **Key Changes:** Introduction of the Aventador SVJ Roadster. The Aventador S remained largely unchanged.

### 2020
*   **Key Changes:** The convertible variant of the SVJ was introduced.

### 2021
*   **Key Changes:** Production continued for existing models.

### 2022
*   **Key Changes:** The final iteration of the Aventador, the Ultimae, was introduced for the 2022 model year. This model combined elements of the S and SVJ, offering 770hp. It marked the end of the Aventador production.

## 5. Must-Have Factory Options

For prospective buyers, certain factory options significantly enhance the ownership experience and resale value of a Lamborghini Aventador:

*   **Transparent Engine Bonnet:** This is a highly desirable option as it proudly displays the iconic V12 engine, a centerpiece of the Aventador's appeal. It adds visual drama and is often sought after by collectors.
*   **Carbon Fiber Exterior and Interior Packages:** Carbon fiber accents not only reduce weight but also elevate the exotic aesthetic of the Aventador. These packages are visually striking and contribute positively to the car's perceived value.
*   **Ad Personam Customization:** While expensive, an Aventador with a unique Ad Personam paint or interior can command a premium, especially if the customization is tasteful and well-executed. It signifies exclusivity and personalization.
*   **Lifting System:** Given the Aventador's low ground clearance, a front-axle lifting system is almost essential for navigating speed bumps, driveways, and uneven terrain without scraping the undercarriage. This is a practical option that protects the vehicle and enhances daily usability.
*   **Parking Sensors with Rear View Camera:** Due to limited rear visibility, these features are highly recommended for easier and safer maneuvering in tight spaces, reducing the risk of costly damage.



## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

### NHTSA Recalls

|---|---|---|
| 18V-391 | 2012-2018 | Engine may stall unexpectedly due to fuel system issues. |
| 20V-151 | 2020 | Faulty internal door handles could trap occupants inside. |
| 21V-927 | 2021 | Detached oil lines could lead to oil leaks and fire risk. |

### Common Issues

*   **ISR Transmission Jerkiness:** The single-clutch ISR transmission, while fast, can be jerky at low speeds. Buyers should test drive extensively in various conditions to assess comfort. This is a characteristic of the transmission, not a defect.
*   **Carbon Ceramic Brake Squeal:** Carbon ceramic brakes are high-performance but can exhibit squealing noises, especially when cold or at low speeds. This is generally considered normal for this type of brake system.
*   **Electrical Gremlins:** Early models (2012-2014) may experience minor electrical issues, often related to infotainment or sensor malfunctions. A thorough pre-purchase inspection (PPI) should include checking all electronic systems.
*   **Tire Wear:** Due to the car's power and weight, tire wear can be significant, especially on the rear. Regular alignment checks and monitoring tire pressure are crucial.

## 7. Market & Depreciation Commentary

The Lamborghini Aventador, as a flagship V12 supercar, has historically demonstrated strong market retention, particularly for limited editions and later models. However, like all supercars, depreciation is a factor, albeit less severe than for some mass-produced luxury vehicles.

*   **Early Models (2012-2014):** These models have experienced the steepest initial depreciation. Prices have largely stabilized, making them potentially attractive for buyers seeking entry into Aventador ownership. However, potential maintenance costs should be factored in.
*   **Mid-Cycle Models (Aventador S, 2017-2020):** The Aventador S, with its significant updates and improved driving dynamics, holds its value better than the earlier LP 700-4. Demand remains relatively strong for well-maintained examples.
*   **Limited Editions (SV, SVJ, Ultimae):** These models, due to their rarity and enhanced performance, tend to depreciate less and can even appreciate in certain market conditions. The Ultimae, as the final naturally aspirated V12 Aventador, is expected to be a strong performer in terms of value retention.
*   **Factors Influencing Value:** Low mileage, desirable color combinations, extensive optional extras (e.g., carbon fiber packages, Ad Personam interiors), and a clean service history significantly impact resale value. U.S. market trends indicate a consistent demand for these vehicles, but economic shifts can influence pricing.

## 8. Conclusion

The Lamborghini Aventador remains a highly desirable supercar, offering a visceral driving experience and undeniable presence. Prospective buyers should prioritize thorough pre-purchase inspections, verify service history, and consider the specific model year and trim level to align with their ownership goals. The U.S. market offers a robust selection, but due diligence is paramount to a satisfying ownership experience.
