# Rolls-Royce Wraith 2014-2021 Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The Rolls-Royce Wraith, produced from 2014 to 2021, represents the pinnacle of ultra-luxury grand touring with a sporting edge. It is a compelling hack candidate due to its significant depreciation from new, offering a chance to experience Rolls-Royce opulence at a fraction of its original MSRP. The main risk lies in the potentially exorbitant maintenance and repair costs associated with such a bespoke and complex vehicle.

## Conclusion
The Rolls-Royce Wraith is a **strong hack candidate** for those seeking unparalleled luxury and presence at a depreciated value. The main risk is the potential for very high repair costs, particularly for engine-related issues. Best ownership logic involves meticulous pre-purchase inspection and setting aside a significant fund for potential maintenance.

## Car Hacking Shortcuts

* Years to buy: 2014-2018 (earlier models offer maximum depreciation, pre-Black Badge premium)
* Options to look for: Starlight Headliner, Bespoke Audio, Driver Assistance Systems, Black Badge Edition (if performance is a priority)
* Preferred colors: Classic Rolls-Royce colors (e.g., Black, Midnight Sapphire, Silver) tend to hold value better. Avoid overly flamboyant bespoke colors unless specifically desired for personal use.
* PPI focus: Engine oil leaks, coolant leaks, turbocharger health, carbon buildup, and functionality of all electronic systems (especially infotainment and cameras).
* Known recalls: No model-specific NHTSA recall found in this pass. Verify by VIN before purchase.
* Market forecast: Expect continued gradual depreciation, with limited editions and highly optioned examples holding value better.
