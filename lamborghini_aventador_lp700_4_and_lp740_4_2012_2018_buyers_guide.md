# Lamborghini Aventador LP700-4 and LP740-4 2012-2018 Buyer Guide

## Introduction
The Lamborghini Aventador, spanning the LP700-4 and LP740-4 generations from 2012 to 2018, represents the pinnacle of naturally aspirated V12 supercars, embodying extreme performance and dramatic styling. It stands as a prime candidate for the Exotic Car Hacks philosophy due to its significant depreciation from new, offering an entry point into a hyper-exclusive segment at a fraction of its original cost. The main risk associated with this vehicle is the potential for high maintenance and repair costs, particularly concerning its complex powertrain and specialized components.

## Known Problems and Recalls

Always perform a Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a qualified Lamborghini specialist before any purchase.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Transmission issue (2012 models) | Check for leaks and proper shifting. Lamborghini addressed this with design changes and improved seals in later models. |
| Bouncy suspension (2012 models) | Test drive on varied surfaces; later models had improved suspension. |
| Electrical issues | Verify proper function of infotainment system, USB/SD card recognition. |
| Headlight issue | Inspect for condensation build-up inside housing and flickering/failing LEDs. |
| Clutch failure | Listen for slippage, check for warning messages, and ensure smooth power delivery. Clutch replacement is costly. |
| Fuel problem | Check for starting issues, sputtering, stalling, or power loss. Inspect for fuel tank leaks. |

### NHTSA Recalls

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 18V-391 | Engine stall, servo steering/brake booster compromised | 2012-2018 | Engine may stall unexpectedly during certain driving conditions (accelerator pedal released and below 2000 rpm in conjunction with automatic shifts from high to low gears, in the idle approach) compromising servo steering and brake booster functionalities. |
| 12V561000 | Headlights non-compliant with FMVSS 108 | 2011-2012 | Headlights fail to conform to FMVSS 108, specifically vertical aiming, which can reduce visibility for the driver and blind oncoming traffic. |
| 17V073000 | Fuel may contact exhaust system | 2012-2017 | In certain driving conditions, while the vehicle has a full gas tank, gasoline may contact the exhaust system. |
| 18V461000 | Brake disc bolts may loosen | 2015-2016 | Bolts centering and supporting the brake discs to the wheel's hub may loosen over time, potentially leading to wheel detachment. |
| 19V696000 | Engine stall when coasting | 2017-2019 | Engine management software may allow the engine to stall when coasting to a stop. |
