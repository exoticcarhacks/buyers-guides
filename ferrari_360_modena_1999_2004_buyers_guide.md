## Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) & Known Issues

A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection by a Ferrari specialist is non-negotiable for any 360 Modena.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Variator and Left Bank Tensioner | If the variator fails, it can lead to severe engine damage. Repair can cost upwards of $4,000. |
| Motor Mount and Frame Brackets | The motor mount frame bracket is prone to cracking on 1999 models. Repair costs average around $1,000. New motor mounts cost about $300 each, with installation adding another $300. |
| F1 Hydraulic Pump Relay | If the relay fails, the pump can overwork itself and burn out. Upgrading to a 50-amp relay costs around $200. |
| Transmission Mounts | All Ferrari 360 Modenas are prone to transmission mount wear over time. Upgraded parts cost around $200, but installation can add up to approximately $700. |
| Pre-Catalytic Converters | Can occasionally fail by breaking apart and potentially being sucked into the engine. Catastrophic consequences if it occurs. |
| Interior Wear | Leather surfaces are prone to drying out and splitting at the seams. Plastic buttons are infamous for becoming sticky or showing signs of wear over time. |
| F1 Transmission Software | Problematic version of the TCU software that led to premature clutch wear. |
| Electrical Issues | Occasional electrical problems, often due to poor grounding associated with its aluminum chassis. Common issues include repeated battery failures and instrument panel illumination faults. |
| Shifter Bushings | Worn shifter bushings can make gear changes feel stiff or imprecise. |
| Ball Joints | Early 360 models experienced ball joint failures. |
