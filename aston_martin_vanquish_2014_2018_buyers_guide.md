# Aston Martin Vanquish (2014–2018) Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Aston Martin Vanquish (2014–2018) replaced the DBS as the brand's flagship V12 grand tourer. It features a full carbon-fiber body and an upgraded 5.9L V12 (AM11) engine. As an Exotic Car Hack, the Vanquish is highly desirable because it combines stunning aesthetics with a significantly improved interior over the older VH platform cars. The critical dividing line for buyers is the transmission: 2014 models use the sluggish 6-speed Touchtronic II, while 2015+ models feature the vastly superior 8-speed Touchtronic III. The biggest risks are expensive electronic faults, adaptive damper leaks, and carbon ceramic brake replacement.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

PPI reminder: Use an Aston Martin specialist. The Vanquish is heavily reliant on complex electronics and expensive carbon fiber body panels.

### Known Problems / Inspection Issues

| Issue | What To Know / Check |
|---|---|
| Adaptive Chassis System (ADS) Faults | The adaptive dampers are prone to leaking fluid, and the sensors can fail, throwing the car into emergency mode. Replacement is very expensive. Check for seeping shocks during PPI. |
| Transmission Issues (Touchtronic) | While the 8-speed is better, both transmissions can suffer from electro-hydraulic control module failures, delayed shifts, or overheating if driven aggressively. |
| Electronic and Wiring Faults | The Vanquish suffers from widespread electrical gremlins: failing window regulators, starter buttons, infotainment screens, and tire pressure sensors. Wiring can degrade due to moisture. |
| V12 Engine Leaks | The AM11 V12 is generally robust but prone to water pump seal failures, intake vacuum leaks, and blocked oil passages if maintenance is deferred. |
| Carbon Ceramic Brake Wear | Standard CCBs can develop microcracks from improper handling (e.g., using acidic wheel cleaners) or track use. Replacement is $10,000+. |
| Paint and PPF Degradation | The carbon fiber body panels can suffer from paint flaking at the joints. Factory Paint Protection Film (PPF) often yellows or peels and needs replacement. |

### Known NHTSA Recalls To Verify By VIN

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 17V-796 | Transmission Park Pawl | 2014 | A communication error between the ECM and TCM can cause the park pawl to not engage, leading to a roll-away risk. |
| 15V-846 | Door Locks | 2014–2016 | Interior door unlocking mechanisms may not function when the vehicle has been locked from the outside. |

## 8. Conclusion

The Aston Martin Vanquish is one of the most beautiful cars of the modern era, but it must be bought correctly. A 2014 model with the 6-speed transmission is a poor hack and difficult to sell. The 2015+ models with the 8-speed Touchtronic III are the sweet spot, while the 2017+ Vanquish S is the ultimate collector spec. Buyers must budget for potential electrical gremlins and leaking adaptive dampers. A clean PPI from an Aston Martin dealer is mandatory to avoid a car with $20,000 in deferred brake and suspension work.
