# Porsche 911 Turbo S 997.2 2011-2012 Buyer Guide

The Porsche 911 Turbo S 997.2, produced from 2011 to 2012, represents the pinnacle of the 997 generation, offering a potent blend of performance and daily usability. This model is a prime candidate for car hacking due to its strong resale value, limited production numbers, and the enduring appeal of the 911 Turbo S lineage. The main risk associated with this vehicle is the potential for expensive repairs, particularly related to its complex engine and advanced braking system.

## Known Issues & Recalls

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Rear lights | Condensation and eventual failure of LED rear lamps due to plastic housing separation. This is a known issue across various Porsche models, including the 997.2. Cost: Expensive replacement |
| Coolant cross-over pipes | Leaks at connections near the front radiator due to perishing rubber sections or corroded metal connectors. Can lead to engine overheating and failure. Cost: New pipes essential |
| DFI / High-pressure fuel pumps | Rare but can fail, causing limp mode or total shutdown. Cost: Expensive due to pump cost |
| Failed cylinder bore walls | Rare |
| Oil pump failure | Rare |
| Camshaft chain issues | Rare |
| Rod through engine case | Rare |
| Bore scoring | Rare, one instance reported due to specific driving conditions (Canadian winters, cold starts) |
| Oil pump solenoid failure | One instance reported, no codes thrown |
| Carbon ceramic brakes | Can be a liability due to extremely high replacement costs for rotors and pads, especially if tracking the car. Inspect for cracks or excessive wear. |

### NHTSA Recalls
| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20E091000 | 20E091000 | 2005-2012 (997 Carrera 4/4S), 2006-2013 (997 Turbo/Turbo S) | Aftermarket Ohlins Front Struts (part number POS 5N20) may experience excessive stress during maximum steering, possibly resulting in breakage of the pin. If the pin fails, it will cause separation of the strut from the vehicle, affecting vehicle control and increasing the risk of a crash. |
| 11V285000 | 11V285000 | 2011 | REPLACING CENTRAL WHEEL BOLTS. AS THE HUBS WEAR, THE WHEELS COULD LOOSEN, INCREASING THE RISK OF A CRASH |
| 11E021000 | 11E021000 | 2011 | AFTERMARKET CENTER WHEEL MOUNTING KITS. IF THE WHEEL RETENTION FAILS, A VEHICLE CRASH COULD OCCUR |
| 11V409000 | 11V409000 | 2012 | SEAT BELT END FITTINGS. SHOULD THIS OCCUR, THE SEAT BELT MAY NOT BE ROUTED OPTIMALLY AROUND THE OCCUPANT, OR MAY POTENTIALLY LOOSEN AT SOME POINT IN THE FUTURE INCREASING THE RISK OF INJURY DURING A CRASH |
