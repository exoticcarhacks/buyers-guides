# McLaren 720S 2018-2021 Buyer Guide

The McLaren 720S, a formidable supercar from Woking, represents a significant leap in performance and design within the Super Series. It stands as an excellent hack candidate due to its blistering performance and relatively accessible price point compared to its hypercar rivals, though its complex electronics and bespoke components pose the primary ownership risk.

## Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) and Known Issues


### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Infotainment System Malfunctions | Owners have reported issues such as screen freezing on startup, reverse camera display failures, Bluetooth connectivity drops, navigation system inaccuracies, unexpected dashboard warnings, and system reboots. These issues are often software-related; ensure the system software is updated to the latest version and check for any persistent fault codes. |
| Hydraulic Suspension Accumulators | Accumulators can fail, leading to suspension fault warnings and a harsh ride. Inspect for leaks and check service history for accumulator replacement. |
| Battery Drainage | The 720S utilizes a lithium-ion battery which is susceptible to rapid discharge if the vehicle is not driven frequently or maintained on a trickle charger. This can lead to starting issues and electronic malfunctions. Always verify the battery's health during a PPI and ensure the factory-supplied trickle charger is used when the car is stored. |
| Door Latch Issues | The soft-close door mechanism, which assists in fully closing the dihedral doors, can fail or require adjustment. This may result in the doors not latching securely, triggering dashboard warnings, or requiring manual force to close. Inspect for proper operation and listen for unusual noises during the closing cycle. |
| Cracking Windshields | Early 720S models (primarily 2018-2019) were susceptible to windshield stress cracks, often originating from the base near the A-pillars. While McLaren implemented revised installation procedures and potentially stronger glass in later production, inspect the windshield thoroughly for any signs of cracking, especially on earlier models. |
| Coolant Hoses and Crossover Pipes | The M840T 4.0L twin-turbo V8 in the 720S runs hot, and over time the coolant hoses and crossover pipes can crack, weep, or develop pinhole leaks. |
| Active Rear Spoiler (Air Brake) Malfunctions | The active rear spoiler, which also functions as an air brake, can experience malfunctions. These often manifest as fault codes on the dashboard, inconsistent deployment, or complete failure to operate. Issues are typically related to the hydraulic actuators, wiring, or position sensors. During a PPI, verify smooth and consistent operation through its full range of motion, and check for any stored fault codes. |

### NHTSA Recalls

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20V-231 | Verify by VIN | 2018-2020 | The noise vibration and harshness foam pad (NVH foam pad) situated underneath the fuel tank can potentially retain corrosive moisture, leading to corrosion and micro-porations in the fuel tank, possibly causing fuel vapor/liquid release and increasing fire risk. |
| 20V-216 | Verify by VIN | 2020 | Incorrectly routed brake pipes may cause the ABS/ESP system to malfunction, leading to rear wheel lock-up and increased risk of a crash. Applies to 2020 models only. |

## Conclusion

The McLaren 720S remains a compelling choice for enthusiasts seeking extreme performance. While its advanced electronic and hydraulic systems are integral to its capabilities, they also represent the primary areas of potential concern for long-term ownership. A thorough pre-purchase inspection by a McLaren specialist is crucial to identify any existing or nascent issues, particularly with the infotainment, suspension, and active aero systems. Prospective buyers should also factor in the potential for high maintenance and repair costs associated with a vehicle of this caliber.
