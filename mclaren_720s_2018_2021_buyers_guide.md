# McLaren 720S 2018-2021 Buyer Guide

The McLaren 720S, a formidable supercar from Woking, represents a significant leap in performance and design within the Super Series. It stands as an excellent hack candidate due to its blistering performance and relatively accessible price point compared to its hypercar rivals, though its complex electronics and bespoke components pose the primary ownership risk.

### NHTSA Recalls

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 20V-231 | Verify by VIN | 2018-2020 | The noise vibration and harshness foam pad (NVH foam pad) situated underneath the fuel tank can potentially retain corrosive moisture, leading to corrosion and micro-porations in the fuel tank, possibly causing fuel vapor/liquid release and increasing fire risk. |
| 25V-216 | Verify by VIN | 2020-2021 | Incorrectly routed brake pipes may cause the ABS/ESP system to malfunction, leading to rear wheel lock-up and increased risk of a crash. |

## Conclusion

The McLaren 720S is a **strong hack candidate** for those seeking hypercar-level performance without the hypercar price tag. The best ownership logic involves meticulous pre-purchase inspection and budgeting for potential maintenance, leveraging its depreciation curve for maximum value.
