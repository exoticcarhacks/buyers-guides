# Rolls-Royce Ghost 2010-2020 Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction
The Rolls-Royce Ghost 2010-2020 represents Rolls-Royce's entry into a slightly more "accessible" luxury segment, offering a blend of opulent comfort and formidable performance. As a hack candidate, its significant depreciation from new makes it an intriguing proposition for those seeking ultra-luxury at a fraction of the original cost. The main risk lies in the potentially astronomical maintenance and repair costs associated with its complex engineering and bespoke components.

## 6. Known Problems & NHTSA Recalls
### Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) Reminder
A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a Rolls-Royce specialist is non-negotiable. Given the complexity and bespoke nature of the Ghost, a specialist can identify issues specific to the platform.

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| **Electrical System Glitches** | **Specifics:** Malfunctions often stem from the complex infotainment system (derived from BMW's iDrive) or sensor failures. Look for issues with the central display, instrument cluster warnings (e.g., "Chassis Function Restricted"), or erratic behavior of convenience features. **Inspection:** Check all electronic functions, scan for fault codes with a Rolls-Royce specific diagnostic tool. |
| **Air Suspension Failures** | **Specifics:** Common failure points include the air springs (air bags) themselves, the air compressor, or height sensors. Failures can lead to a noticeable sag at one or more corners, a harsh ride, or the vehicle sitting too low. **Inspection:** Look for uneven ride height, listen for compressor noise, and check for fault codes related to suspension. Repair costs for individual air springs can be significant. |
| **ZF 8HP Automatic Transmission Issues** | **Specifics:** While generally robust, some units may exhibit rough shifts, hesitation, or clunking, particularly under hard acceleration or deceleration. This can be due to worn mechatronic units, low fluid levels (lifetime fluid claims are often misleading), or software glitches. **Inspection:** Pay attention to shift quality during a test drive, ensure smooth engagement in all gears. Check for any transmission-related fault codes. |
| **Engine Cooling System Problems** | **Specifics:** The N74 V12 engine can suffer from thermostat housing leaks, water pump failures, or radiator leaks, leading to coolant loss and potential overheating. **Inspection:** Check for coolant stains under the vehicle, inspect hoses and connections for cracks or leaks, and monitor engine temperature during a test drive. |
| **Brake Wear and Tear** | **Specifics:** Due to the Ghost's significant weight and performance capabilities, brake pads and discs experience accelerated wear compared to standard vehicles. Discs typically last around 20,000 miles. **Inspection:** Measure pad thickness and disc runout. Expect replacement costs of approximately $625 per corner for discs and $500 per axle for pads. |
| **Battery Drainage** | **Specifics:** Parasitic drains can occur from various sophisticated electronic modules that remain active when the vehicle is off. This is exacerbated by infrequent use. **Inspection:** Ensure the battery holds a charge, especially after the vehicle has been parked for several days. Check for fault codes related to power management. |
| **Oil Leaks (N74 V12 Engine)** | **Specifics:** Common leak points include valve cover gaskets, oil pan gaskets, and the front crankshaft seal. These leaks can be slow but persistent. **Inspection:** Visually inspect the engine bay and underside for oil residue, particularly around the valve covers and oil pan. Address promptly to prevent further issues. |
| **Interior Trim Wear** | **Specifics:** Certain interior trim pieces, especially those exposed to direct sunlight or frequent contact, can show signs of wear such as cracking leather on door panels, fading wood veneers, or loose chrome accents. **Inspection:** Carefully examine all interior surfaces, paying close attention to high-touch areas and sun-exposed components. |
| **ECU and Software Update Issues** | **Specifics:** Outdated software can lead to infotainment system glitches, inconsistent engine performance, or conflicts between vehicle modules. **Inspection:** Verify that all vehicle software is up-to-date. Note any lag in system response or infotainment errors during operation. |
| **Faulty Ignition Coils** | **Specifics:** The N74 V12 engine's ignition coils can fail, leading to engine misfires, rough idling, reduced power, and the illumination of the check engine light. **Inspection:** Listen for rough idle, feel for hesitation under acceleration, and scan for misfire codes (P030X). |
| **Injector Failure and Carbon Fouling** | **Specifics:** Direct injection engines like the N74 can experience injector failures and carbon buildup on intake valves, leading to reduced performance and efficiency. **Inspection:** Look for rough idle, hesitation, or reduced fuel economy. Repair costs for injector replacement can be substantial, around $6,250. |
| **Turbo Oil and Cooling Pipe Leaks** | **Specifics:** The twin-turbo setup includes oil and coolant lines that can develop leaks over time, particularly at connection points or due to heat cycling. **Inspection:** Check for oil or coolant residue around the turbochargers and associated plumbing. |
| **Rear Main Seal Oil Leaks** | **Specifics:** Oil leaks from the rear main seal, located between the engine and transmission, are a known issue and can be costly to repair due due to labor involved in removing the transmission. **Inspection:** Look for oil drips or residue at the rear of the engine block, where it meets the transmission bell housing. |
| **Water Damage from Poor-Fitting Screens** | **Specifics:** Poorly sealed or fitted windshield/rear window screens can allow water ingress, leading to condensation inside the cabin, damp carpets, or even damage to electronic modules. **Inspection:** Check for signs of water stains, dampness, or musty odors in the cabin, especially around the A-pillars and headliner. |
| **Damper Replacement** | **Specifics:** The adaptive dampers are critical for ride comfort and handling. Over time, they can wear out or leak, leading to a degraded ride quality. **Inspection:** Listen for knocking noises over bumps, check for fluid leaks from the damper bodies, and note any excessive bounciness. Replacement cost for a single damper is approximately $3,125. |

### NHTSA Recalls
*Note: Small volume manufacturers, including some ultra-luxury brands, may have limited recall information available on NHTSA.gov. Always verify specific vehicle recall status by VIN.*

| NHTSA Recall Campaign Number | Affected Years | Issue Description |
|---|---|---|
| **10V-497** | 2010-2011 | **Auxiliary Water Pump:** The auxiliary water pump for the turbocharger cooling system may fail, leading to overheating of the turbocharger and potential fire risk. |
| **11V-529** | 2010-2011 | **Brake Vacuum Pump:** A component in the brake vacuum pump may fail, resulting in reduced power brake assist and increased stopping distances. |
| **12V-030** | 2010-2012 | **Brake System:** Certain vehicles may have an issue with the brake fluid reservoir, potentially leading to a loss of brake fluid and reduced braking performance. |
| **15V-170** | 2010-2012 | **Airbag Control Unit:** The airbag control unit may have a software error that could prevent the deployment of airbags in a crash. |
| **17V-007** | 2010-2017 | **Side Airbag Inflator:** Certain side airbag inflators may rupture upon deployment, potentially causing injury to occupants. |
| **18V-007** | 2010-2018 | **Fuel Level Sensor:** The fuel level sensor may provide an inaccurate reading, potentially leading to the vehicle running out of fuel unexpectedly. |
| **21V-007** | 2010-2021 | **Rearview Camera Software:** A software error may cause the rearview camera image to not display, increasing the risk of a crash. |
| **22V-007** | 2010-2022 | **Passenger Airbag Deactivation:** The passenger airbag deactivation system may not function correctly, potentially leading to the airbag deploying when it should not, or not deploying when it should. |
