
# Rolls-Royce Ghost 2010-2020 Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction
The Rolls-Royce Ghost 2010-2020 represents Rolls-Royce's entry into a slightly more "accessible" luxury segment, offering a blend of opulent comfort and formidable performance. As a hack candidate, its significant depreciation from new makes it an intriguing proposition for those seeking ultra-luxury at a fraction of the original cost. The main risk lies in the potentially astronomical maintenance and repair costs associated with its complex engineering and bespoke components.

## 6. Known Problems & NHTSA Recalls
### Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) Reminder
A thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a Rolls-Royce specialist is non-negotiable.

### Known Problems
| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| Electrical System Glitches | malfunctioning instrumentation screens, faulty dashboard lights, inconsistent sensor readings, software bugs, sophisticated wiring systems |
| Air Suspension Failures | component failure (air compressor or struts), uneven ride |
| Transmission Troubles | jerks, shift delay, gear hesitation due to insufficient/aged transmission fluid, worn clutch components, software errors |
| Engine Cooling System Problems | thermostat issues, leaking radiator, overheating |
| Brake Wear and Tear | rapid wear of brake pads and discs |
| Battery Drainage | phantom drains from inactive systems, battery aging, long idle durations |
| Oil Leaks | worn valve cover gaskets or oil seals, old/improperly sealed oil filters |
| Interior Trim Wear | cracking, fading, loosening of trim components due to sun exposure, harsh cleaning agents, lack of conditioning |
| ECU and Software Update Issues | outdated ECU software, conflicts between system modules, lag in system response, infotainment errors |
| Faulty Ignition Coil | engine misfires, rough idling, poor performance, damage to engine components |
| Injector failure | engine injector failure and carbon fouling of valve seats — cost ~$6,250 |
| Turbo oil and cooling pipe leaks | leaks in turbo oil pipes and cooling pipes |
| Rear main seal oil leaks | oil leaks from the rear main seal between the engine and transmission |
| Water damage from poor-fitting screens | condensation and baggy seat leather due to water ingress |
| Brake discs and pads wear | brake discs last ~20,000 miles — discs cost ~$625/corner, pads cost ~$500/each |
| Damper replacement | replacement damper — cost ~$3,125 |

## 8. Conclusion
The Rolls-Royce Ghost 2010-2020 is a **strong hack candidate** for those seeking unparalleled luxury at a significant discount. The main risk is the high cost of potential repairs, particularly for complex electrical and air suspension systems. Best ownership logic involves thorough pre-purchase inspections and budgeting for premium maintenance to ensure a sublime ownership experience.

## 9. Car Hacking Shortcuts
* Years to buy: 2010-2013 for maximum depreciation benefit, 2014+ for Series II refinements.
* Options to look for: Extended Wheelbase (EWB), Starlight Headliner, Massaging Seats.
* Preferred colors: Classic Rolls-Royce colors like Diamond Black, Jubilee Silver, or Midnight Sapphire maintain value.
* PPI focus: Thorough inspection of air suspension, electrical systems, and engine for oil leaks and cooling system integrity.
* Known recalls: Verify all NHTSA recalls by VIN, especially for auxiliary water pump and airbag issues.
* Market forecast: Expect continued gradual depreciation, but strong demand for well-maintained examples with desirable options.
