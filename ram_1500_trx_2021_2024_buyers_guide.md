## Problems & Recalls

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|:------|:-------------------|
| Front CV Axle issues | **Premature wear/failure of front CV axles, often exacerbated by aftermarket lift kits or suspension modifications.** Symptoms include clunking/knocking noises, vibration during acceleration, or reduced power transmission. Inspect boots for tears, check for excessive play in the joints, and ensure proper CV angles if modified. |
| Rear Driveshaft Clunk | **Clunking noise from the rear driveshaft, typically during acceleration or deceleration.** This can be caused by worn U-joints, excessive backlash in the differential, or loose hardware. Inspect U-joints for play and ensure driveshaft bolts are torqued correctly. |
| Uconnect Operation issues | **Specific Uconnect 5 bugs reported include intermittent freezing, slow response times, or connectivity issues with wireless Apple CarPlay/Android Auto.** Check for the latest software updates and test all infotainment functions thoroughly during a pre-purchase inspection. |
| Warped brake rotors and vibration under braking | **Premature warping of front brake rotors, leading to steering wheel vibration and pulsing during braking, especially after heavy use (e.g., towing, aggressive driving).** Inspect rotors for runout and check pad wear. Consider upgrading to performance rotors and pads if consistent heavy use is anticipated. |
| Leak into passenger compartment | **Water leaks into the passenger footwell, often manifesting as dampness under the rubber floor mats.** Common sources include clogged sunroof drains, improperly sealed windshields, or issues with the HVAC condensate drain. Inspect carpets and under-mat areas for moisture and check drain lines. |
| HVAC system issues | **Common HVAC problems include weak airflow, inconsistent temperature control, or unusual noises from the blower motor.** This can be due to a failing blower motor, clogged cabin air filter, or refrigerant leaks. Test all fan speeds and temperature settings. |
| Failed bearing attached to the rear axle shaft | **Premature failure of the bearing supporting the rear axle shaft, potentially leading to grinding noises from the rear, wheel wobble, or fluid leaks.** This can sometimes be related to excessive stress from off-road use or heavy loads. Inspect for play in the rear wheels and listen for abnormal noises during a test drive. |
