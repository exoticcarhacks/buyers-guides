
# Lamborghini Aventador SV (2016-2017) Buyer's Guide

## 1. Introduction

The Lamborghini Aventador LP 750-4 Superveloce (SV) represents the most extreme and track-focused iteration of the original Aventador platform, offering enhanced performance and reduced weight. Available in the U.S. market for the 2016 and 2017 model years, the SV Coupe was joined by the Roadster variant in 2016, providing an open-top driving experience without compromising the SV's raw power and agility. These models were designed for enthusiasts seeking the pinnacle of Lamborghini's V12 performance.

## 2. Changes by Model Year

Given its status as a special edition model, the Lamborghini Aventador SV experienced no significant year-over-year changes between the 2016 and 2017 model years for the SV variant itself. The SV Coupe was initially unveiled in 2015, with the Roadster following in 2016. Both models carried over largely unchanged for their limited production run. The primary distinction was the introduction of the Roadster variant, offering an open-top driving experience while retaining the performance enhancements of the SV Coupe. It is important to note that the Aventador S, a different model with various updates, was introduced in 2017, succeeding the standard Aventador LP 700-4, but it is distinct from the SV models covered in this guide.

## 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

For the 2016-2017 model years, the Lamborghini Aventador SV was itself a special, limited-production variant of the standard Aventador. The primary trim levels/special editions available in the U.S. were:

*   **Lamborghini Aventador LP 750-4 Superveloce (SV) Coupe:** This is the hardtop version, distinguished by its increased power output of 750 PS (740 hp), significant weight reduction, and enhanced aerodynamic features compared to the standard Aventador. Production of the SV Coupe was limited to 600 units globally.
*   **Lamborghini Aventador LP 750-4 Superveloce (SV) Roadster:** This open-top variant offered the same performance characteristics as the Coupe but featured a removable hardtop for an exhilarating open-air driving experience. Production of the SV Roadster was even more exclusive, limited to just 500 units worldwide.

These models represented the pinnacle of Aventador performance before the subsequent introduction of the Aventador S and later the SVJ.

## 5. Must-Have Factory Options

From an enthusiast and value perspective, several factory options stand out for the Lamborghini Aventador SV, enhancing both the driving experience and potential resale value:

*   **Visible Carbon Fiber Exterior Packages:** Given the SV's focus on lightweight performance, opting for visible carbon fiber elements (front, rear, engine bay, engine bonnet) significantly enhances its aggressive aesthetic and reinforces its track-bred pedigree. These options, while costly, are highly sought after by collectors and contribute to the car's exotic appeal.
*   **Special Metallic or Pearl Effect Paint Colors:** While standard colors are available, Lamborghini's vibrant special metallic or pearl effect paints (e.g., Rosso Bia, Arancio Atlas) are iconic and can significantly boost the car's presence and desirability. These unique hues often command a premium in the used market.
*   **Carbon Fiber Hard Top (for Roadster):** For the SV Roadster, the removable hard top in visible carbon fiber (especially the carbon forged composite option) is a must-have. It not only reduces weight compared to the standard black top but also visually integrates with the car's carbon fiber theme, making it a more cohesive and desirable package.

These options not only elevate the car's visual appeal and performance aesthetic but also tend to be favored by discerning buyers, potentially aiding in stronger value retention.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls


**NHTSA Recall Campaigns:**

*   **17V-073 (February 2017):** This recall addressed a potential fuel system issue where certain Aventador vehicles could experience fuel tank overfilling, leading to fuel vapor leakage and, in extreme cases, a risk of fire.
*   **18V-461 (July 2018):** This recall pertained to an engine control unit (ECU) software issue that could cause the engine to stall or enter a limp mode, affecting vehicle performance and safety.


**Known Mechanical/Reliability Problems:**

*   **Battery Issues:** A recurring problem, even in low-mileage vehicles, is related to the battery. The standard VW-sourced Varta battery, if aged, can lead to low voltage alerts, various error codes, and even cause the car to stall. Battery replacement is labor-intensive, requiring the removal of the entire front trunk.
*   **Transmission/Clutch Issues:** The Aventador's Independent Shifting Rod (ISR) single-clutch transmission, while fast, can be jerky at low speeds. More critical issues include:
    *   **Transmission Leaks and Failures:** More prevalent in earlier 2012 Aventador models, but the platform is known for these potential issues, despite Lamborghini implementing design changes and improved seals in later models.
    *   **Clutch Wear and Failure:** Due to the single-clutch system and immense power, clutch wear is a significant concern. Premature clutch failure can lead to slippage, loss of power, or stalling, with very costly replacement.
    *   **Clutch Wear Sensor Malfunction:** Malfunctions can trigger warning messages and force the car into a 'limp mode.'
    *   **Gearbox Malfunction:** Reports exist of Aventador SVs experiencing gearbox malfunctions, leading to the car being stuck in gear (e.g., 1st gear) and unable to start, a complex and expensive issue.
*   **Suspension Problems:** Some owners have reported issues with leaky shocks, which can be an expensive repair, especially when serviced at official Lamborghini dealerships.

## 8. Conclusion

The Lamborghini Aventador SV (2016-2017) stands as a monumental achievement in supercar engineering, representing the most aggressive and track-focused iteration of the original Aventador platform. Its limited production numbers, combined with a ferocious V12 engine, advanced aerodynamics, and significant weight reduction, make it a highly desirable machine for collectors and driving purists alike. While the SV Coupe offers the purest driving experience, the Roadster provides the added thrill of open-air motoring without sacrificing performance.. Despite these considerations, the Aventador SV remains a compelling choice for those seeking an uncompromised, visceral supercar experience with strong potential for long-term value retention, especially for well-optioned and meticulously maintained examples.

## 9. Car Hacking Shortcuts

1.  **Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a Specialist:** Never skip a thorough PPI from an independent Lamborghini specialist. Focus on the condition of the ISR transmission and clutch, suspension components (for leaky shocks), and a full diagnostic scan for any electrical or battery-related fault codes. This is your best defense against costly surprises.
2.  **Battery Management:** Invest in a high-quality trickle charger and use it consistently, especially if the car is not driven daily. Aventadors are notorious for battery drain, and a healthy battery can prevent a cascade of electrical issues and error codes. Consider upgrading to a more robust aftermarket battery if the original shows signs of weakness.
3.  **Clutch Life Awareness:** Understand that the ISR single-clutch transmission's life is heavily dependent on driving style. Avoid excessive low-speed crawling and aggressive launches to prolong clutch wear. Factor in potential clutch replacement costs into your ownership budget.
4.  **Recall Verification:** Before purchase, ensure all NHTSA recalls (specifically 17V-073 and 18V-461) have been addressed by a Lamborghini dealership. Obtain documentation of these services.
5.  **Service History Review:** Demand a complete and verifiable service history. Consistent maintenance by authorized Lamborghini service centers or reputable specialists is crucial for the longevity and value of an SV.
6.  **Aftermarket Warranty Consideration:** Given the high cost of repairs, explore reputable aftermarket warranty options that specifically cover exotic vehicles. Carefully review the terms and exclusions related to the powertrain and electrical systems.
7.  **Forum and Owner Group Engagement:** Join online forums and owner groups dedicated to the Aventador SV. These communities are invaluable resources for real-world experiences, troubleshooting tips, and recommendations for specialists.
