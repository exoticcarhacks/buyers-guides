# Acura NSX Type S (2022) Buyer Guide

## 1. Introduction

The 2022 Acura NSX Type S serves as the swan song for the NC1 generation, limited to just 300 units in the United States. It offers a significant performance upgrade over the standard NSX, pushing output to 600 horsepower via GT3 Evo turbochargers, enhanced cooling, and aggressive aerodynamic revisions. As an Exotic Car Hack, the Type S trades the massive depreciation curve of the standard NSX for extreme exclusivity and collector appeal. While it holds its value far better than the standard car, buyers must still be prepared for the immense complexity of the hybrid powertrain, including the notorious 75,000-mile engine-out service and potential hybrid battery degradation.

## 6. Common Problems / Recalls

PPI reminder: Use an Acura NSX certified technician. The hybrid system requires specialized diagnostic tools.

### Known Problems / Inspection Issues

| Issue | What To Know / Check |
|---|---|
| 75,000-Mile Valve Adjustment Service | Like the standard NSX, the Type S requires an engine-out valve adjustment at 75,000 miles. Quotes for this service range from $15,000 to $20,000+. While most Type S models are currently low mileage, this must be factored into long-term ownership. |
| Hybrid Battery Degradation | The complex hybrid system relies on a high-voltage battery pack. Out-of-warranty replacement is extremely expensive (estimated $5,000+). Verify the hybrid system warranty status (typically 8 years/100k miles, or 10 years/150k miles in CARB states). |
| Carbon-Ceramic Brake Rotor Damage | Inspect CCB rotors carefully during PPI (standard if equipped with the Lightweight Package). Cracks or chips in the carbon-ceramic rotors require full replacement, which costs thousands of dollars per corner. |
| Matte Paint Maintenance (Gotham Gray) | If purchasing a Gotham Gray Matte model, inspect the paint meticulously. Matte paint cannot be polished or buffed to remove scratches; any damage requires repainting the entire panel. |

### Known NHTSA Recalls To Verify By VIN

*At this time, there are no major NHTSA recalls specific to the 2022 NSX Type S, as the fuel pump and crossover pipe issues from earlier years were resolved prior to production. However, always run the specific VIN through the NHTSA database during a PPI.*

## 8. Conclusion

The 2022 Acura NSX Type S is a spectacular, limited-production send-off for the hybrid supercar era. With only 300 units in the U.S., it operates in a completely different market bracket than the standard 2017–2021 NSX, commanding collector premiums rather than suffering from massive depreciation. The ideal hack is a Lightweight Package-equipped model, preferably in a rare color like Gotham Gray Matte. While reliability is generally strong, the looming threat of a $20,000 engine-out service at 75,000 miles and the high cost of hybrid battery replacement mean a meticulous PPI by an NSX specialist is absolutely mandatory.
