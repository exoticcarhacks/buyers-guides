# Ferrari LaFerrari 2014-2015 Buyer Guide

The Ferrari LaFerrari, produced from 2014 to 2015, stands as a pinnacle of hybrid hypercar engineering and a prime candidate for the discerning Exotic Car Hacks enthusiast. This limited-production marvel combines a naturally aspirated V12 engine with an electric motor, delivering unparalleled performance and exclusivity. As a hack candidate, its rarity and technological prowess offer unique opportunities, but the primary risk lies in the complexity and potential cost of maintaining its advanced HY-KERS system.

## Common Problems & Recalls

Always conduct a thorough Pre-Purchase Inspection (PPI) by a qualified Ferrari specialist.

### Known Problems

| Issue | What To Know/Check |
|---|---|
| HY-KERS high-voltage battery pack failure | Battery cells degrade or develop manufacturing defects, especially on low-mileage stored cars and cost 5,000 - 10,000 |
| HY-KERS electric motor sensor failure | Internal stator sensor fails, requiring either specialist repair or complete motor replacement and cost ,800 - 6,000 |
| DCT gearbox sensor and clutch wear | Internal wiring insulation degrades from heat, causing sensor errors; clutch packs wear with aggressive use and cost ,100 - 0,700 |
| Magnetorheological damper oil leak | SCM dampers leak magnetorheological fluid as seals age, typically after 40,000-60,000 km or 8+ years and cost ,600 - 4,000 |
| Carbon ceramic brake disc replacement | Full carbon ceramic disc and pad set replacement is extremely costly but rarely needed on low-mileage cars and cost 9,000 - 8,000 |
| Sticky interior coating degradation | Rubberized soft-touch coating on buttons and trim degrades into a sticky, tacky mess over time and cost ,500 - 4,000 |
| 12V battery drain | Conventional battery drains within 7-14 days if the car is not driven or kept on a trickle charger and cost 30 - 80 |

### NHTSA Recalls

| NHTSA Recall | Campaign | Affected Years | Issue |
|---|---|---|---|
| 15V-306 | N/A | 2014-2015 | Due to an error in the vehicle configuration settings, in the event of a punctured tire the Tire Pressure Monitoring System’s additional information screen displays an incorrect warning message. The message displayed states “Low tyre pressure – Max speed 50 mph” incorrectly. Instead, the display should state “Low tyre pressure – Do not proceed”. The incorrect message was intended for use with runflat tires. |
| 15V-433 | N/A | 2015 | The Driver’s Side Air Bag could possibly deploy in a rotated orientation due to an improperly assembled Driver’s Side Airbag Module. |

### Platform Defects

Brake fluid leak: Defective master cylinder/brake booster assembly leading to partial or total loss of braking ability. Ferrari has known about this since at least June 2015. Two recalls (brake fluid cap replacement and software update) are considered inadequate. Affects nearly every Ferrari sold in the U.S. since 2005, including LaFerrari 2014-2015. Class action lawsuit filed against Ferrari and Bosch GmbH.

## Conclusion

The Ferrari LaFerrari is a **strong hack candidate** for those seeking ultimate exclusivity and hypercar performance. The main risk remains the potential for costly repairs to its complex hybrid system, particularly the HY-KERS battery. Best ownership logic dictates meticulous maintenance, careful storage, and leveraging its rarity for long-term value retention.
