## Audi R8 V8 (2008-2012) Buyer's Guide

### 1. Introduction

The Audi R8 V8, specifically the first generation produced from 2008 to 2012, marked Audi's bold entry into the supercar segment. Combining everyday usability with exotic looks and thrilling performance, the R8 quickly became a favorite among enthusiasts. Powered by a 4.2-liter V8 engine, it offered a compelling package of power, handling, and distinctive design, including its iconic sideblades. Available with both a 6-speed manual and an R-tronic sequential manual transmission, the V8 model laid the groundwork for the R8's legacy as a formidable contender in the high-performance sports car market.

### 2. Changes by Model Year

The Audi R8 V8 Gen 1 saw subtle but important changes throughout its production run in the U.S. market:

*   **2008:** The inaugural year for the Audi R8 in the U.S., featuring the 4.2-liter V8 engine producing 420 hp. It was available with a 6-speed manual or R-tronic sequential manual transmission. Key features included Audi magnetic ride suspension, quattro all-wheel drive, an aluminum ASF chassis, and LED lighting.
*   **2009:** No significant changes to the V8 Coupe. The base MSRP increased slightly from the initial 2008 launch prices.
*   **2010:** No significant changes to the V8 Coupe. The base MSRP remained consistent with 2009.
*   **2011:** Introduction of the R8 Spyder (convertible) for the V8 model, offering a soft-top roof while retaining the V8 engine and transmission options. MSRPs for both Coupe and Spyder models saw adjustments.
*   **2012:** No major changes for the V8 model before the facelift in 2013. MSRPs remained relatively stable.

### 3. Trim Levels / Special Editions

For the Audi R8 V8 Gen 1 (2008-2012) in the U.S. market, the primary trim levels were:

*   **R8 4.2 Quattro Coupe:** The standard coupe model with the 4.2-liter V8 engine.
*   **R8 4.2 Quattro Spyder:** Introduced in 2011, this was the convertible version of the V8 model.

There were no widely recognized special editions for the V8 Gen 1 R8 in the U.S. market during this period. Special editions like the GT were typically V10 models.
